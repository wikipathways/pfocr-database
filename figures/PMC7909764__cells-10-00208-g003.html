Senolytic therapies

Senolytic therapies. Senolytics mainly target the Senescent Cell Anti-apoptotic Pathways (SCAPs). The SCAPs are activated to protect senescent cells from apoptosis triggered by any form of pro-apoptotic insults, including the SASP. The most common SCAPs are Hsp90, Bcl-2 family proteins, p53, and the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway. Several senolytic agents identified to date are depicted. Directed targeting of senescent cells is achieved by the nanoparticle-based delivery of senolytics.
