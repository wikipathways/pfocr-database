Proposed pathway of cAMP signaling after FID addition during in vitro maturation

Proposed pathway of cAMP signaling after FID addition during in vitro maturation. Primarily, FID incited PKA, which collaborated closely with calcium and ERK1/2 pathways. PKA upregulated cholecystokinin (CCK) and putatively increased the intracellular calcium pool. Calcium was involved in the regulation of genes implicated in glucose metabolism and steroidogenesis. In parallel, PKA inhibited cumulus expansion genes through the ERK1/2 pathway with concomitant stimulation of their expression via MAPK14 (p38). (?) PKA regulated DUSP1 transcripts, suggesting its possible implication in ERK1/2 phosphorylation.
