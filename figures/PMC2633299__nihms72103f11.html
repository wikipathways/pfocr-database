UVB light upregulates prostaglandin synthases and prostaglandin receptors in mouse keratinocytes

UVB light stimulates the release of arachidonic acid from keratinocyte cell membranes and increases COX-2 and various prostaglandin synthases and prostaglandin receptors (indicated by the arrows pointing upward) that contribute to its biological effects in the skin. Regulation of UVB-induced expression of the prostaglandin synthases and prostaglandin receptors by the MAP kinases (p38 and JNK) and/or PI3K are shown in parenthesis.
