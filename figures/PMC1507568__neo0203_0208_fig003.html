The p53 response is triggered by many different stresses involving both DNA-damaging and non-DNA-damaging agents

The p53 response is triggered by many different stresses involving both DNA-damaging and non-DNA-damaging agents. These include ribonucleotide synthesis inhibitors resulting in perturbations of nucleotide pools [], thymidine dinucleotides [], media depletion [], hypoxia [], antioxidants [], inhibition of ubiquitylation or the proteasome proteolysis pathway [], DNA strand breaks [], bulky DNA lesions [], DNA topoisomerase inhibitors [], blockage of RNA polymerase II [], heat shock [], cold shock [], viral infection [], pRb deregulation [] and oncogene expression [].
