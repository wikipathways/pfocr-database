Synopsis of FGFR2b-mediated dermal-epithelial signaling in keratinocytes and sebocytes of sebaceous glands

Synopsis of FGFR2b-mediated dermal-epithelial signaling in keratinocytes and sebocytes of sebaceous glands. DHT-stimulated fibroblasts increase the expression of FGF7 and FGF10 which bind to epithelial FGFR2b. FGFR2b signaling increases expression of Shh which activates Gli-dependent MC5R, involved in sebocyte differentiation and lipogenesis. Both, FGFR2b as well as IGF1R activate MAPK- and PI3K-dependent pathways, especially the upregulation of SREBP-1, the major transcription factor of lipogenesis. Androgen-mediated upregulation of EGFR-signal transduction increases the transcription factor p63 which induces synthesis of FGFR2b. IGF-1 stimulates 5α-recuctase activity. IGF-1 activates AR by PI3K-dependent phosphorylation of Foxo-1. Keratinoycyte-derived IL-1α induces dermal expression of FGF7 thereby upregulating keratinocyte proliferation in a double paracrine pathway.
