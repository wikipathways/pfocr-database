TLR2-dependent activation of Beta-catenin pathway in dendritic cells induces regulatory responses and attenuates autoimmune inflammation

Innate sensing of zymosan via TLR2 efficiently induces Akt and Erk activation. Akt then activates β-catenin by directly phosphorylating it at Ser552 and indirectly by inactivating GSK-3β activity. Activated β-Catenin interacts with TCF4 in the nucleus, and transcriptionally induces IL-10 and Aldh1a2 gene expression in DCs, which are critical for inducing T regulatory responses and limiting TH1/TH17 cell differentiation. Activation of Erk and Akt/β-catenin pathway work synergistically to induce IL-10 and Aldh1a2. Dectin-1 signaling does not play a major role in β-catenin activation but promotes induction of pro-inflammatory cytokines that are critical of TH1/TH17 cell differentiation.
