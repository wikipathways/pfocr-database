miRNA pathways in myeloid differentiation and myeloid-derived malignancies

miRNA pathways in myeloid differentiation and myeloid-derived malignancies. A: Schematic of adult definitive hematopoiesis. Multipotent, self-renewing HSCs (yellow background) give rise to lymphoid and myeloid multi-potent progenitors (orange background), which differentiate into mature blood cells (red background). B: Schematic of miR-regulated signaling cascades in myeloid differentiation. Cytokines and growth factors bind to their respective receptors to stimulate the activation of downstream signaling cascades. G-CSF signals primarily through the Ras/MEK/Erk MAPK pathway to induce granulocytic differentiation (blue arrows). Aberrations in Ras signaling, via gain-of-function mutations or otherwise, are known causes of MPD and AML (red knob-endline). All human Ras mRNAs possess let-7 binding sites in their 3′UTRs, subjecting the initiation of this cascade to let-7-mediated regulation. miR-34a targets MEK1 to inhibit cell proliferation during megakaryocyte differentiation. Expression of the transcriptional repressor Gfi1 is induced during the transition from CMP to GMP. Gfi1 negatively regulates the expression of miR-21 and miR-196b. Gfi1 loss-of-function mutations in mice and humans arrest myeloid development, and offer one underlying cause of SCN. Co-expression of miR-21 and miR-196b in wild-type bone marrow is sufficient to recapitulate a Gfi1-mutant block in neutrophil formation. In contrast, the AP-1 driven expression of the myeloid-enriched miR-21 amplifies Ras signaling by targeting several direct antagonists of the Ras/MEK/Erk pathway such as BTG2. miR-21 also targets several indirect antagonists of Ras signaling crosstalk within the PI3K/Akt pathway, including PTEN and PDCD4. The JNK and p38 MAPK signaling cascades are also regulated by miRNA. miR-24 represses MKP-7, a negative regulator upstream of JNK and p38 phosphorylation. Additionally, miRNAs play marked roles in the regulation of other lineages by targeting the expression of essential cell-surface receptors. miR-28 targets the thrombopoietin receptor to inhibit megakaryocyte differentiation (orange arrow). Overexpression of miR-28 is associated with MPN (red knob-end line). miR-24 targets the type I activin receptor ALK4 to inhibit erythropoiesis (maroon arrow). miR-221 and miR-222 also inhibit erythropoiesis by targeting the SCF receptor kit.
