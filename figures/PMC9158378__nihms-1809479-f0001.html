Sonic hedgehog signaling pathway is susceptible to both genetic and environmental dysregulation

Canonical Shh signaling consists of SHH production, secretion, receptor binding, signal transduction, and transcription factor activation. Mutations have been identified in humans diagnosed with HPE in each of the proteins included in the schematic except for Smoothened (SMO). Furthermore, environmental chemicals found in commonly consumed plant products have been shown to alter SMO and GLI activity, and ethanol and Vitamin D3 are purported to inhibit Shh signaling at multiple steps along the pathway. Emphasis here has been placed on environmental chemicals with a high probability for human exposure rather than providing a comprehensive list of Shh pathway antagonists.
