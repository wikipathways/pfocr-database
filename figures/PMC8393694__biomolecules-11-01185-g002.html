Targeting of Native Proteins to the Endoplasmic Reticulum-Associated Degradation (ERAD) Pathway: An Expanding Repertoire of Regulated Substrates

The biosynthetic pathway that leads to the generation of ergosterol (in yeast and fungi) and cholesterol (in higher organisms). The ergosterol and cholesterol biosynthetic pathways are catalyzed by a cascade of enzymes, including those shown as well as others that were not highlighted in the text. Select examples of feedback loops are also shown. Key enzymes regulated by ERAD in yeast (ergosterol biosynthetic pathway, in blue) and mammals (cholesterol biosynthetic pathway, in orange) are also noted. Two factors (ApoB and ACAT2) that act after cholesterol synthesis in mammals are also highlighted. Where studied, select examples of proteins that facilitate substrate degradation by the ERAD pathway and that act on specific enzymes in these pathways are displayed in bold.
