Genes and pathways identified from GWAS and/or studies of monogenic allergy-related diseases

Genes and pathways identified from GWAS and/or studies of monogenic allergy-related diseases. Genes identified from GWAS are in bold while genes identified from studies of monogenic diseases are in normal font. A generic cell as well as the skin/mucosal barrier are depicted in order to illustrate the cellular location of gene products. For loci with multiple associated genes where one or more genes have a demonstrated function relevant for atopic disease, only these well-established genes are shown for clarity. It should be noted that the genes and pathways named in this figure might not represent the true causal gene or pathway associated with a given locus since these have not been definitively established for many GWAS findings.GWAS results have been aggregated from the most powerful GWAS (>3 genome-wide significant loci) on allergy/allergic sensitization, eczema, asthma, and eosinophilic esophagitis: Allergy/allergic sensitization: Ramasamy A, 2011, J Allergy Clin Immunol; Hinds DA, 2013, Nat Genet; Ferreira MA, 2013, J Allergy Clin Immunol; BÃ¸nnelykke K, 2013, Nat Genet.
