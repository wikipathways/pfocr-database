Proposed aliphatic pathway regulatory model

Proposed aliphatic pathway regulatory model. A, Aliphatic biosynthetic pathway with structures and enzymes. Enzymes in black are ones with promoters included in this study. Those in gray were not cloned. The use of n indicates the number of carbons introduced to the side chain from the elongation cycle and can vary from 1 to 7. The promoters are color coded based on their pathway membership as follows: indolic s are pink, aliphatic regulatory genes are purple, and aliphatic peripheral genes are red. The aliphatic biosynthetic genes are parsed into the core pathway (green), elongation (blue), modifying (yellow), and seed specific (orange) to visualize regulation along the linear model of the pathway. B, A proposed model of aliphatic pathway regulation. In this model, the pathway is regulated by the JA pathway via the bHLH’s MYC2/MYC3/MYC4 interacting with the MYB28/MYB29/MYB76 proteins and binding the respective promoters. C, Transcriptional analysis of the aliphatic pathway in mutants missing the MYB28 and MYB29 s (blue) or MYC2/MYC3/MYC4 s (red). The value is shown as the relative value of the transcript in the mutant with the wild type set to 1 for each gene. The genes are ordered by their position in the biosynthetic pathway. The transcriptional data presented are from previous publications with full statistical analysis (; ). WT, Wild type JA-ILE, isoleucyl jasmonic acid; JAZ, jasmonate ZIM domain proteins; Coi1, Coronatine Insensitive1; BCAT4, BRANCHCHAIN AMINOTRANSFERASE4; BAT5, BILE ACID TRANSPORTER5; IMD3, ISOPROPYL MALATE DEHYDROGENASE3; MAM1, METHYLIOALKYLMALATE SYNTHASE1; IPMI1, ISOPROPYL MALATE ISOMERASE1; CYP, CYTOCHROME P450; GGP1, GAMMA GLUTAMYL PEPTIDASE1; GSOH, GLUCOSINOLATE HYDROXYLATION; GSOX1-5, GLUCOSINOLATE OXIDASE1-5; GSTF11, GLUTHATHIONE-S-TRANSFERASE11; CSLyase, CYSTEINE-S-CONJUGATE β-LYASE; UGT, UDP-GLYCOSYLTRANSFERASE; SOT, SULFUR TRANSFERASE; AOP3, ALKENYLHYDROXYPROPYL3; BZO1, BENZOYLOXY1.
