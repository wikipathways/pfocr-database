Processes of hypoxia and angiogenesis and the canonical fibroblast growth factor (FGF) signaling pathway and newly acquired insights into their relation to the development of the osteoarthritic (OA) chondrocyte hypertrophic phenotype

Overview of the processes of hypoxia and angiogenesis and the canonical fibroblast growth factor (FGF) signaling pathway and newly acquired insights into their relation to the development of the osteoarthritic (OA) chondrocyte hypertrophic phenotype. The processes and canonical FGF signaling pathway was updated with recent insights in the role of the processes of hypoxia and angiogenesis and the FGF signaling pathway in the hypertrohic switch occuring during OA development. In green pro-chondrogenic relations are shown, while pro-hypertrophic relations are depicted in red.
