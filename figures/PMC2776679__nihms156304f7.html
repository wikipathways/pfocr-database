Egr-1 and Hipk2 are required for the TrkA to p75NTR switch that occurs downstream of IGF1-R

The progressive activation of IGF1-R signaling in the brain that accompanies aging leads to a naturally occurring TrkA to p75NTR switch. The signaling pathway downstream of IGF1-R requires IRS2, phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K), the lipid second messenger phosphatidyilinositol-triphosphate (PIP3), and is under the regulatory control of PTEN and p44/p53 [,]. PTEN converts PIP3 into PIP2, whereas p44 downregulates PTEN. As a result of p44 overexpression, p44+/+ mice display hyperactivation of IGF1-R signaling, premature aging, and an early TrkA to p75NTR switch [,]. The TrkA to p75NTR switch requires Egr-1 and Hipk2; Egr-1 activates the transcription of p75NTR, whereas Hipk2 downregulates TrkA. This event most likely requires additional molecules that provide specificity to the signals mediated by Egr-1 and Hipk2 (indicated as a question mark). The downregulation of TrkA is expected to increase the half-life and signaling activities of p75NTR (see text), therefore “potentiating” the effects caused by the above transcriptional events. Finally, the activation of p75NTR has been linked to neuronal death and increased production of Aβ (please, see text). The neuronal death may –at least in some extent- be activated by Aβ itself acting as a ligand for p75NTR []. The sequence of positive events downstream of IGF1-R is shown by the green arrows. The points of inhibitory control are shown in red. Finally, the effects caused by Egr-1 and Hipk2 downstream of IGF1-R (described in this paper) are in blue.
