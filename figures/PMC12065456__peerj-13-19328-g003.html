Communication between cancer-associated fibroblasts and specific tumor cells via integrins

Various integrins contact proteins in the tumor microenvironment and other receptors on cancer-associated fibroblasts, thus transmitting signals through FAK, JNK, MAPK, and NF-κB signaling pathways. Consequently, signals promote cancer-associated fibroblast differentiation, tumor progression, and invasion.
