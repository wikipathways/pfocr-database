Antitumor mechanism of Tan IIA

The antitumor mechanism of Tan IIA. Tanshinone IIA inhibits cancer cell proliferation, induces differentiation and apoptosis through MAPK, AMPK/Skp2/Parkin, ER stress and mitochondrial pathway, inhibits of invasion and migration through Notch1/NF-κB signaling, induces of autophagy in cancer cells through PI3K/Akt/mTOR signaling pathway, and inhibits angiogenesis by inhibiting the activity of VEGF. The arrow represents the promotion effect, and the T area represents the inhibition effect.
