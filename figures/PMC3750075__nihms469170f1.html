ACK1/TNK2, a non-receptor tyrosine kinase (NRTK), represents a paradigm of tyrosine kinase signaling that appears to be addictive in cancer cells

ACK1/TNK2, a non-receptor tyrosine kinase (NRTK), represents a paradigm of tyrosine kinase signaling that appears to be addictive in cancer cells []. Although it is ubiquitously expressed, its multi-domain structure and its ability to interact with a number of transmembrane RTKs suggests that cell type specific activation of ACK1 is dependent on RTKs that are predominantly operational in a particular cell type [; ; ; ; ; ; ] (). For example, while ACK1 is activated by EGF stimulation in some prostate (LAPC4), ovarian (A2780-CP), lung (H292) and pancreatic (Panc-1) cancer cell lines, other cancer derived lines such as the breast cancer cell line (MCF7) and the pancreatic cancer cell line (CD18) are reliant on insulin stimulation for ACK1 activation [; ; ; ; ; ]. ACK1 signaling acquires further importance due to its ability to be activated in the same cell by multiple ligands. For example, it is activated by both insulin and heregulin ligands in the breast cancer cell line, MCF7 []. This observation becomes particularly significant in cancers that develop resistance to inhibition of a singular RTK pathway and have activated alternate RTK regulated pathways for their survival.
