Mito-nuclear carbon metabolism in Toxoplasma

Toxoplasma actively catabolizes host glucose via a canonical TCA cycle and also catabolizes glutamine via the GABA shunt and TCA cycle. Mitochondrial-localized MDH-FAD may participate in the TCA cycle, whereas cytoplasmic-localized MDH mediates the interconversion of oxaloacetate and malate. Malate is utilized by ME in the nucleus to produce pyruvate, a step that is essential for the growth and survival of Toxoplasma.
