Concurrent BMP Signaling Maintenance and TGFB Signaling Inhibition Is a Hallmark of Natural Resistance to Muscle Atrophy in the Hibernating Bear

A transcriptomic reprogramming of UPS components involved in TGF-β and BMP regulation prevails in muscle during brown bear hibernation. Scheme showing the E3s/DUBs enzymes regulation of brown bear vastus lateralis muscle transcripts involved in TGF-β and BMP signaling pathways and depicting (i) their relationships [,] and (ii) the difference in their expression levels between hibernation and activity periods. Red and green boxes indicate, respectively up- down-regulated genes during hibernation compared to the summer season, and white boxes indicate unchanged genes. Dashed lines show the SMAD-independent pathway and full lines the canonical signaling. Arrows indicate activation and bars inhibition. (n = 6 bears/season, the same individuals were sampled and analyzed in summer and winter, padj < |0.05|; ). SBEs: SMAD Binding Elements. Created with BioRender.com.
