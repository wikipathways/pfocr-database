Molecular Insulin Actions Are Sexually Dimorphic in Lipid Metabolism

Sexual dimorphism in the insulin signaling pathway. The sex-dependent function of proteins in the insulin signaling pathway involves sex-dependent differences in adipose tissue function and consequently in the development and progression of obesity, dyslipidemia and insulin resistance, signs associated with metabolic diseases such as metabolic syndrome, T2DM, CVDs (, , , , , ).
