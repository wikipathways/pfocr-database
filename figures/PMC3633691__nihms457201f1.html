Pathways of putrescine, GABA, ornithine, and arginine degradation

Pathways of putrescine, GABA, ornithine, and arginine degradation. Enzymes 1–4 constitute the transaminase pathway of putrescine degradation. Enzymes 5–10 define the glutamylated putrescine (GP) pathway of putrescine degradation. Enzymes 11–15 show the pathway of arginine degradation. Ornithine is degraded by AstA-dependent succinylation (not shown), which generates succinyl ornithine (). The transaminase and GP pathways are independently regulated (). Ntr regulators control the transaminase pathway and the arginine catabolic pathway (enzymes 1–4 and 11–15), but none of the other enzymes (; ; ) (this paper).
