HIF-mediated gene expression in metabolic reprogramming

HIF-mediated gene expression in metabolic reprogramming. (A) Metabolic reprogramming during embryogenesis results in a shift in bioenergetic metabolism (ATP demand away from mitochondria OXPHOS to a dependence on glucose utilization through glycolysis pathways). These reprogramming changes are driven by physical isolation from atmospheric O2 and the resultant activation of heterodimeric O2-sensing bHLH-PAS nuclear transcription factors belonging to the family of HIFs. During conditions of normoxia, O2 is able to bind to the enzyme prolyl hydroxylase (PHD), which then hydroxylates HIF-1α at two positions, allowing the binding of von Hippel–Lindau tumor suppressor protein (VHL) and activation of ubiquitin-mediated HIF-1α protein degradation. This action prevents HIF-mediated gene expression by removing the transcription factor from the cytosol. Under conditions of hypoxia, HIF-1α is allowed to accumulate and it is translocated to the nucleus where it combines with its heterodimerization partner, HIF-1β/ARNT, and binds to the HRE (5′-ACGTG-3′) to induce the expression of hundreds of genes. (B) The metabolic reprogramming that occurs in developing cells and tissues in response to moderate/severe hypoxia allows the developing conceptus to thrive in normally hostile conditions. Induced expression of HIF target genes (shown in orange/red fonts) shows that the transition to glycolysis dominated bioenergetics along with several other beneficial consequences. With metabolic reprogramming comes increases in glucose uptake/transport, upregulation of all 10 enzymes in the glycolysis pathway, increased pentose phosphate pathway activity and NADPH synthesis, inhibition of TCA cycle and mitochondrial ETC function, altered transcriptional controls, and the modulation of many other pathways, including hexosamine biosynthesis, phospholipid biosynthesis, nucleotide synthesis, and one-carbon metabolism. This representation of HIF-upregulated genes in metabolic reprogramming is not complete and should not be viewed as a comprehensive list [Figure based, in part, from Dengler et al. ()]. ARNT, aryl hydrocarbon receptor nuclear translocator; bHLH, basic helix loop helix; ETC, electron transport chain; HIF, hypoxia-inducible factor; HRE, hypoxia response element; OXPHOS, oxidative phosphorylation; PAS, Per-Arnt-Sim; PHD, prolyl hydroxylase.
