Molecular mechanisms of TNBC

Molecular mechanisms of TNBC. RTKs promote tumorigenesis through the Ras/mitogen-activated protein kinase and PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathways. The phosphorylation of ERK, the Wnt/β-catenin pathway and the TGF-β/Smad pathway activates EMT, and regulates the migration and invasion of tumor cells. In the AR pathway, AR can bind to chaperone proteins to promote the transcription of target genes in the nucleus. At the genetic level, BRCA1/2 mutations can also promote the development of TNBCs. In the tumor microenvironment, tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes and the immune checkpoint system can allow evasion from recognition by the host immune system.
