Physiology of Astroglial Excitability

Membrane Molecular Pathways of Na+Signaling in Astrocytes. Influx of Na+ occurs though (1) Na+-permeable channels, which include ionotropic receptors (AMPAR, NMDAR, P2XR - AMPA, NMDA glutamate receptors and ionotropic purinoceptors); channels of the TRP family (TRPC1/4/5 channels that operate as a part of store-operated Ca2+ entry and hence generate Na+ influx in response to the depletion of ER Ca2+ stores; as well as TRPA and TRPV channels); voltage-dependent Nav channels and [Na+]o-activated Nax channels; (2) through Na+-dependent SLC transporters that include excitatory amino acid transporters EAAT1,2, GABA transporters GAT 1,3, glycine transporters GlyT, NA transporters NET or concentrative adenosine transporters CNT2/3. The main pathway for Na+ exit is provided by Na+-K+ pump, NKA. The Na+-Ca2+ exchanger NCX fluctuates between forward and reverse mode and couples Na+ and Ca2+signaling. Modified from Verkhratsky et al.
