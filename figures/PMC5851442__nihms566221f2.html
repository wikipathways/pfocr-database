Compatible solute biosynthesis

Compatible solute biosynthesis. A representative metabolic pathway illustrating reactions from multiple cyanobacterial species are combined to show the metabolism of compatible solutes and glycogen (purple) as a function of primary photosynthates derived from the Calvin Cycle. The major compatible solutes for freshwater (sucrose and trehalose), marine (glucosylglycerol and glucosylglycerate), and halophilic cyanobacteria (glycine betaine) are shown. It must be stressed that these reactions are not necessarily all contained in any one cyanobacterial species, but are a combination of known pathways from representative freshwater, marine, and halophillic strains (S. elongatus 7942, S. elongatus 7002, Synechocystis 6803 & Aphanothece halophytica).Abbreviations: 3HP – 3-phosphohydroxypyruvate, ADP-GLC – ADP-glucose, DMG – N,N-dimethylglycine,F6P – frustose-6-P, FBP – frusctose-1,6-bisphosphate, FRU – fructose, G1P – glucose-1-phosphate, G3P – glucose-3-phosphate, G3PG – glucosyl-3-phosphoglycerol, G3PGA – glucosyl-3-phosphoglycerate, G6P – glucose-6-phosphate, GAP – glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate, GG – glucosylglycerol, GGA – glucosylglycerate, GG-P – glucosylglycerol-phosphate, GLB – glycine betaine, GLC – glucose, GLG – glycogen, GLY – glycine, P-SER – phosphoserine, S6P – sucrose-6-phosphate, SAR- sarcosine, SER – serine, SUC – sucrose, TRE – trehalose, UDP-GLC – UDP-glucose
