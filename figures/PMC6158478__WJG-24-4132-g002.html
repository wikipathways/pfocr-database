Biochemical pathways of lysophosphatidic acid synthesis and degradation

Biochemical pathways of lysophosphatidic acid synthesis and degradation. LPA can be produced extracellularly and intracellularly as signaling mediators and membrane components, respectively. There are five major pathways for LPA production, (1) the lysophospholipids-ATX (LPLs-ATX) pathway, (2) the phosphatidic acid - phospholipase A1 or A2 (PA-PLA1/PLA2) pathway, (3) the de novo glycerophosphate acyltransferase (GPAT) synthesis pathway, (4) the monoacylglycerol kinase (MAGK) pathway, and (5) the oxidative modification of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) pathway. In the upper right corner of the figure, there are catalytically active isoforms (ATXα, ATXβ, ATXγ, ATXδ and ATXε), which are expressed in different tissues.
