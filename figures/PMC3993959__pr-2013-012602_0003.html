Bioinformatic analyses expose alterations in nucleic acid binding and regulatory proteins

Bioinformatic analyses expose alterations in nucleic acid binding and regulatory proteins. (A) PANTHER analysis revealed enrichment for nucleic acid binding proteins (top). Expansion of this family of proteins identified RNA binding proteins (center) involved in mRNA processing (bottom) as substantially enriched. In addition, transcription factors (top) and other DNA binding proteins (center) were also enriched. (B) KEGG pathway analysis revealed dysregulation of spliceosomal components. Robust coverage of proteins was observed for proteins involved in spliceosomal processing of mRNA (yellow for identifications) and several spliceosomal components were differentially regulated in HIV-1 infected MDM (green = decreased expression in HIV-1-infected cells, pink = increased expression).
