Molecular mechanisms that regulate quiescence and activation of McSCs niche in telogen and anagen, respectively

Molecular mechanisms that regulate quiescence and activation of McSCs niche in telogen and anagen, respectively. In telogen, HFSCs-derived signaling molecules, such as TGF-β, Sfrp1, DKK3, Dab2, Jagged, keep McSCs quiescent by activating the intracellular signaling pathway. In early anagen, paracrine-derived signaling molecules, such as Edn1/2 and Wnt, induce regeneration and migration of McSCs.
