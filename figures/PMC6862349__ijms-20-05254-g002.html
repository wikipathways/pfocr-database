A model for jasmonic acid (JA) biosynthesis, metabolism and signaling pathways

A model for jasmonic acid (JA) biosynthesis, metabolism and signaling pathways. JA-Ile is generated from trienoic fatty acid through the octadecanoid pathways. JA metabolism pathways can convert JA into active or inactive compounds. Coronatine insensitive1 (COI1), a F-box protein, acts as JA receptor, which can bind bioactive JA to trigger the formation of receptor complex COI1-JA-Ile-JAZ, hence to promote the ubiquitination and degradation of jasmonate zim domain (JAZ) proteins, then resulting in the release of downstream transcription factors, such as MYC2, and activation of JA responsive genes. OPC-8:0, 3-oxo-2(cis-2’-pentenyl)-cyclopentane-1-octanoic acid; OPCL, OPC-8:0 CoA ligase; ACX, acyl-CoA oxidase; KAT, 3-ketoacyl-CoA thiolase; MFP, multifunctional protein; JAR1, jasmonate resistant 1; JA-Ile, jasmonoyl-l-isoleucine.
