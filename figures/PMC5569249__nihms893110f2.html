miRNA pathways in mature immune cell function

miRNA pathways in mature immune cell function. Schematic of TLR signaling in response to pathogenic stimuli. Following TLR activation, subsequent downstream signaling through MyD88/TRAF (shown) or TRAM, TRIF, and TIRAP (not shown) results in NFκB nuclear localization, which promotes the expression of several miRNAs, including miR-21, miR-101, miR-147, miR-146a/b, and miR-155. MAPK signaling also enhances the early inflammatory response by inducing the nuclear translocation and activation of AP-1, resulting in the production of early pro-inflammatory mediators such as TNF-α and IL-1. miR-155 enhances inflammation by targeting the TLR antagonists SHIP1 and SOCS1. Conversely, miR-147 antagonizes TLR activation, potentially by targeting upstream signaling molecules such as MyD88. miR-146a/b also negatively regulate TLRs by targeting the pro-inflammatory molecules IRAK1, IRAK2, and TRAF6. Deregulation of TLR signaling occurs in chronic auto-inflammatory disorders, such as RA. Patients with RA also exhibit altered expression of miR-155 and miR-146 (red knob-end line), further suggesting a link between these miRNA and the TLR signaling pathway. TLRs are also expressed on HSCs and drive myeloid-biased proliferation and differentiation in response to stimulation with microbial ligands. The aberrant overexpression of miR-155 in the bone marrow progenitor compartment leads to the development of an MPD in a SHIP1-dependent mechanism (red knob-end line). Conversely, miR-146a (and miR-145—not pictured) are deleted in 5q–Syndrome, a subtype of MDS. The MAPK and PI3K/Akt signaling cascades also play an important role in the regulation of TLR signaling. MAPK signaling is associated with early, NFκB-independent inflammation and the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines TNFα, IL-1β, and IL-6 via AP-1. miR-101 enhances the inflammatory response by targeting MKP-1, an antagonist of both JNK and p38 MAPKs. In contrast, PI3K/Akt signaling has been reported to negatively regulate the inflammatory response via the production of IL-10. miR-21 is robustly upregulated in response to TLR stimulation, and may target the PI3K inhibitor PTEN to drive enhanced PI3K/Akt signaling and the resolution of inflammation by enhanced IL-10 production. AP-1, activator protein 1; IRAK1/2, IL-1R-associated kinase 1 and 2; MAPK, mitogen-activated protein kinase; MDS, myelodysplastic syndrome; miR, microRNA; MPK1, map kinase phosphatase 1; MPD, myeloproliferative disease; MyD88, myeloid differentiation primary response gene 88; NFκB, nuclear factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer of activated B cells; PAMP, pathogen-associated molecular pattern; PI3K, phosphatidylinosital 3-kinases; PTEN, phosphatase and tensin homolog; RA, rheumatoid arthritis; SHIP1, SH2-domain-containing inositol-5-phosphatase 1; SOCS1, suppressor of cytokine signaling 1; TLR, Toll-like receptor; TRAF6, TNF-receptor-associated factor 6. Black arrows represent normal TLR signaling, red lines indicate abnormal signaling, arrowhead represents causal mechanism, ⊥-ended dashed lines represent inhibitory mechanism, knob-end represents aberrant expression associated with abnormal signaling/phenotype.
