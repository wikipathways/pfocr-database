JNK pathways controlling contradictory responses in neurons

JNK pathways controlling contradictory responses in neurons. Depending on the context, axonal injury can result in neuronal cell death (A), axonal degeneration (B) and/or regeneration (C), and JNK is required for triggering each of these different responses. Abbreviations: JNK, c-Jun amino-terminal kinase; MKK4/7, mitogen-activated protein kinase kinase 4/7; DLK, dual leucine zipper kinase; JIP, JNK-interacting protein; ROS, reactive oxygen species; Bcl, B-cell lymphoma; ATF, activating transcription factor; MAP1B, microtubule associated protein 1B; DR6, death receptor 6; STAT, signal transducer and activator of transcription; Sprr, small proline rich proteins; Klf, Kruppel-like transcription factor.
