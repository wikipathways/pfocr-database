CDK4 in the Pathogenesis of Pancreatic Cancer

Pathway map showing the mitotic cell cycle progression, through the events of DNA replication (S phase) and mitosis (M phase), separated temporally by the G1 and G2 phases. Cyclin-dependent kinases (CDKs), activated by specific cyclins to form CDK/cyclin-complexes, modulate the activity of key substrates to regulate the cell’s progression through the cell cycle phases. Cyclin-CDK inhibitors (CKIs), including p16INK4a, p15INK4a, p27Kip1 and p21Cip1 contribute to the negative regulation of CDK activities. CDK4/6 (the focus of this study) is shown in red. Image taken from: KEGG PATHWAY database (Kyoto, Japan) []. Accession number: map04110.
