Paeonol protects against atherogenesis

Schematic diagram of molecular mechanism underlying paeonol protects against atherogenesis. Paeonol-induced ROS elimination is associated with inhibition of NOX/ROS pathway. Paeonol mainly suppresses the MAPKs and NF-κB cascade to weaken inflammatory responses and EC apoptosis. In addition, paeonol activates PPAR-α/CPT-1 pathway and represses SREBP-1c signaling to accelerate TG catabolism and block TG synthesis, respectively. Then, paeonol weakens foam cell formation by increasing level of reverse transport axis LXRα/ABCA1 and reducing activities of JNK signaling involved in CD36 production. Moreover, paeonol mediates autophagic factors and cell cycle-related single molecules to block the VSMC proliferation via the AMPK/mTOR pathway.
