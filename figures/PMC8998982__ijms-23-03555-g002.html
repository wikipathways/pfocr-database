Cyclin-Dependent Kinase Synthetic Lethality Partners in DNA Damage Response

MYC-dependent control of gene expression and synthetic lethality partners of MYC. In response to mitogenic stimuli, the RAS-RAF-MEK-ERK pathway triggers MYC-dependent gene expression of enzymes involved in the control of glycolysis, glutaminolysis, nucleotide metabolism, unfolded protein response, general protein synthesis, and ribosomal protein synthesis, many of which were identified as synthetic lethality partners of MYC (indicated as pink shadows). For example, CDK9 and CDK12, which control the mRNA elongation by RNA polymerase II via phosphorylation of the CTD domain of the enzyme, were identified as MYC-SL partners. Regulators of MYC stability that prevent the proteasomal degradation of the protein, and four DDR components, namely ATR, CHK1, CDK1, and DNA-PKcs, also constitute a pool of MYC-SL partners.
