Identification of kinase inhibitor targets in the lung cancer microenvironment by chemical and phosphoproteomics

Depicted are all proteins identified by drug affinity chromatography after background subtraction (as described for ) supplemented with all phosphoproteins identified by phosphoproteomics. Protein-protein interactions between observed proteins were retrieved from public databases. Each phosphosite is indicated as a red circle around the respective protein node. Red protein nodes indicate proteins that are (at least in part) of “mouse” origin in the respective xenograft samples and thus may originate from the tumor microenvironment, grey nodes indicate proteins never observed with any mouse-specific peptides. Dark blue pie slices: proteins involved in the MAPK signaling pathway, light blue pie slices: proteins involved in the integrin signaling pathway.
