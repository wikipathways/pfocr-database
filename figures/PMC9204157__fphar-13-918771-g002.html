Targeting Growth Factor and Cytokine Pathways to Treat Idiopathic Pulmonary Fibrosis

The critical role of the TGF-β pathway and drugs targeting the TGF-β pathway in IPF. (A) Latent TGF-β (TGF-β trapped in LAP) binds to ECM through LTBP. (B) TGF-β is detached from LAP and activated only in the presence of specific stimuli. (C) Activated TGF-β activates the downstream Smad-dependent pathway (canonical pathway) and Smad-independent pathway (non-canonical pathway) by combining with TBR. (D) The TGF-β pathway ultimately causes phenotypic reprogramming of AECs, fibroblasts and immune cells and influences fibroblast-to-myofibroblast differentiation, ECM synthesis, angiogenesis, Th2 polarization, and apoptosis.
