Epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR)/extracellular signal-regulated kinases 1 and 2 (ERK1/2) signaling pathway

Hypothetical mechanism for the stimulation of the epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR)/extracellular signal-regulated kinases 1 and 2 (ERK1/2) signaling pathway in cumulus cells of porcine cumulus-oocyte complexes (COCs) derived from small follicles (< 4 mm). Intercellular communication between mural granulosa cells, cumulus cells, and oocytes occurs through paracrine and autocrine signals and gap junctional communication (GJ). In vivo, follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH) induces luteinizing hormone (LH) receptors (LHR) and EGFR that can be stimulated by LH and amphiregulin (AREG), epiregulin (EREG), and betacellulin (BTC), respectively. In vitro, developing oocytes cannot stimulate the EGFR/ERK1/2 pathway without the addition of dibutyryl cAMP sodium salt (dbcAMP) and oocyte-secreted factors (OSFs) leading to a low developmental competence of the oocyte. Addition of dbcAMP and OSFs, such as bone morphogenetic protein (BMP15) and growth differentiation factor 9 (GDF9), in an AREG-stimulated IVM system promotes the EGFR/ERK1/2 signaling pathway, consequently with an improved developmental potential of the oocyte.
