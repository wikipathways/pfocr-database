Pathobiology of biliary epithelia

A. Cholangiocyte secretory functions. The most widely accepted model of ductal bile formation involves [] secretin activation of the cAMP-PKA signaling pathway, which then leads to [] activation of CFTR, causing extrusion of Cl−. This then stimulates the [] Cl−/HCO3− exchanger which secretes HCO3− and creates an osmotic gradient that leads to the [] passive movement of water across AQP1. More recently, an alternative hypothesis suggests that [] activation of CFTR leads to the release of ATP, which then phosphorylates IP3R on the endoplasmic reticulum. This causes the release of Cl− via the [] Ca2+-dependent Cl− channel, which creates a gradient leading to [] activation of the Cl−/HCO3− exchanger. Ca2+ is also transported via a [] Ca2+- and cAMP-independent Cl− channel. Finally, the apical membrane also contains [] K+ channels (SK2 and IK-1) and a [] Na+-HCO3− cotransporter (mice). On the basolateral domain, there are two types of K+ channels: [] SK2 and [] IK-1. There is also [] a Na+/K− ATPase, [] a Na+/H+ exchanger (NHE1), [] a Na+/K+/Cl− cotransporter, [] a Na+/HCO3+ cotransporter (rats), [] a Na+-dependent Cl−/HCO3− exchanger (humans), and water channels, [] AQP1 and [] AQP4. Within the cell, [] carbonic anhydrase allows for the production of HCO3−. Abbreviations: ATP, adenosine triphosphate; AQP, aquaporin; cAMP, cyclic adenosine monophosphate; CFTR, cystic fibrosis transmembrane conductance regulator; IK-1, intermediate-conductance K+ channel; PKA, protein kinase A; NHE, Na+/H+ exchanger; SK2, small conductance K+ channel.B. Cholangiocyte absorptive functions. Cholangiocytes have a number of transporters that allow the absorption of a variety of molecules []. Glucose is absorbed through SGLT1, Na+ is absorbed through [] NHE2 and [] NHE3, glutamate is absorbed through a glumate transporter, bile acids are absorbed through [] ASBT and [] iBAT and [] water is absorbed through AQP1. On the basolateral membrane, [] glucose is transported via GLUT1, bile is transported via [] Ostα-Ostβ and [] t-ASBT, water is transported via [] AQP1 and [] AQP4. Lastly, the basolateral membrane contains a [] Na+/H+ exchanger (NHE3). Abbreviations: ASBT, apical sodium-dependent bile acid transporter; AQP, aquaporin; BA, bile acids; GLUT, glucose transporter; GT, glutamate transporter; iBAT, ileal bile acid transporter; NHE, Na+/H+ exchanger; OST, organic solute transporter alpha; SGLT, Na+-Glucose cotransporter.
