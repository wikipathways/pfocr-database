PrRP physiological functions and signaling—summary

PrRP physiological functions and signaling—summary. PrRP and its agonist exerts its effect through GPR10. Blue arrow represents activation of the signaling pathway, T-bar represents blocking of the signaling pathway. PrRP stimulated calcium release (Ca2+) in calcium mobilization assay and rapidly activated extracellular signal-regulated protein kinase (ERK – blue). It also activated c-Jun N-terminal protein kinase (JNK—light blue) and phosphorylated cAMP response element-binding protein (CREB—grey). Pertussis toxin (PTX—black) blocked the ERK and Akt activation induced by PrRP. PrRP activated the PI3K B/Akt-mammalian target of rapamycin (PI3K-Akt-mTOR) pathways in leiomyoma cells (PI3K—pink, PKB/Akt—green,.mTOR—yellow). PrRP significantly stimulated both the PKA (dark green) and PKC (orange) pathways.
