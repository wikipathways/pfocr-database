Genes with mutations that cause monogenic, severe T-cell immunodeficiency in humans can be intrinsic to the thymic epithelium or to T cells

Genes with mutations that cause monogenic, severe T-cell immunodeficiency in humans can be intrinsic to the thymic epithelium or to T cells. (A) Genetic defects that are intrinsic to thymic epithelial cells ultimately affect the antigen-presentation pathway. (B) Genetic defects that are intrinsic to T cells include those that affect T-cell receptor (TCR) signaling, cytokine signaling, somatic recombination, or basic cellular processes. Other genes that are important to these pathways or processes, but have not been linked to severe T-cell deficiency in humans are shown in grey. Copyright © 2008. Nature Publishing Group.
