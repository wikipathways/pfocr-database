Changes of FAs metabolism in hamster liver of HFD group (A) and HFD+ADT group (B)

Changes of FAs metabolism in hamster liver of HFD group (A) and HFD+ADT group (B). Compared with the HFD group, ADT administration significantly inhibited the overexpression of ACC1, FAS and SCD1, activated FADS1 and FADS2, thereby reducing endogenous synthesis of PA, SA and OA, and improved the desaturated metabolic pathway of EFAs. In addition, ADT upregulated the levels of FATPs, L-FABP, and CPT1α, thus increasing the uptake, binding/isolation, transport, and β-oxidation of FAs. More importantly, ADT obviously increased the expression of Mitofusin1 to promote mitochondrial fusion state and maximal β-oxidation. PA, palmitic acid; SA, stearic acid; OA, oleic acid; EFAs, essential fatty acids, including LA and ALA; MFN1, Mitofusin1.
