Lonafarnib Inhibits Farnesyltransferase via Suppressing ERK Signaling Pathway to Prevent Osteoclastogenesis in Titanium Particle-Induced Osteolysis

Proposed scheme for Lon suppression of osteoclast production. Titanium particles stimulate macrophages and other cells to release pro-inflammatory substances (such as IL-1β and TNF-α) which bind to the inflammatory receptors or increase osteoclast-related cytokines, such as RANKL. When RANKL binds to RANK, NF-κB and MAPKs (including P38, JNK and ERK) pathways are activated, following the nuclear translocation and auto-amplification of NFATc1. As a result, the osteoclast-relevant genes such as Ctsk, c-fos, Mmp9, Dcstamp, and Acp5 are upregulated. Our results demonstrate for the first time that Lon inhibited FTase to attenuate the activation of ERK pathways, subsequently resulted in decreasing of NFATc1 as well as its downstream gens.
