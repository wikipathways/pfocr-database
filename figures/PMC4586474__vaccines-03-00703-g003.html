TGF-Beta-IDO-SHP axis

The TGF-β-IDO-SHP axis. The TGF-β-IDO-SHP axis activates the non-canonical NF-κB pathway []. In splenocytes, SHP-1 inhibits the protein kinase IRAK1 and tips the balance of activation of the canonical versus non-canonical NF-κB signaling pathway in favor of the latter, resulting in upregulated production of type I interferon []. In addition to TGF-β, the aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) has also been shown to play a role in IDO production [,]. Interestingly, kynurenines produced from IDO’s enzymatic activity can also bind and activate as an endogenous ligand, AhR [,].
