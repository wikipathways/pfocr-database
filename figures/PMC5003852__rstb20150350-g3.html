Main mediators of NVC responses to distinct cortical afferent pathways

Main mediators of NVC responses to distinct cortical afferent pathways. Various cell types contribute to the changes in diameter of the cortical microvessels that lead to the haemodynamic response. Depending on the subcortical pathway, pyramidal cells act directly through COX-2 derived PGE2 or indirectly by releasing glutamate (Glu) that then acts on perivascular astrocytes and GABA interneurons. GABA released from different subtypes of interneurons, depending on the afferent input, modulates pyramidal cell activity through GABA-A receptor activation. Astrocytes can dilate microvessels by releasing EETs and activation of BK channels, with subsequent activation of Kir channels in smooth muscle cells. The black line shows the known contribution of mediators, the dotted line indicates that these mediators have not been fully characterized (BF) or that their role remains ambiguous (LC), and the interrupted line indicated no involvement in the NVC response (BF). Other mediators or modulators have been identified depending on the pathway, and involvement of endothelial pathways is not illustrated.
