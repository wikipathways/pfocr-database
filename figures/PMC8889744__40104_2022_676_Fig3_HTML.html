Review of the amino acid metabolism in placental function response to fetal loss and low birth weight in pigs

The L-arginine-NO biosynthetic pathway regulates key vasculogenic and angiogenic factors in endothelial cells and vascular smooth muscle cells in the placenta. Arginine stimulates placental NO synthesis mainly by enhancing the generation of BH4, an essential cofactor for NOSs. Estrogen and progesterone augmented the constitutive and inducible NOS activities and the BH4 concentration in the porcine placenta, thus playing synergetic roles in promoting the placental NO synthesis. The porcine placenta lacks arginase and arginine decarboxylase and thus cannot synthesize ornithine from arginine. NO interacts with critical mediators of angiogenesis (VEGF, PIGF, and the angiopoietins). The induction of angiogenesis by angiopoietin and VEGF proteins is dependent on the amount of VEGF and Ang-1/Ang-2 in endothelial cells. Besides, NO diffuses to the underlying VSMCs where they act on GTP to produce cGMP via guanylate cyclase, which is capable of dilating blood vessels.
