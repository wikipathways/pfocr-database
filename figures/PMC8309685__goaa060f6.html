lncRNA-PACER upregulates COX-2 and PGE2 through the NFKB pathway to promote the proliferation and invasion of colorectal-cancer cells

LPS, TNF-α, and other physiological or non-physiological stimuli regulate the phosphorylation and subsequent degradation of IκB, and the subsequent nuclear translocation of NF-κB. The homologous dimer of NF-κB1, which comprises two p50 subunits, is inactive for COX-2 transcription because of the lack of an activated domain. PACER physically interacts and sequesters excessive p50, and transforms the homologous dimer p50/p50 into a p65/p50 heterodimer with transcription activity, thus activating the transcription of COX-2. Increased COX-2 enhances PGE2 synthesis, which promotes the proliferation, invasion, and metastasis of CRC cells. In turn, the high expression of PGE2 decreases PACER expression and provides a negative feedback. PACER, p50-associated cyclooxygenase-2 extragenic RNA; COX-2, cyclooxygenase-2; CRC, colorectal cancer; LPS, lipopolysaccharide; NF-κB, nuclear factor kappa B.
