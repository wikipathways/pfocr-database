Studies on hydrogenase

The proposed mechanism of activation and catalysis by [NiFe]-hydrogenase. The activation/inactivation pathway and the proposed catalytic cycle are shown in the upper 2 rows and in the lower circuits, respectively. For Dg enzyme, the EPR signal and FTIR wave number are correlated to the structure (3 diatomic ligands to Fe, omitted for clarity) in each shaded box: e.g., the top left box illustrates that the unready enzyme has an EPR signal Ni-A, and the FTIR wave number of its CO ligand is 1947 cm−1. Ni-SI, Ni-SU and Ni-R are EPR silent (−). The blank circle between Ni and Fe indicates the vacant bridging site. Cys* is the Cys residue substituted with Sec residue in the [NiFeSe]-hydrogenase, and is Cys546 for DvM enzyme, Cys530 for Dg enzyme, or Sec493 for D. baculatum enzyme. The plain box at the top right shows the Ni-Fe center of DvM enzyme with sulfur species as a bridging ligand to Ni and Fe.) Ni(III)-S-Cys* of Ni-C1952 form is isoelectronic with Ni(II)-S+-Cys*, both of which contribute to the EPR signal of Ni-C1952.) The absence of the bridging ligand in Ni-SI form was confirmed by X-ray crystallography,) and the presence of the bridging hydride ligand in Ni-C form was suggested by structural) and spectroscopic) studies. The potentiometric titration of the enzyme suggested the one-electron Nernstian process between Ni-C and Ni-R forms.) The dotted boxes on the catalytic cycle show expected intermediates not detectable by EPR or FTIR. The bound H2 of the transient form is shown as the 6th ligand to Ni, but other forms such as H2 as the bridging ligand to Ni and Fe, or as the 6th ligand exclusive to Fe, may be possible. The midpoint potentials for individual steps are pH dependent, and those at pH 7.7 for Dg enzyme. The midpoint potentials for the DvM enzyme forms in the catalytic circuit are similar to those for the Dg enzyme forms.)
