JAK-STAT pathway mutations and variants in human disease

Type I/II cytokiens are further classified into 7 groups based on their shared receptor subunits: γc, hormone-like, βc, dimeric, gp130, interferons, and IL-10 family cytokines. IL-13 uses a “private” receptor system but is often grouped with the γc cytokines because it shares the IL-4 receptor alpha subunit (IL4-Rα). Activating mutations (red) are reported in human JAK1, JAK2, JAK3, and TYK2, whereas inactivating mutations (blue) are reported in JAK1, TYK2, and JAK3. Amongst the STAT genes, activating mutations or variants are reported in STAT1, STAT2, STAT3, STAT4, STAT5B, and STAT6; inactivating mutations are reported in STAT1, STAT2, STAT3, STAT4, and STAT5B.
