Kinase-Catalyzed Biotinylation to Map Cell Signaling Pathways: Application to Epidermal Growth Factor Signaling

Comprehensive network mapping of the K-BMAPS hits. The 219 hits were assessed using GeneMANIA 3.5.0 application in Cytoscape 3.7.1, Phosida, PhosphositePlus, and the literature for physical and functional relationships with EGFR (red), downstream kinases in the EGFR pathway, or specific biological functions. Hit proteins that are a known substrate or have a direct protein–protein interaction with either EGFR or major EGFR pathway kinases (AKT1, ERK1, PKM, or ILK) are displayed (blue). Proteins that are associated with specific biological functions, such as GTPase-signaling, the Wnt pathway, cell adhesion, ubiquitination, transcription, translation, and cell cycle progression, were also clustered (yellow). Hit proteins that indirectly associated with EGFR are shown in the outer circle of the map (black).
