Mining for novel cyclomaltodextrin glucanotransferases unravels the carbohydrate metabolism pathway via cyclodextrins in Thermoanaerobacterales

Proposed CM-CD pathway in G− bacteria (I), G+ bacteria (II), and archaea (III). Note the proteins involved in the four steps of the CM-CD pathway. (i) Synthesis: four-domain CGTases in G−, three- and conventional five-domain CGTases in G+ , and five-domain CGTases in archaea with either ECBM20/Earch domains at the C-terminal region. (ii) Translocation/Internalization: MdxEFG-(X/MsmX) transporter system. The CDP in G− is also shown. Note that while the cyclo/maltodextrin-binding protein MdxE is an untethered component of the periplasmic space in G−, it is predicted to be anchored to the cytoplasmic membrane outer surface via a lipid moiety in G+ and archaea. Although the MdxX enzyme is a dedicated ATPase in G−, MsmX is a promiscuous ATPase in G+ and archaea. Cyclo/maltodextrin translocation into the cytoplasm by the two permease subunits MdxFG is triggered by the ATPase activity of MdxX/MsmX. (iii) Degradation: CDase, GA, and GP. Hexagons represent individual glucose molecules. (iv) Metabolic assimilation: Pgi, PfkA, and PykF. While Pgm and HK are not included in the CM-CD gene clusters of Fig. , the asterisks in HK*, Pgi*, and PykF* represent the modified EMP pathway in archaea.
