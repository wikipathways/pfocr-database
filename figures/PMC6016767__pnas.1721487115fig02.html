Three TFs—CRF4, SNZ, and CDF1—regulate 53% of the N-uptake/assimilation pathway genes

Three TFs—CRF4, SNZ, and CDF1—regulate 53% of the N-uptake/assimilation pathway genes. A time-based machine learning approach DFG (, ) was used to infer TF-target influence in an N-response GRN in shoots (). Validated genome-wide targets of three TFs in this GRN—CRF4, SNZ, and CDF1—are shown to regulate 53% (35/65) genes in the N-uptake/assimilation pathway (SI Appendix, Fig. S5B and Dataset S1, Tables S5–S7 and S15). TF edges to N-responsive genes (green nodes) that are predicted by the GRN and validated by TF perturbations are shown by asterisks and thicker edge width (Dataset S1, Table S14). Gray circles indicate other cellular processes validated to be regulated by these three TFs (Dataset S1, Tables S8–S10).
