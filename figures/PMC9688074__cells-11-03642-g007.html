Long Noncoding RNAs in the Regulation of Cellular Immune Response and Inflammatory Diseases

Mechanism of lncRNAs in osteoarthritis. In IL-1β-induced OA model, overexpression of lncRNA HOTAIR promotes the expression of MMPs and exacerbates OA. Overexpression of lncRNA HOTAIR increases the activity of Wnt/β-catenin pathway by regulating miR-17-5p/FUT2 axis, which aggravates the injury and apoptosis of chondrocytes. Overexpression of lncRNA OIP5-ASI inhibits apoptosis and inflammation by targeting miR-29b-3p/PGRN. Emodin inhibits the Notch/NF-κB pathway by upregulating lncRNA TUG1, which alleviates the apoptotic and inflammatory response of chondrocytes. LncRNA ARFRP1 is significantly upregulated in LPS-induced chondrocytes, and the downregulation of lncRNA ARFRP1 inhibits activation of NF-κB signaling pathway by targeting miR-15a-5p/TLR4 axis. Knockdown of lncRNA FOXD2-AS1 alleviates inflammatory injury by regulating the miR-27a-3p/TLR4 axis.
