Synopsis of significant mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) signaling pathway in the dentate gyrus (DG) of mice exposed to MPTP according to the Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) analysis

Synopsis of significant mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK) signaling pathway in the dentate gyrus (DG) of mice exposed to MPTP according to the Kyoto Encyclopedia of Genes and Genomes (KEGG) analysis. Notably, MPTP intoxication was associated with decreased expression of genes (blue) involved in transcription [FBJ murine osteosarcoma viral oncogene homolog (Fos), and nuclear receptor subfamily 4, group A, member 1 (Nr4a1)] and MAPK phosphatase activity [dual specificity phosphatase 1 (Dusp1) and Dual specificity phosphatase 6 (Dusp6)].
