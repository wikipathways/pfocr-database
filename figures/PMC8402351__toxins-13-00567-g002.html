Panorama of the Intracellular Molecular Concert Orchestrated by Actinoporins, Pore-Forming Toxins from Sea Anemones

Intracellular MAPK signaling pathways activated under extracellular signaling and StII action. Membrane perturbation induced by various stimuli provokes the activation of the MAPKs cascade. The targets of MAPKs in the cytosol and nucleus are responsible for the biological response leading to cell survival or death. Pore formation by StII induces ERK1/2 activation through MEK1/2 pathway. In normal BHK cells, the inhibition of ERK1/2 increases cell death, suggesting their involvement in cellular defense []. However, the ERK1/2 activation in tumor Raji cells is related to cellular death []. Pore formation by StII causes K+ efflux through the StII’s pore and an increase in intracellular Ca2+ from the external medium and ER []. As a result, MAP3K activation takes place, eliciting p38 phosphorylation. The activation of p38 kinase is associated with cell survival in BHK [] and P3X63Ag cells (unpublished results). * Indicates those processes in which the intervention of StII has been identified.
