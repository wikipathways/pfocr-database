Wnt/Beta-catenin cell signaling pathway

Wnt/β-catenin cell signaling pathway. (A) In the absence of stimuli (OFF-STATE), the Fz receptors are regulated by a group of antagonist proteins, such as SFRP, which prevent further receptor-ligand interaction. In the cytoplasm, a degradation complex is formed, to which β-catenin is recruited and phosphorylated at specific residues by the GSK3β and CK1 kinases. These phosphorylated sites are recognized by βTrCP ubiquitin ligase, which mediates β-catenin proteosomal degradation. In the nucleus, the Groucho/TLE repressor binds to TCF/LEF, avoiding its transcriptional activation; (B) In the presence of Wnt ligands (ON-STATE), LRP5/6 and Fz dimerize; subsequently, Axin binds to LRP5/6, whereas Disheveled (Dvl) interacts with Fz, allowing Axin-Dvl binding and the disassembly of the β-catenin degradation complex. Finally, β-catenin is released in the cytoplasm and translocated to the nucleus, aided by its binding partner FOXM1, where it binds to TCF/LEF and detaches the Groucho/TLE repressor.
