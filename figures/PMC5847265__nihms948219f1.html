Pediatric Pharmacogenomics: A systematic assessment of ontogeny and genetic variation to guide the design of statin studies in children

Statins as a class inhibit endogenous cholesterol production by competitive inhibition of HMG-CoA reductase (HMGCR), which catalyzes conversion of HMG-CoA to mevalonate, an early rate-limiting step in cholesterol synthesis. The effect of statins is shown in the context of genes involved in the metabolism and transport of plasma lipoproteins that affect atherosclerosis and cardiovascular disease risk. A more detailed description of individual genes and gene products can be found at http://www.pharmgkb.org/pathway/PA2031 (accessed May 14, 2012).
