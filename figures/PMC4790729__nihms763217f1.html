Therapeutic Potential of Insulin-Like Growth Factor-1 in Central Nervous System Disorders

IGF-1 acts on its receptor to initiate several pathways responsible for protein translation, which in turn aids in cell growth, migration and differentiation. Activation of the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway by IGF-1 leads to activation of S6 kinase proteins and phosphorylation of eIF4E, which in turn lead to increased mRNA translation ().
