Reactions involved in the flux of fatty acids into . De novo glycerolipid synthesis is shown in white arrows, acyl transfer reactions are indicated by dashed lines, and the movement of the lipid glycerol backbone through the pathway is shown in solid lines. Major reactions (in thick lines) controlling the flux of fatty acid from into are as follows: acylation reaction of acyl editing by (A); deacylation reaction of acyl editing by the reverse action of or phospholipase A (B); and the interconversion of and by (C). Substrates are in boldface, enzymatic reactions are in italics. FAD, Fatty acid desaturase; FAS, fatty acid synthase; GPAT, acyl-CoA: acyltransferase; LPA, lysophosphatidic acid; LPAT, acyl-CoA:LPA acyltransferase; PA, phosphatidic acid; PLC, phospholipase C; PLD, phospholipase D.
