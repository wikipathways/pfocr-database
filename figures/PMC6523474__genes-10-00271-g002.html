Acute phase response (APR) is central to the FL induced response in skin, brain, and liver of all three model organisms

The acute phase response (APR) is central to the FL induced response in skin, brain, and liver of all three model organisms. Four main regulators (IL1B, TNF, OSM, and IL-6) are central to this response (top of pathway in dark orange). The expression of these regulators controls four major signaling cascades, the MEK/MKK cascade (dark blue), the NFkβ cascade (green), the JAK/STAT cascade (purple), and the Ras/Raf cascade (light blue). The pathways from , and that overlap with the APR (designated AP in each table) all fall into one of these signaling cascades under the control of one of the four primary regulators (Table on the right). The products following the APR are highlighted in light orange and each helps induce an inflammatory and/or immune response in the organism. Pathways in the table on the right are in red font if they are up-modulated and green font if they are down-modulated. Pathways labeled by an * are in the mouse liver as well as other organs tested. The color indicated for these pathways follows the primary response and is; therefore, opposite in the mouse liver.
