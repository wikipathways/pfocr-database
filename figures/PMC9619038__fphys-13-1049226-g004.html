Editorial: Sirtuins and brain homeostasis

Anti-neuroinflammatory actions of sirtuins, through inactivation of p65 subunit of NF-κB, activation of DNMT1 and anti-oxidative effects. Aβ aggregates activate cell membrane receptors that signaling though the NF-κB signaling pathway. SIRT1 exerts neuroprotective and anti-inflammatory effects, inhibiting both Aβ production and pro-inflammatory activation of microglial cells through abrogating NF-κB and IL-1β dependent signaling pathways . Both SIRT1 and SIRT2 can inhibit neuroinflammation via direct deacetylation of p65. SIRT1 and mitochondrial SIRT3 counteracts oxidative stress through FoxO3A deacetylation, resulting in MnSOD activation and/or direct MnSOD activation via deacetylation. Thus limited formation of ROS inhibits NLRP3 activation. SIRT2 may inhibit NLRP3 through deacetylation of α-tubulin.
