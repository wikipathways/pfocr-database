Proteins Secreted By Embryonic Stem Cells Activate Cardiomyocytes Through Ligand Binding Pathways

All transcripts from ligands through transcription factors (except LimK) depicted in the model exhibited a statistically significant increase in conditioned media at 24 or 48 hours compared to the level at the beginning of the study. None were significantly increased in unconditioned media. The boxes contain proteins significantly elevated in the conditioned media which could act as ligands for the specified pathways---nomenclature provided in . Transcripts associated with the GPCR (G-Protein-Coupled Receptor) pathway and the process of cytokinesis are depicted. Transcripts associated with the RTK (Receptor Tyrosine kinase) pathway are also shown including transcription factors (FOS, FOSL1, JUNB, JUND and MYCN) associated with cyclin activation and proliferation. A separate pathway associated with cardiomyocyte differentiation exhibited significant elevation of transcription factors AP2a, MEF2D, STAT1 and 3. Transcripts associated with cytokine activation of the JAK-STAT pathway included multiple JAKs and elevation of STAT3 and STAT1 associated with cell cycle progression. The microarray intensity data representing nomenclature of all transcripts associated with these signal transduction pathways are provided in .
