Endothelial cell metabolism in normal and diseased vasculature

A, In diabetes, hyperglycemia triggers mainly increased ROS production through eNOS uncoupling and PPP impairment resulting in stalled glycolytic flux with glycolytic intermediates being diverted into alternative metabolic pathways leading to additional excess ROS and AGEs production. B, Atherosclerosis is characterized at a metabolic level mainly by eNOS uncoupling resulting in excess ROS production and loss of NO-dependent vasodilation.
