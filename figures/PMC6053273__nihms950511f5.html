The neurogenic activity of (2R,6R)-hydroxynorketamine

Schematic representation of the neurogenic activity of (2R,6R)-hydroxynorketamine. The (2R,6R)-HNK-associated activation of the AMPA-selective Glutamate Receptor 1 (GluR1) allows entry of extracellular Ca2+ and subsequent activation of mTORC1, a key step in mitochondrial biogenesis and activity. Accumulation of metabolites such as citrate and L-leucine ensues with feed-forward mechanism by which CREB posttranslational modification via acetylation and phosphorylation impacts expression and translation of neurogenic genes and synaptic Trkb signaling pathway components to promote neurogenesis and synaptogenesis.
