MICRORNAs IN ER STRESS: DIVERGENT ROLES IN CELL FATE DECISIONS

Several microRNAs have recently been described to play a role in the cell fate outcome, survival or apoptosis, under conditions of endoplasmic reticulum (ER) stress. The three canonical unfolded protein response sensors, Activating Transcription Factor 6 alpha (ATF6α), Inositol Requiring Enzyme 1 alpha (IRE1α) and Protein kinase-like ER Kinase (PERK) are activated under ER stress. ATF6α inhibits miR-455, which inhibits calreticulin, and mir-702 is an inhibitor of ATF6α. Mir-30c-2-3p antagonizes the IRE1α target mRNA X-box binding protein-1 (XBP-1). IRE1α directly cleaves several microRNAs, including miR-17, miR-34a, miR-96 and miR-125b. In itself, IRE1α is repressed by the microRNA, miR-199a-5p. IRE1α-dependent degradation of these microRNAs derepresses caspase 2 and thioredoxin interacting protein (TXNIP), thus, promoting apoptosis. PERK induces the XBP-1 inhibitory miR-30c-2-3p. It also induces miR-211, which inhibits Chop transcription via hypermethylation of its promoter. CHOP-dependent microRNA miR-708 promotes cell survival by inhibiting the ER folded protein, Rhodopsin.
