Progress on Ras/MAPK Signaling Research and Targeting in Blood and Solid Cancers

G-protein-coupled receptor subunits Gαs and Gαi interaction with the Ras-Raf pathway. When activated by a ligand, the GPCR is induced to exchange its bound GDP for GTP, freeing the α subunit to act on the cell. αs acts on adenylyl cyclase (AC) to increase the production of the second messenger cAMP from ATP []. In turn, cAMP activates Epac-1 [] and protein kinase A []. Epac-1 and C3G, a downstream molecule from PKA, are guanine-nucleotide exchange factors for Rap-1 and induce the exchange of GDP to GTP to activate it [,]. Both activate Rap-1, which can modulate the activity of BRaf []. Protein Kinase A has other mechanisms of action as well. It can directly prevent the activation of CRaf through phosphorylation.
