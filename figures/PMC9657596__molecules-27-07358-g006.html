Lactucin, a Bitter Sesquiterpene from Cichorium intybus, Inhibits Cancer Cell Proliferation by Downregulating the MAPK and Central Carbon Metabolism Pathway

Lactucin downregulates Raf-MEK-ERK by inhibiting upstream trans-membrane peptides. Biotinylated Lactucin probes bound to targeted proteins are immobilized to streptavidin agarose through biotin, azide, and a linker molecule (A). Visualizing SDS resolved A549 protein lysate showed biotinylated Lactucin probe binds five peptides (B). Lactucin downregulates A549 lung adenocarcinoma cells Raf-MEK-ERK pathway and Central Carbon Metabolism in Cancer cells (C).
