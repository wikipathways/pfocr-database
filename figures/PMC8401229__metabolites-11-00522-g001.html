N-acetyltaurine and Acetylcarnitine Production for the Mitochondrial Acetyl-CoA Regulation in Skeletal Muscles during Endurance Exercises

The preventive mechanisms for acetyl-CoA accumulation in the mitochondria through the acetylation of taurine and carnitine. An excess accumulation of mitochondrial acetyl-CoA metabolized from fatty acid and acetate would cause the metabolic stagnation of the glycolytic pathway as negative feedback through allosteric inhibition of PDH. To prevent the accumulation of mitochondrial acetyl-CoA, taurine and carnitine play buffering roles on the acetyl moiety of mitochondrial acetyl-CoA. Consequently, ACT and NAT are produced from fatty acids and acetate, respectively, and then excreted to extracellular fluids.
