SFRP2 overexpression in tumor endothelium leads to enhanced tumor angiogenesis via the Wnt/Ca2+signaling pathway

SFRP2 overexpression in tumor endothelium leads to enhanced tumor angiogenesis via the Wnt/Ca2+signaling pathway. SFRP2 is overexpressed on tumor endothelial cells (TEC) in several types of cancer. SFRP2 can directly bind to an FzD5 receptor, activating the Wnt/Ca2+pathway. Eventually, the transcription factor NFATc3 will activate several gene transcription events, leading to enhanced tumor angiogenesis.
