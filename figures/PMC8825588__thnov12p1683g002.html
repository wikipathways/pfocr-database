Tumor-derived extracellular vesicles as messengers of natural products in cancer treatment

The biological function and therapeutic potential of apoptotic bodies. A) Apoptotic bodies are secreted by budding directly through the cell membrane. Apoptosis leads to an increase in the influx of calcium ions (Ca2+), which triggers proteases, and these activated proteases mediate the destruction of the cytoskeleton. ADP-ribosylation factor-6 (ARF-6) initiates the signal cascade, and finally activates the rho-related protein kinase (ROCK) signaling pathway to trigger blistering, and finally release of apoptotic bodies. The initiation of apoptosis also activates apoptotic enzymes to mediate the shedding of apoptotic bodies.
