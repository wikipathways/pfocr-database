MicroRNAs Regulate Cellular ATP Levels by Targeting Mitochondrial Energy Metabolism Genes during C2C12 Myoblast Differentiation

Pearson correlation coefficient analysis identified a number of miRNAs-mRNA correlation interactions. Only those interactions in which both miRNAs and target mRNAs significantly associated with differentiation and ATP level (FDR < 0.1) during C2C12 myotube induction were utilized to model an miRNA-regulatory network (IPA pathway designer tool). White nodes indicate mRNA target; edges indicate regulatory interaction between miRNA and target gene. Solid line label denotes negative regulation with negative correlation interaction; dashed line denotes indirect positive regulation with positive correlation interaction.
