COVID-19 Immunobiology: Lessons Learned, New Questions Arise

Innate immune detection of SARS-CoV-2 results in the production of interferons, cytokines and chemokines (). Innate immune cells detect SARS-CoV-2 via different mechanisms/pathways. (A) Viral RNA activates the MyD88–NF-kB and IRF pathways inducing production of inflammatory cytokines and type I interferons. Additionally, the cGAS–STING and the MDA-5/RIG-I pathways, also sense the cytoplasmic viral RNA or viral replicative intermediates, and lead to the production of type I and III interferons (IFNa/β and IFNλ). In turn, these interferons, acting in an autocrine/paracrine way via the IFNAR1/2 or IFNAR2/IL10R pathways, induce the expression of ISGs. (B) PRRs like TLR4 also recognize PAMPs-DAMPs released by damaged cells and induce the production of inflammatory cytokines through the NF-kB pathway ().
