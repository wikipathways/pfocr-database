Regulation of receptor-mediated endocytosis by CRL pathways

Regulation of receptor-mediated endocytosis by CRL pathways. (A) Upon IFNα stimulation, the type I IFN receptor subunit IFNαR1 is phosphorylated and downstream signaling occurs []. Phosphorylated-IFNαR1 interacts with β-TrCP2, which targets it to an SCF-mediated ubiquitylation pathway []. Ubiquitylated type I IFN receptor undergoes endocytosis and, ultimately, lysosomal degradation. (B) In C. elegans, the GLR-1 subunit of AMPA-type glutamate receptors (AMPARs) undergoes clathrin-mediated endocytosis upon ubiquitylation by a CUL3KEL8pathway []. It is unclear whether upon glutamate stimulation, AMPAR signaling leads to plasma membrane translocation of the CUL3-neddylating enzyme DCNL3 (red question mark). Once at the plasma membrane, DCNL3 mediates neddylation of CUL3 complexes (i.e. CUL3KEL8) [], thus enhancing ubiquitylation of corresponding substrates (i.e. GLR-1).
