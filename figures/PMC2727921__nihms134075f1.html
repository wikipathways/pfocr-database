Genetic polymorphisms, their allele combinations and IFNB treatment response in Irish multiple sclerosis patients

Signaling via the JAK-STAT pathway by IFN-β (IFNAR2, JAK1, TYK2, PRKCA and PIAS1) [,,] causes upregulation (GBP1, PRKR, PNPT1, IL10) [,,,,] or downregulation (DUSP1 and LEP) [,,] of various genes. Also indicated are proinflammatory molecules and their receptors involved in the pathogenesis of multiple sclerosis, which may be indirectly affected by IFN-β treatment and that use JAK1, JAK2 and TYK2. [,]. Other genes highlighted in the analysis include those encoding IL10RB (part of the IL10 receptor complex []) and proteins involved in apoptosis such as CASP3 [,,] and CASP7 []. ISRE: Interferon stimulated response element.
