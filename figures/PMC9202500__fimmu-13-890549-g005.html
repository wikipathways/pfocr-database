Functions of Viroporins in the Viral Life Cycle and Their Regulation of Host Cell Responses

Viroporins regulate apoptosis. Apoptosis can be activated through two major signaling pathways, the death receptor-mediated pathway, and the mitochondrial pathway. Viroporins can induce apoptosis by changing calcium ion concentration, reducing mitochondrial membrane potential, recruiting apoptosis-related factors, and activating endoplasmic reticulum stress. Some viroporins can also inhibit apoptosis. Ub, Ubiquitin. Figure adapted from ().
