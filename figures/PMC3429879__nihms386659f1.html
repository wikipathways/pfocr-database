Therapeutic Mechanisms of Treatment in Cervical and Vaginal Cancer

In step 1, human papillomavirus (HPV)-E6 protein promotes p53 degradation. In doing so, p53 may not be able to bind M2b as it normally does in a protein–protein interaction, freeing M2b (a small subunit β) to associate with M1 (the large subunit α) and form catalytically active ribonucleotide reductase (RNR) (perhaps most often in its α6β2 higher order form). In step 2, HPV-E7 protein aids in retinoblastoma (Rb) protein degradation, activating transcription factors that may elevate RNR subunits. Together, ribonucleotide diphosphate (rNDP) substrates are reduced to 2′-deoxyribonucleoside diphosphate (dNDP) in step 3. Once phosphorylated to a deoxynucleoside triphosphate (dNTP), DNA may be repaired. Enzymes in a complementary salvage pathway can also lead to dNDP production in a fourth pathway. cdN = cytosolic deoxynucleotidase; dCK = deoxycytidine kinase; dGK = deoxyguanine kinase; dN = deoxynucleoside; DNC = deoxynucleotide carrier; dNMP = deoxynucleoside monophosphate; ENT = equilibrative nucleoside transporter; mdN = mitochondrial deoxynucleotidase; mtDNA = mitochondrial DNA; NDP = nucleoside diphosphate; PNP = purine nucleoside phosphorylase; TK = thymidine kinase; TP = thymidine phosphorylase.
