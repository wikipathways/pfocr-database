Proteins that Are Rhythmic in the Pentose-Phosphate Pathway Peak in Anti-phase to Those in Glycolysis

Enzymes and products within the pentose-phosphate pathway are shown along the left side, while enzymes and products within glycolysis are shown along the right side. Bolded and colored arrows and squares denote enzymes considered rhythmic in our analysis (p < 0.05), and the color denotes how many hours away it is from peak abundance or phase. For example, warm colors show that an enzyme is at a relatively high level at CT12, while cold colors show that an enzyme is at a low level near CT12. Inset panels show the eJTK rhythmic protein fit, with colored dots showing the time from peak protein level, by time of day (CT; see ). CT, circadian time.See also and .
