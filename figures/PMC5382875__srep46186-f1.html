Cytosolic co-delivery of miRNA-34a and docetaxel with core-shell nanocarriers via caveolae-mediated pathway for the treatment of metastatic breast cancer

(a) Preparation of the CNCs. (b) Proposed mechanism for the cancer cell killing. The CNCs enter the blood vessel via systemic administration. Then, they accumulate in tumor tissue through the EPR effect, are taken up by cancer cells, obtain cytosolic delivery, and finally induce cell apoptosis via the synergistic anticancer effect of DTX and miRNA-34a.
