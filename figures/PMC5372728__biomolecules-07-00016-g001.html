Lipolytic and lipogenic pathways in white adipose tissue

Overview of the lipolytic and lipogenic pathways in white adipose tissue. Lipolysis is predominantly activated by the β-adrenergic signaling pathway that increased the release of free fatty acids (FFA) and glycerol. Lipogenesis is stimulated by insulin and enhances the uptake of FFA and synthesis and storage of triacylglycerol (TAG). The shaded areas represent adipocytes. Abbreviations: epinephrine (Epi); norepinephrine (NE); cyclic adenosine monophosphate (cAMP); adenosine triphosphate (ATP); phosphodiesterase (PDE); protein kinase A (PKA); hormone sensitive lipase (HSL); phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3K); adipose tissue triglyceride lipase (ATGL); diacylglycerol (DAG); monoacylglycerol (MAG); extracellular signal related kinase (ERK); adenosine monophosphate kinase (AMPK); ATP-citrate lyase (ACL); Acetyl Coenzyme A (CoA) carboxylase (ACC); fatty acid synthase (FASN); fatty acid transport proteins (FATPs); lipoprotein lipase (LPL); mitogen activated protein kinase (MAPK); cluster of differentiation 36 (CD36); glucose transporter-4 (GLUT4).
