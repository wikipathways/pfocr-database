Challenges in Paragangliomas and Pheochromocytomas: from Histology to Molecular Immunohistochemistry

Pseudohypoxia-driven PPGLs. The pseudo-hypoxia cluster of PPGL is traditionally associated to worse clinical outcome, but a closer look at the sub-stratification of this cluster reveals an association between mutations in genes implicated in the regulation of the tricarboxylic acid (TCA) cycle and increased metastatic potential, as opposed to TCA cycle non-aberrant PPGLs within the same cluster. While both subgroups activate the HIF signaling pathway, TCA cycle mutated tumors also exhibit epigenetic dysregulation as a consequence of the accumulation of onco-metabolites (fumarate, succinate, and α-ketoglutarate (α-KG) derivatives. The risk of metastatic events among patients with pseudohypoxia-driven PPGLs is much higher in TCA cycle aberrant cases (40.5% of patients) than in TCA cycle non-aberrant cases (11.2% of patients) []
