Self-Renewal of Macrophages: Tumor-Released Factors and Signaling Pathways

Signaling pathway involved in macrophage self-renewal. In response to CSF-1 and GM-CSF, SIRT1 regulates macrophage self-renewal through c-Myc and E2F-dependent pathways []. Soluble CSF-1R ligands (CSF1 and IL-34) trigger the Ras/MEK1/2/ERK1/2 pathway, which, in turn, activates Ets transcription factors increasing the expression of cyclin-D and c-Myc [,]. In addition, by recruiting Src family kinases (SFK), CSF-1R also induces the MEK5/ERK5 pathway leading to macrophage proliferation []. Indeed, MEK5/ERK5 activation support TAMs proliferation by suppressing p21 expression []. IL-4 can induce macrophage proliferation through STAT6-mediated transcription [] and activates PI3K/AKT signaling, which is important for its pro-proliferative activity []. In response to GM-CSF, the JAK2/STAT5 pathway is triggered inducing macrophage proliferation [,]. Moreover, IL-4 and GM-CSF activate Ets, c-Myc, and KLF4 by inhibiting c-Maf and MafB expression downstream of the Bhlhe40/41 transcription factors [,] and work together with the ADO/A2A pathway to induce macrophage self-renewal via PI3K/Akt and MEK1/2/ERK1/2 signaling []. The molecular mechanisms of other signaling pathways, such as TREM2/DAP12, in macrophage proliferation remain to be documented. PI3K, phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase; A2A, ADORA2A adenosine receptor.
