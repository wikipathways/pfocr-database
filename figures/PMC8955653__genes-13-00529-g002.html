An Expanding Toolkit for Heterochromatin Repair Studies

Main DSB repair mechanisms. DSBs are mainly repaired by HR (mostly in S/G2) or NHEJ. NHEJ involves no or minimal processing of the DSB. HR relies on DSB resection to form ssDNA that ‘invades’ homologous sequences on the sister chromatid or homologous chromosomes to enable DNA synthesis and repair. In mitotic cells, synthesis-dependent strand annealing (SDSA) is the most common HR pathway, although break-induced replication (BIR) also frequently occurs near telomeres. Double-Holliday junction (dHJ) repair is better characterized in meiosis. Extensively resected DSBs can also utilize the Rad51-independent pathway of single strand annealing (SSA) when repeated sequences are available, while alternative-end joining pathways (Alt-EJ), such as MMEJ, involve limited resection to expose micro-homologies.
