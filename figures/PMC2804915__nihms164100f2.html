Biochemical pathway of junction remodeling at the BTB, apical ES and hemidesmosome

A biochemical pathway of junction remodeling at the BTB, apical ES and hemidesmosome. Three cellular events, namely spermiation, BTB restructuring and transition to G2–MI, occur while primary spermatocytes cross the BTB at stages VIII–XI of the seminiferous epithelial cycle. These events require strict coordination and are regulated by a unique biochemical pathway. At spermiation, MMP-2 is activated by cytokines (e.g. TGF-β3 and/or TNFα, denoted by a dark brown hexagon and a dark brown circle, respectively) to cleave the α6β1 integrin–laminin α3β3γ3 adhesion complex (green) at the apical ES. This event is likely to involve the phosphorylation of focal adhesion kinase (FAK; lilac) and the transforming gene of Rous sarcoma virus (c-Src; pink), resulting in the internalization of laminin fragments. Thereafter, biologically active laminin fragments induce BTB restructuring to facilitate transit of preleptotene spermatocytes by activating ERK (orange) and/or p38 MAPK (red). These MAPKs also help to prepare spermatocytes for transition to G2–MI. Alternatively, biologically active laminin fragments can exert their effects at the hemidesmosome by reducing the level of β1 integrin, and this might also involve the participation of FAK and c-Src. Destabilization of the hemidesmosome apparently serves as a feedback loop to further destabilize the BTB and to facilitate its restructuring. Moreover, cytokines produced by Sertoli and/or germ cells and secreted into this microenvironment might also stimulate testosterone (T, denoted by a blue star) production and binding to the androgen receptor (AR; blue). This can also activate ERK and/or p38 MAPK, resulting in BTB restructuring and spermatocyte migration. In short, the events of spermiation, BTB disassembly, hemidesmosome remodeling and cell cycle progression are coordinated by MAPKs.
