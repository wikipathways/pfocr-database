Endothelial Progenitor Cells for the Vascularization of Engineered Tissues

Review of angiogenesis and endothelium specification. (A) Summary of angiogenesis, Reprinted with permission from Macmillan Publishers Ltd: Adams and Alitalo © 2007. (a) New vessels arise from preexisting vessels on exposure to proangiogenic (+) factors VEGF-A and ANG-2 that induce vascular ECs to remove intercellular and PC, secrete proteases that degrade the basement membrane and surrounding ECM, alter polarity, and migrate. The degree of exposure and vicinity to VEGF-A determines tip cell selection. VEGF-A and tip cell VEGFR-2 interactions promote transmembrane Notch ligand DLL4 expression, laterally inhibiting tip cell differentiation of adjacent stalk cells by promoting Notch signaling. (b) Enhanced vascular permeability releases plasma proteins, which form a provisional matrix that interacts with VEGF-A, Semaphorins, and SLIT proteins, creating an angiogenic gradient that directs migrating sprouts. Tip cells secrete PDGF-B to recruit mural cells. Stalk cells maintain parent vessel connectivity through intercellular tight junctions and deposition of basement membrane. (c) Encountering tip cells initiates the end of angiogenesis by reforming intercellular junctional contacts. Lumen formation occurs by re-establishing apicobasal polarity, redistributing junctional EC proteins and recruiting actomyosin. Fusion of intracellular vacuoles also creates lumen through Rho family GTPases Cdc42 and Rac1. (d) Neovessel ECs secrete EGFL7 into the surrounding ECM to assist in tubulogenesis. Stabilization of mural cell contacts promotes vessel maturation by initiating quiescence. TGF-β1 is released by ECs to further mural cell differentiation toward a vascular SMC phenotype. (B) Organotypic endothelium types, modified and reprinted with permission from Macmillan Publishers Ltd.: Hoffman and Calabrese © 2014 and with permission of Springer, Pries and Kuebler Nonfenestrated continuous endothelium possesses tight and adherens junctions that regulate permeability. Wnt signaling, Wnt7a, Wnt7b, Wnt3a genes, β-catenin, and astrocyte SSeCKS control BBB differentiation. Fenestrated endothelium in the EG contains diaphragms regulated through EG-VEGF, VEGF-A, and type II membrane glycoprotein PV-1. Podocyte-secreted VEGF and the Rho/Rac signaling pathway specify the kidney glomeruli. Discontinuous endothelium contains fenestrae 100–200 nm in size and lack a continuous basement membrane. BBB, blood–brain barrier; DLL4, Delta-like 4; ECM, extracellular matrix; EG, endocrine-gland; EGFL7, epidermal growth factor-like domain-7; PC, pericyte contacts; PDGF-B, platelet-derived growth factor subunit B; PV-1, plasmalemmal vesicle-associated protein-1; SSeCKS, Src-suppressed C kinase; TGF-β1, transforming growth factor beta 1.
