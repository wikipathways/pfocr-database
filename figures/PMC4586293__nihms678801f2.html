Neurohormetic Phytochemicals: An Evolutionary - Bioenergetic Perspective

SIRT1 functions as an intracellular energy sensor to detect the concentration of NAD+, and regulates metabolic status by deacetylating various target proteins including transcription factors such as FOXO3 and PGC-1α. Dietary energy restriction, exercise and phytochemicals such as resveratrol, EGCG, icariin, butein and fisetin activate SIRT1. AMPK serves as a detector of cellular energy states that is activated when the cellular AMP+ADP)/ATP ratio rises. AMPK and SIRT1 influence each other; activation of AMPK increases the intracellular level of NAD+, thus stimulating the activity of SIRT1 and, in turn, SIRT1 deacetylates liver kinase B1 (LKB1) which can increase the activity of AMPK. AMPK also directly regulates FOXO3. Energy restriction, exercise and resveratrol activate the AMPK pathway, leading to increased NAD+ and SIRT1 activity. The mTOR pathway up-regulates protein synthesis while inhibiting autophagy. IGF-1 activates receptors coupled to the PI3K – Akt pathway which can activate mTOR. SIRT1 negatively regulates mTOR signaling pathway. AMPK inhibits the activity of mTOR complex either by phosphorylating the regulatory-associated protein of mTORC1 or phosphorylation of TSC1/TSC2. Resveratrol, EGCG and curcumin decrease both the expression and phosphorylation of Akt/PKB and PI3K. Likewise, curcumin, resveratrol and gartanin inhibit phosphorylation of mTOR, S6K1 and 4E-BP1.
