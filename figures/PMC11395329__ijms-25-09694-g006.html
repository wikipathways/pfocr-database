MiR-27a-5p functions in hDPCs

Schematic diagram of miR-27a-5p functions in hDPCs. LPS binding to TLR4 stimulates the NF-κB signaling pathway, which induces the synthesis of proinflammatory mediators (IL-6, IL-8, and MCP1) and miR-27a-5p in hDPCs. In turn, miR-27a-5p downregulates the expression of proinflammatory mediators by suppressing TAB1 and IRAK4, important intermediates of the NF-κB signaling pathway, RELA, a subunit of NF-κB, and FSTL1. LPS: lipopolysaccharide; hDPCs: human dental pulp cells
