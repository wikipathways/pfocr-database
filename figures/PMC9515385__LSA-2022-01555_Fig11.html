Scheme depicting the relevance of the PGD2 pathway in AD and its potential as an AD therapeutic target demonstrated by timapiprant (TIMA), a DP2 antagonist

Tg-AD rats at 11 mo of age exhibit AD pathology. We propose that manipulating PGD2 signaling through, for example, DP2 receptor antagonists such as TIMA, could prevent/mitigate AD pathology by several mechanisms. Further details are presented in the discussion, based on our current results with the Tg-AD rat model treated with TIMA, and studies published by others.
