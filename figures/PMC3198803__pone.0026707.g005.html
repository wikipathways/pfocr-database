CcpA Ensures Optimal Metabolic Fitness of Streptococcus pneumoniae

Glc is oxidized to pyruvate via the Embden-Meyerhof-Parnas pathway (Glycolysis, orange box). Homolactic fermentation reduces pyruvate into lactate, whereas mixed-acid fermentation leads to other products, such as formate, acetate and ethanol (Pyruvate metabolism, yellow box). Gal is converted to G6P by the Leloir pathway (light pink box) and to dihydroxyacetone phosphate or glyceraldehyde 3-phosphate by the tagatose 6-phosphate pathway (light orange box). Pathways for capsule, peptidoglycan and phosphorylcholine biosynthesis are shown in light blue, light purple and light green boxes, respectively. Putative and functional characterized genes encoding depicted metabolic steps are shown in white boxes. Proposed pathways were reconstructed based on genome information (http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/genomes/lproks.cgi), literature and database surveys (KEGG, MetaCyc).
