Mechanism of metformin in intervertebral disc degeneration

Potential mechanism of metformin in intervertebral disc degeneration. In MSCs, metformin promotes formation of MVB or amphipathic bodies, induces phosphorylation of SNAP29 and promotes EV release. In the NP, high doses of metformin reduce AMP by inhibiting mitochondrial complex I; low doses of metformin may bind to PEN2, and both pathways activate AMPK thereby enhancing autophagy. Autophagy reduces the expression of p16INK4a, SA-β-gal, p21WAF1 and p-p53 and inhibits senescence; autophagy also inhibits Bax, Caspase and leads to barrier function. In addition, autophagy reduces the activation of cGAS/STING signaling pathway and phosphorylation of P65, thereby reducing COX2 and the inflammatory mediator PGE2. In AFSCs, metformin inhibits extracellular migration of HMGB1 and decreases expression of inflammatory mediators. Figure adapted from Server Medical Art (https://smart.servier.com/).
