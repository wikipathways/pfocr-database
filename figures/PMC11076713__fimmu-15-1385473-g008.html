Schematic model: The interferon and interleukin 27-dependent JAK-STAT signaling pathway

Schematic model: The interferon and interleukin 27-dependent JAK-STAT signaling pathway. Based on our results, we suggest that MDMs stimulated with type I, II, and III IFNs, or IL-27 (IFN-π)? activate different components of the JAK-STAT signaling pathway. Specifically, STAT1, the primary transcription factor involved in IFN-dependent signal transduction, forms a STAT1/STAT1 homodimer (for IFN-II and IL-27) or complex with STAT2 and IRF9 (for IFN-I and IFN-III). These complexes translocate into the nucleus and induce the coordinated expression of a robust ISG-dependent pro-inflammatory and antiviral programs that “interferes” with viral replication
