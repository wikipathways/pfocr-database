Epigenetic Regulation of the Wnt/Beta-Catenin Signaling Pathway in Cancer

Interplay of the Wnt pathway with other pathways. Summary of the interaction of the Wnt pathway components (depicted in green) with other signaling pathways, including the Hippo pathway (depicted in blue), TGF-β pathway (depicted in magenta), Hedgehog pathway (depicted in purple), and Notch pathway (depicted in red). The Hippo pathway helps in the stabilization of β-catenin by inhibiting YAP’s interaction with the Wnt destruction complex, thereby preventing the recruitment of βTrCP. TGF-β signaling has synergistic effects with Wnt signaling. SMAD7 can help in preventing the GSK-3β mediated β-catenin degradation. The Hedgehog effector Gli1/2 can inhibit Wnt target gene expression by directly binding to the β-catenin/TCF complex. It can also drive the expression of the Wnt inhibitor sFRP1. The Notch signaling transcription factor, NICD, interacts with β-catenin. NICD can promote Wnt signaling by promoting the expression of the Wnt ligands. However, the Wnt inhibitor DKK2 is also a target of NICD. NICD itself is a substrate of GSK-β.
