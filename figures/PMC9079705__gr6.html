Interaction among gut microbes, the intestinal barrier and short chain fatty acids

Main effects of short-chain fatty acids (SCFA) on the immune barrier. SCFA protect the intestinal immune barrier by lowering the levels of tumor necrosis factor α (TNF-α), interferon-γ (IFN-γ) and interleukin (IL)-6, activating the expression of G-protein coupled receptors (GPCR) to participate in the intestinal-mediated inflammatory and immune response, inhibiting the lipopolysaccharide-induced activation of nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB), promoting potassium ion (K+) efflux and hyperpolarization in intestinal epithelium cells (IEC) as well as IL-18 formation, suppressing histone deacetylase (HDAC) and downregulating the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines. Moreover, SCFA could improve the intestinal integrity by inhibiting the Toll-like receptor 4 (TLR4) signalling pathway. Source: ; ; ; ; ; ; ; ; ; .
