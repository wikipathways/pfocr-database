Association of cytoplasmic peptidyl Cys with functional networks according to reduction and oxidation of Cys residues

Association of cytoplasmic peptidyl Cys with functional networks according to reduction and oxidation of Cys residues. Percent reduction values for the 20% most reduced Cys (Panel A) and the 20% most oxidized Cys (Panel B) were analyzed by Ingenuity Pathway Analysis (IPA). In this analysis, symbols with colors represent proteins associated with peptides identifed with percent oxidation data in . Open symbols represent components of the functional network for which data were not available. Asterisks indicate that multiple identifers in the dataset fle map to a single gene/chemical in the global molecular network. In A, the top network associations of the most highly reduced Cys were with cancer, gastrointestinal disease, cellular assembly and organization and protein synthesis (Score 58). A large number of these proteins are associated with the cytoskeleton. In B, the most oxidized peptides had network associations with cancer, cell signaling and post-translational modifcation (Score 51).
