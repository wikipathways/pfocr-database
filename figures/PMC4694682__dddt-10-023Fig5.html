Polyphenols activate Keap1/Nrf2/ARE pathway and increase the expression of detoxification/antioxidant enzymes.Notes: In the cytoplasm, Keap1 protein is always bound to Nrf2 transcription regulator and prevents its signaling. Polyphenols directly or indirectly cause dissociation of Nrf2–Keap1 complex and subsequent nuclear translocation of Nrf2. In the nucleus, Nrf2 binds to the ARE in the regulatory region of the target genes and stimulates transcription of detoxification/antioxidant enzymes HO-1, GCL, GPx, GST, SOD, CAT, PRX, and Trx.Abbreviations: ARE, antioxidant response element; CAT, catalase; EGCG, epigallocatechin–gallate; ERK, extracellular signal-regulated kinase; GCL, γ-glutamylcysteine synthetase; GPx, glutathione peroxidase; GST, glutathione S-transferase; HO-1, heme oxygenase-1; Nrf2, Nuclear factor E2-related factor 2; PKC, protein kinase C; PRX, peroxiredoxin; SOD, superoxide dismutase; Trx, thioredoxin.
