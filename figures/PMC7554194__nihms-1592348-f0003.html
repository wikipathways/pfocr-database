Schwann cells integrate signals from the extracellular matrix (ECM) including mechanical forces, laminins and collagens

ECM stretching or rigidity (green arrow) modulates the hippo signalling pathway, activation of which phosphorylates the transcriptional coactivators YAP and TAZ, causing their retention in the Schwann cell cytoplasm. When dephosphorylated by unknown inhibitors of the hippo pathway, YAP and TAZ migrate to the Schwann cell nucleus where they act to repress Gnas (encoding Gs-α) and to upregulate genes that promote radial sorting (Itga6 encoding α6 integrin and Dag1 encoding dystroglycan) and myelination (Egr2 encoding early growth response 2/KROX20 and Pmp22 encoding peripheral myelin protein 22). Molecular components of the ECM important for Schwann cell development during radial sorting and myelination include collagen XV (receptor unknown), collagen IV which binds GPR126, laminin211 which binds GPR126, dystroglycan, integrins α7β1 and integrins α6β1, and laminin411 which binds integrins α6β1.
