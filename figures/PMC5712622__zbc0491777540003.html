Akt/mTOR pathway represents a major axis of phosphorylation signal transduction in response to phagocytosis

The Akt/mTOR pathway represents a major axis of phosphorylation signal transduction in response to phagocytosis. Shown is a schematic representation of Akt and mTOR signal transduction pathways with proteins undergoing significant phosphorylation level changes under POS stimulation highlighted in pink. Phosphorylation change events were divided into two categories: decreased (Clusters 1 and 2; 14 phosphosites) and increased (Clusters 3, 4, and 0; 40 phosphosites). Phosphorylation events outlined in yellow were not detectable under control conditions.
