Sphingolipids as targets for treatment of fungal infections

Metabolic pathways for (A) mammals and (B) fungi are shown. Glucocerebrosidases can metabolize complex glycolipids, represent by dotted arrow. Sphingolipidbiosynthetic pathway inhibitors are shown in bold italic. These are: Myriocin (SPT inhibitor); Fumonisin B1 (CERS inhibitor); THI (2-acetyl-5-tetrahydroxybutyl imidazole, SPL inhibitor); PDMP (D-threo-1-phenyl-2-decanoylamino-3-morpholino-1-propanol, GCS inhibitor); Aureobasidin A (SM synthesis and IPC synthaseinhibitor), Scyphostatin (inhibitor of neutral sphingomyelinase), Australifungin (fungal CERS inhibitor), BHBM(N′-(3-bromo-2-hydroxybenzylidene)-2-methylbenzohydrazide, GlcCer biosynthesis inhibitor) and D0 (3-bromo-N′-(3-bromo-4-hydroxybenzylidene) benzohydrazide, GlcCer biosynthesis inhibitor). ASA: Arylsulfatase A; CDase: Ceramidase; CERS: Ceramide synthase; CERK: Ceramidekinase; CGT: Ceramide galactosyltransferase; CST: Cerebroside sulfotransferase; DES: Desaturase; GCS: Glucosylceramide synthase; HYD: Sphingolipid hydroxylases; IPCS: Inositol phosphorylceramide synthase; IPT: Inositol phosphoryl transferase; ISC: Inositol sphingolipidphospholipase C; KSR: 3-ketosphinganine reductase; LCS: Lactosylceramide synthase; MIPCS: Mannosylinositol phosphorylceramide synthase; SK: Sphingosine kinase; SPL: Sphingosine-1-phosphate lyase; SMase: Sphingomyelinase; SMS: Sphingomyelin synthase; SMT: Sphingolipid methyltransferase; SPP: Sphingosine-1-phosphatephosphatase; SPT: Serine palmitoyl transferase.
