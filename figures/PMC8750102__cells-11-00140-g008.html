Amino Acid Metabolism in Cancer Drug Resistance

Glutathione metabolism is involved in drug resistance in cancer cells. Glutathione enters the cytosol via plasma membrane glutathione transporters such as SLC13A3 or is synthesized through the glutathione biosynthesis pathway. Glutamine-derived glutamate is the major source of glutathione biosynthesis. Most cysteine is taken up by the circulation, and in its oxidized dimer form, cystine is taken up via SLC7A11. Additionally, cysteine can be synthesized from serine and methionine via the transsulfuration pathway. Reduced glutathione can convert hydrogen peroxide and lipid peroxide to water and alcohol, respectively, protecting cells from oxidative damage.
