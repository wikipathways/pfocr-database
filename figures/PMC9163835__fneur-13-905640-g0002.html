Quercetin Can Improve Spinal Cord Injury by Regulating the mTOR Signaling Pathway

TNF-α binds to its receptor, activates PI3K, and promotes Akt phosphorylation. The Akt activates the mTOR. The HMGB1 binds to the TLR4 to inhibit the activation of the AMPK and trigger MAPK. The inhibition of the AMPK reduces the phosphorylation of TSC1/2, thereby hindering the activation of the mTOR. The MAPK promotes the phosphorylation of JNK, p38, and ERK to activate the mTOR. The mTOR stimulates STAT3 and facilitates the phosphorylation of IKKα/β. The mTOR can suppress the TFEB activation, which in turn may inhibit the IKKα/β phosphorylation. The activation of the NF-κB promotes the release of p65. When p65 is released into the cytoplasm, it translocates to the nucleus and upregulatesthe expression of inflammatory factors.
