Innovative Potential of Statins in Cancer: New Targets for New Therapies

(A) Schematic summary of Mevalonate Pathway (MVP) and chemical structure of HMGCR inhibitors mainly studied in cancer (simvastatin, lovastatin, pravastatin, fluvastatin, pitavastatin, atorvastatin and rosuvastatin); the pharmacophore moiety in red and the ring system moiety in blue). (B) Overview of the main molecular targets of statins in cancer. I Antiproliferative effect: statins inhibit the proliferation of cancer cell increasing ER stress markers GRP78 and ERp44, phosphorylating S6 and 4EBP1 and inhibiting mTOR pathway (Okubo et al., ), and inhibiting HDACs (Lin et al., ; Chou et al., ). Statins involve LKB1-AMPK-p38MAPK-p53 survivin signaling cascade thanks to the phosphorylation and acetylation of p53 in cancer cells (Huang et al., ). II Pro-apoptotic effect: statins enhance apoptosis through inhibition of survivin protein expression (see above) (Huang et al., ), by inhibiting HIF-1α/PPAR-γ/PKM2-mediated glycolysis (Feng et al., ), induce caspase 3/7/8/9 protease, decrease the expression of cyclin D1 and CDK4 Bcl-XL (Okubo et al., ), and block the transcription factors NF-κB (Lee et al., ; Branvall et al., ). III Migration and invasion inhibition: statins induce T-cell suppression and B-cell survival (Branvall et al., ), and enhance the WT p53-dependent autophagy (Chou et al., ). IV Effects in CSC: statins can reduce the expression of Sonic Hedgehog (Shh) and consequently, the migration and invasion of cancer cells (Yin et al., ). Other cancerogenic pathways are reviewed in Iannelli et al. ().
