P2Y receptor-mediated signalling cascades in C6 cells

Overview of P2Y receptor-mediated signalling cascades in C6 cells. Green and red lines represent stimulatory (green arrows) and inhibitory (red squares) actions respectively. Dashed lines are incomplete characterised pathways. P2Y2 receptor stimulation enhances ERK-dependent proliferation through a PLC-dependent pathway while P2Y12 receptor stimulation enhances cell proliferation by RhoA- and PKCζ-dependent activation of ERK (Claes et al. []; Grobben et al. []; Tu et al. []; Van Kolen and Slegers, unpublished data). P2Y12 receptor stimulation also inhibits cAMP-dependent induction of differentiation by reactivation of PKB which requires Src/Pyk2 complex formation and Rap1 activation. Formation of the Src/Pyk2 complex requires Ca2+ and PLD2 which is constitutively active (Claes et al. []; Van Kolen and Slegers []; Van Kolen et al. []). Cyclic AMP-dependent inhibition of PKB and ERK is suggested to depend on inhibition of Rap1 (Wang et al. []). The negative modulation of PI 3-K by the P2Y1 receptor is only displayed in the presence of serum (Czajkowski et al. [])
