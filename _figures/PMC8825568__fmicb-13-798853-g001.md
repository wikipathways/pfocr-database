caption: Bacteria virulence factors that avoid NETs release against bacterial infection.
  Bacteria have evolved to develop different virulence factors to avoid the function
  of NETs, inhibiting different steps in the pathways required for the NETs release.
  B. Pertussis or GAS inhibit the action of ROS production by streptolysin O during
  the NETs pathway, which in the end inhibits the release of the structure. Nucleases
  are the main virulence factor shared among the bacteria which dismantle the NETs
  structure, and in this sense, the bacteria can disseminate and generate the disease.
  GAS, Group A Streptococcus; MPO, myeloperoxidase; NE, neutrophil elastase; SOMV,
  small vesicles from the outer membrane into the environment; PAD4, peptidylarginine
  deiminase 4; Nuc, nuclease; LL37, cathelicidin.
