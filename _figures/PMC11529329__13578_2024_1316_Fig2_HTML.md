caption: RPs interacting with p53 and MDM2. p53 is regulated by MDM2, forming a negative
  feedback loop. RPs can directly bind to p53 mRNA or inhibit p53 degradation by binding
  to MDM2. (1) The increased RPS1 binds to RPL11, thereby attenuating RPL11-MDM2-p53
  signaling. (2) The expression of SNORA18L5 in liver tissues preventing RPL5 and
  RL11 to bind to MDM2. (3) RPL28 may inhibit programmed cell death and lead to sorafenib
  resistance by regulating p53. (4) RPLP2 can activate TLR4 and PI3K/AKT signaling
  pathways and promotes the translocation of HIF-1α into the nucleus, finally promoting
  aerobic glycolysis in HCC cells. (Created in BioRender. Su, Q. (2023) www.BioRender.com/z40r347)
