caption: . Myostatin and insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1) signaling pathways in
  skeletal muscle. Myostatin and other transforming growth factor β family members
  signal via activin receptor II (ActRII), Smad2, and Smad3, which blocks muscle differentiation
  and leads to muscle atrophy. Inhibition of regulatory-associated protein of mammalian
  target of rapamycin (mTOR) and mTOR complex 1 (mTORC1) has an additive effect on
  myostatin signaling. The IGF-1/Akt pathway induces skeletal muscle hypertrophy.
  ALK, activin receptor-like kinase; GDF11, growth differentiation factor 11; IGFR,
