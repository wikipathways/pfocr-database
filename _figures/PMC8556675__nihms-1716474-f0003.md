  dissociation from GPCR activates AC, initiating canonical signaling pathway; GαI
  = G-protein-alpha-I, recruitment inhibits signaling through canonical pathway; ATP
  = adenosine triphosphate; cAMP = cyclic adenosine monophosphate; AMP = adenosine
  monophosphate; AC = adenylate cyclase, enzyme that catalyzes the conversion of ATP
  to cyclic AMP; PKA = protein kinase A; PDE = phosphodiesterase, an enzyme that converts
  cAMP to AMP; CREB = cAMP response element binding protein; CBP/p300 = CREB-binding
  protein, a transcription co-activator; GRK = G-protein coupled receptor kinase;
  β-arrestin = a scaffolding protein that changes the conformation of the GPCR, can
  mediate intracellular signaling independent of the β2-AR; MAPK = mitogen activated
  protein kinase, a family of intracellular signaling proteins; ERK1/2 = extracellular
  signal-regulated kinase, a conventional MAPK signaling pathway (typically: Ras-Raf-MEK-ERK
  chain of proteins); p38 = a conventional MAPK signaling pathway; AP-1/NFκB = Activator
  protein-1/nuclear factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer of activated B cells, pro-inflammatory
