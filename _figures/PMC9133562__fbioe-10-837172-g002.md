caption: 'Bone morphogenic protein (BMP) signaling in vivo: TGF-β superfamily members
  include activin, inhibin, Mullerian inhibitor substance, and bone morphogenetic
  proteins (BMPs). BMP is the largest subfamily, with more than 30 BMP ligands in
  humans. BMPs transduce signals through a polymeric cell surface complex of which
  there are two classes, type I and type Ⅱ receptors. Both type I and type II receptors
  are single-channel transmembrane proteins with an intracellular serine/threonine
  kinase domain. Type II receptors phosphorylate type I receptors after ligand assembly,
  and the activated type I receptor recruit specific SMAD1/5/8 (blue pathway) and
  SMAD2/3 (yellow pathway), which combine with SMAD4 to form trimers which translocate
  to the nucleus. SMADs bind to DNA and recruit chromatin remodeling factors and tissue-specific
  transcription factors to regulate gene expression.'
