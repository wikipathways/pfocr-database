caption: 'Intracellular fatty acid metabolism. A simplified cartoon of fatty acid
  metabolism pathways. Fatty acids are transported in the circulation as triacylglycerol
  (TAG) in lipoproteins and hydrolyzed by lipoprotein lipase (LPL) or they are bound
  to albumin and are transported across the plasma membrane. A CoA is ligated to fatty
  acid (FA), and the fatty acyl-CoA (FA-CoA) can enter the glycerolipid synthesis
  pathway for storage or the mitochondria for oxidation. ACS, acyl-CoA synthase; AGPAT,
  acyl-CoA: acylglycerol-3-phosphate acyltransferase; ATGL, adipose triglyceride lipase;
  DAG, diacylglycerol; DGAT, diacylglycerol acyltransferase; GPAT, glycerol-3-phosphate
  acyltransferase; HSL, hormone-sensitive lipase; LPA, lysophosphatidic acid; MAG,
  monoacylglycerol; MAGL, monoacylglycerol lipase; PA, phosphatidic acid.'
