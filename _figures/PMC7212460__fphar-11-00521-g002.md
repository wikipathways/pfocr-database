caption: Canonical Wnt/β-catenin pathway. (A) In the absence of Wnt signals, β-catenin
  is degraded by the destruction complex, comprised of Axin, adenomatous polyposis
  coli (APC), and glycogen synthase kinase 3 (GSK3). Low cytoplasmic β-catenin levels
  ensure the inactivation of TCF-4, a transcription factor that can promote cancer
  cell proliferation. (B) Overexpression of Wnt ligands and (C) APC gene mutation
  lead to β-catenin accumulation in cancer cells, forming an active complex with transcription
  factors; thus, promoting cancer cell proliferation.
