caption: 'Activation of NMDA receptors (NMDARs) and L-type voltage-sensitive calcium
  channel (Cav1.2) triggers calcium influx and induces calcium-dependent signaling
  molecules such as calcineurin (CaN) and Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent protein kinases
  (CaMKs). Calcium influx also activates members of the cAMP signaling pathway, such
  as protein kinase (PKA) via Ca2+-sensitive adenylate cyclase (ACs). These signaling
  molecules modulate the synapse-to-nucleus shuttling of transcription modulators
  (HDAC4, CRTC1 and NF-κB) via phosphorylation and dephosphorylation. Then, these
  transcriptional modulators contribute to the control of activity-dependent gene
  transcription which is required for synaptic plasticity and memory formation. P:
  phosphorylation; the red arrow marked with “ON” represents gene transcription.'
