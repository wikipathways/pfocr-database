caption: Linear ubiquitination and NF-κB activation. Formation of linear ubiquitin
  chains is implicated in NF-κB activation induced by different pathways, for example
  via TNF or IL-1 receptors, Toll-like receptors or NOD2 receptors. All these pathways
  involve the formation of signaling complexes to which LUBAC is recruited via K63-linked
  ubiquitin. K63-linked polyubiquitin is generated by cIAP1/2 at the TNFR complex
  and by TRAF6 at the IL-1R and at TLRs. LUBAC then assembles M1-linked ubiquitin
  on K63-ubiquitinated NEMO (and other substrates within the pathway). Oligomerization
  of NEMO activates the associated kinases IKKα and IKKβ, required for NF-κB activation.
