caption: Double function of the ACPA-CD147 axis in NLRP3 inflammasome signaling in
  RA macrophages. Anti-citrullinated peptide/protein antibodies (ACPAs) from rheumatoid
  arthritis (RA) patients bind to the CD147-integrinβ1 complex on macrophages, which
  initiates two signals through its signaling pathways. In one pathway, the ACPA-CD147
  axis primes the inflammasome signal through the PI3K-Akt pathway and results in
  high NLRP3 transcription. In the other pathway, the axis activates the inflammasome
  signal through the ATP-K+ efflux pathway and produces activated caspase-1 and IL-1β.
  Blocking the key molecule CD147 may stop these two signals to suppress the inflammasome
