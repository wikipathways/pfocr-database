caption: 'Schematic review of the molecular mechanisms linking diabetes mellitus and
  colorectal cancer. Insulin and insulin-like growth factor (IGF-1) signaling pathways
  are shown, along with their effect on apoptosis, proliferation, and protein synthesis.
  The activated mitogen activated protein kinases (MAPK) pathway is associated with
  proliferation and the phosphatidyl-inositol-3-kinase (PI3K)/protein kinase B (Akt)
  pathway mainly leads to survival (inhibition of apoptosis). Moreover, protein synthesis
  is induced via the Akt pathway. The complexity of the system and the cross-talk
  between the individual pathways are presented. MEK: Mitogen extracellular kinases;
  ERK: Extracellular signal-regulated kinases; mTOR: Mammalian target of rapamycin;
  Ras: “Rapid accelerating sarcoma” serine/threonine-kinase; Raf: “Rapid accelerating
  fibrosarcoma” serine/threonine-kinase; Ryk: Related to tyrosine kinases; SOS: Silencing
  of survival signals; Grb2: Growth factor receptor binding protein 2; APC: Activated
  protein C; GSK-3β: Glycogen synthetase kinase-3β; Axin: Activated extracellular
  index.'
