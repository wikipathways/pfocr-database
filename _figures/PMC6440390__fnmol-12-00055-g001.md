caption: 'Schematic view of the main neuronal Ca2+ players. Many Ca2+ transport proteins
  contribute to Ca2+ handling: the inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate receptor (InsP3R),
  the ryanodine receptor (RyR) at the sarco/endoplasmic reticulum (SR/ER) membranes,
  and the voltage (VOC), the receptor (ROC)-activated and the store-operated (SOC)
  Ca2+ channels of the plasma membrane. Ca2+ extrusion depends on the activity of
  the plasma membrane Ca2+ ATPase (PMCA) and the plasma membrane Na+/Ca2+ exchanger
  (NCX). Ca2+ reuptake in the intracellular stores is operated by the ER/SR Ca2+ ATPase
  (SERCA) and the secretory pathway Ca2+ ATPase (SPCA) of the Golgi apparatus. The
  mitochondrial Ca2+ handling systems and the cytosolic Ca2+-binding/buffering proteins
  are indicated. MCU, mitochondrial Ca2+ uniporter. NCLX, mitochondrial Na+/Ca2+ exchanger.
  VDAC, voltage-dependent anion channels. MPTP, mitochondrial permeability transition
  pore. CaM, calmodulin. CR, calretinin. CB-28K, calbindin D-28K. NCS-1, Neuronal
  Calcium Sensor 1.'
