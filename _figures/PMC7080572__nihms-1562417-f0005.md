caption: Model of striatal signaling during reward and punishment. Rewarding stimuli
  and their predictors elevate dopamine signaling which excites a low-affinity dopamine
  receptor-expressing output circuit, increasing the sensitivity of these projection
  neurons to glutamatergic afferents that promote incentivized responding. Alternatively,
  aversive stimuli promote VTA KOR activation, decreasing naturally-occurring dopamine
  signaling, and disinhibiting a different, aversion-sensitive output circuit characterized
  by the expression of high affinity dopamine receptors. The activation of this pathway
  promotes conditioned avoidance.
