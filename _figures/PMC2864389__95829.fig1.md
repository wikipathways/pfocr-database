caption: The PI3K/AKT signaling pathway. An activated tyrosine kinase receptor (RTK)
  recruits adaptators such as Gab2 or IRS family proteins, which bind to the regulatory
  p85 subunit of PI3K. The latter activates the catalytic p110alpha, beta and delta
  subunits of PI3K. Activated PI3K complex transforms PI(4,5)P2 into PI(3,4,5)P3.
  The latter recruits PDK1 and AKT to the plasma membrane where AKT is phosphorylated
  by PDK1 on Thr308. PDK2, which is mTORC2, phosphorylates AKT on Ser473. Fully activated
  AKT modulates several substrates important for cell survival, cell cycle and cell
  growth.
