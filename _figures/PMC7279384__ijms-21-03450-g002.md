caption: In hypothalamus, leptin exerts an anorexigenic power, binding to its receptor
  (LepR-b) and activating a signaling pathway, which induces the activation of proopiomelanocortin
  (POMC) neurons and the inhibition of releasing agouti-related protein (AgRP). The
  same anorexigenic power is exerted by insulin when it binds to its IRS receptor.
  In contrast, ghrelin exerts orexigenic effects, binding to the growth hormone secretagogue
  receptor (GHSR) and promoting hunger, activating AgRP neurons.
