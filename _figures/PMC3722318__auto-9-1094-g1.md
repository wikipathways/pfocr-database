caption: Figure 1. TFEB gene transfer interrupts the pathogenic vicious cycle of SERPINA1
  deficiency. In livers of PiZ mice, mutant SERPINA1 accumulation activates the NFKB
  pathway that aggravates the burden of intracellular mutant SERPINA1. NFKB-dependent
  increase of IL6 and NFKB activation enhance mutant, human SERPINA1 and mouse SERPINA1
  expression. TFEB gene transfer increases disposal of SERPINA1 polymer in autolysosomes
  by increasing lysosomal biogenesis and autophagy flux. Therefore, TFEB interrupts
  the positive feedback loop that results in increased NFKB activation, IL6, and SERPINA1
  expression.
