caption: Microbiota alteration contributes to CCA carcinogenesis. Increased levels
  of commensal Helicobacter spp. during a parasitic OV infection result in chronic
  inflammation and abnormal cell proliferation of cholangiocytes. (1) Increased levels
  of conjugated bile acids (TUDCA and GUDCA) within the bile duct lumen from the increases
  in Helicobacter spp. lead to cholangiocyte inflammation through the NF-kB pathway.
  Proinflammatory cytokine IL-1, profibrotic cytokine TGF-β, and angiogenetic vascular
  endothelial growth factor are upregulated in cholangiocytes. (2) Helicobacter spp.
  can activate the mitogen-activated protein kinase pathway resulting in cholangiocyte
  proliferation. (3) Helicobacter spp. can dysregulate the cell cycle of cholangiocytes
  by phosphorylation of the RB, a tumor-suppressing protein, and then release transcription
  factor E2F resulting in abnormal cholangiocyte proliferation. E2F, E2 factor; GUDCA,
  glycoursodeoxycholic acid; NF-kB, nuclear factor-kappa B; OV, Opisthorchis viverrini;
  RB, retinoblastoma; TUDCA, tauroursodeoxycholic acid.
