caption: Proposed model for L. donovani-mediated host TLR2 downstream signaling that
  activates cPLA2 and Cox2, culminating in PGE2 generation. TLR2 stimulation by a
  leishmanial protein (possibly LPG) activates signaling via PI3K and PLC. This results
  in an elevation of intracellular calcium levels, which, along with ERK phosphorylation,
  leads to activation of cPLA2. This increase in calcium levels also activates the
  calcium-calcineurin-NFAT pathway and specifically induces NFATc2 binding to the
  −73/−77 NFAT binding site on the cox2 promoter, activating cox2 transcription.
