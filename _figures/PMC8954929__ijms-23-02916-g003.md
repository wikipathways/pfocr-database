  NTRK fuses with the 5′ region of a partner gene, producing a chimeric protein. This
  fusion protein leads to constitutive activation of downstream pathways, resulting
  in ligand independent pathway signaling and subsequently cause oncogenesis. NTRK
  inhibitors are able to treat NTRK fusion malignancy by blocking the tyrosine kinase,
  hampering the signal pathway in both wide type and fusion protein producing tumor
  cells.
