caption: An active form of vitamin D, calcitriol, directly enhances the expression
  of Rspo2 by binding to the vitamin D receptor (VDR). Rspo2 binds to Lgr5 and enhances
  the agrin-LRP4-MuSK signaling pathway to facilitate the clustering of acetylcholine
  receptor (AChR) clusters. Calcitriol markedly increases synaptic vesicles likely
  via Rspo2. Rspo2 also indirectly enhances the expressions of Rapsn and Dups6 to
  facilitate AChR clustering. Solid and broken lines indicate direct and indirect
  effects, respectively
