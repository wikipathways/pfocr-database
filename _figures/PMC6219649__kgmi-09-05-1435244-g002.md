caption: Characterized pathways for Hyp metabolism. (A) Pathway for Stickland fermentation
  using l-proline (Pro) as an electron acceptor. The product of Pro reduction, 5-aminovalerate,
  can be further oxidized and reduced by certain Clostridiales to generate short-chain
  fatty acids and ammonia. (B) HypD provides an anaerobic route for Hyp metabolism.
  (C) In mammals, Hyp is oxidized to glyoxylate and pyruvate in a multistep pathway.
  (D) An oxidative pathway for Hyp metabolism generates α-ketoglutarate (α-KG) and
  ammonia as sources of carbon and nitrogen.
