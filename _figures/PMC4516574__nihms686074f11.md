caption: The hypothetical model illustrating p53 transgene mediated inhibition of
  Type I IFN signaling during VSV infection. (Left) In the absence of a functional
  p53, VSV infection is detected through IRF-3 and NF-kB mediated signaling pathways
  and results in production of high levels of Type I IFNs and subsequent IFN signaling
  that stimulates high levels STAT1 phosphorylation and ISG expression. (Right) VSV-p53
  infection is detected through IRF-3 and NF-kB mediated antiviral signaling pathways,
  however p53 transgene expression inhibits the NF-kB antiviral signaling pathway
  and results in reduced IFN production and reduced STAT1 phosphorylation and ISG
  expression.
