caption: Wnt/β-catenin pathway is closely related to myeloma microenvironment. The
  activation of Wnt signaling in osteoblasts regulates the interaction of multiple
  cell growth factors (Wnt3a, Wnt5a, Wnt10b, Wnt16 and IL-6) with the bone marrow
  microenvironment. DKK protein, SFRPs, sclerosin and Wif1 secreted by MM cells antagonize
  Wnt signal. Identically, other molecules, such as TGF-β, IL-7 and CCL3, also block
  Wnt signaling. LGR4/R-spondings can activate Wnt signal via protecting LGR4-expressing
  MM cells against secreted Wnt antagonists.
