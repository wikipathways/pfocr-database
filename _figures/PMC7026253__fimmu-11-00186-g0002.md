  in the gut of inflammatory bowel disease (IBD). 5-HT released from EC (enterochromaffin)
  cells directly or indirectly acts on immune cells (such as macrophages, dendritic
  cells, and T cells) as well as epithelial cells to produce more proinflammatory
  cytokines to exasperate gut inflammation. Blocking the 5-HT signaling of macrophages
  and dendritic cells reduces the release of proinflammatory cytokines and increases
  IL-10 production.
