caption: Regulation of HCC cells by SIRT4. SIRT4 depletion promotes hepatocellular
  carcinoma (HCC) genesis by regulating the AMPKα-mTOR axis. SIRT4 silencing in tumor-associated
  macrophages (TAMs) promotes HCC development through PPAR signaling-mediated macrophage
  activation. SET8 inhibits sirt4 transcription by regulating H4K20me1 and KLF4, thereby
  enhancing aerobic glycolysis and promoting the growth of HCC cells. SIRT4 deficiency
  promotes GNPAT acetylation, inhibits FASN degradation, promotes lipid synthesis,
  and promotes HCC. SIRT4 promotes the senescence of SMCC7721 cells in HCC by inhibiting
  the JAK2-STAT3 signaling pathway.
