caption: Represents the schematic diagram of pLysRS-Ap4A signaling activation during
  cancer metastasis. During chronic inflammation, the immune cells, such as mast cells
  (MCs), tumor-associated macrophages (TAMs), etc., release an abundance of growth
  factors, cytokines, chemokines, prostaglandins, and reactive oxygen species in the
  tumor microenvironment which can promote tumor metastasis. The released growth factors,
  chemokines and cytokines cause the activation of the pLysRS-Ap4A-regulated MITF
  pathway. Thus, it promotes cancer cell survival, proliferation, migration and metastasis.
  Hence, the pLysRS-Ap4A-regulated MITF pathway is the main hallmark in both cancer
  and immune cells.
