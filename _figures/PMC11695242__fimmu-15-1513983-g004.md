caption: Roles of Periodontopathogen-Derived BEVs in Periodontal Tissue Destruction.
  (A) BEVs cause periodontal tissue destruction by impairing bioactivity of periodontal
  tissue cells such as gingival epithelial cells, human gingival fibroblasts (HGFs)
  and human umbilical vein endothelial cells (HUVECs). BEVs can also disrupt and enter
  the epithelial layer to play toxic roles. (B) BEVs induce bone destruction by releasing
  virulence factors and influencing the function of bone marrow stromal cells (BMSCs)
  and osteoclasts, to promote osteoclastogenesis and inhibit osteogenesis. BEVs can
  also be endocytosed by human periodontal ligament stem cells (hPDLSCs) and activate
  the NLRP3 inflammasome via the NF-κB (p65) signaling pathway. influencing osteogenic
  differentiation and mineralization
