caption: TLR agonists as promising agents against HBV infection. After the agonist
  activates the corresponding TLR signaling pathway, downstream signaling molecules
  are activated, such as PI3K/AKT, MEK1/2, ERK2, JNK, NF-κB or IRF3, promote the antiviral
  cytokine IFN-α/β and the secretion of pro-inflammatory or inflammatory cytokines
  IL-6, IL-8, IL-12, and chemokines, and promote anti-HBV replication. HBsAg and HBxAg
  inhibit the activity of the TLR downstream signaling molecules.
