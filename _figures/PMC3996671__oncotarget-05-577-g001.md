caption: Ras-mediated upregulation (green) or downregulation (red) of autophagy is
  depicted in the scheme. Ras can promote autophagy via the Rac1/MKK7/JNK pathway
  and subsequent upregulation of Atg5/Atg7 [, ]. Ras was also shown to induce autophagy
  through the Raf-1/MEK1/2/ERK pathway which inhibits the binding of Bcl-2/Mcl-1 to
  beclin 1 leading to the formation of the class III PI3K complex [, ], or in a GAIP-mediated
  manner []. Inhibition of autophagy by Ras is mediated by the activation of the class
  I PI3K/Akt/mTOR1 pathway and subsequent inhibition of the ULK1/Atg13/FIP200 complex
  [, ]. Autophagy induced by Ras can, in turn, affect tumor progression by modulating
  cell death, cell proliferation, mitochondrial integrity and sensitivity to matrix
  detachment and metabolic stress.
