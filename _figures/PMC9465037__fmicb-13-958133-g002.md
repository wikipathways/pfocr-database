caption: Cyclic di-adenosine monophosphate regulates the host’s innate immune response.
  c-di-AMP produced by pathogens can be recognized by host cells and activate host
  innate immune responses. It can induce a type I IFN response in a STING-dependent
  manner, trigger the nuclear factor kappa B (NF-κB) pathway by activating ERAdP or
  inhibiting RECON, induce NLRP3-dependent inflammasome response, and activate host
  cell autophagy, promoting the production and secretion of cytokines.
