caption: Roles of 14–3-3 proteins in MAPK, PI3K/AKT, TGF-β/Smad, Wnt/β-Catenin and
  Hippo/YAP signaling pathway. In the MAPK pathway, 14-3-3 regulates the activation
  of ERK1/2, JNK, and p38, influencing the transcriptional activity of c-JUN and AP-1,
  and modulating cell viability. In the PI3K/AKT pathway, 14-3-3 interacts with p85,
  AKT, and FOXO, affecting the activity of transcription factors like FOXO and regulating
  cell cycle and proliferation. In the TGF-β/Smad pathway, 14-3-3 interacts with Smad
  and PDK1, among others, influencing downstream transcription expression and participating
  in functional regulation. Within the Wnt/β-Catenin pathway, 14-3-3 interacts with
  β-Catenin, Dvl, and GSK-3β, thereby impacting downstream transcription expression
  and contributing to the regulation of cell apoptosis. Lastly, in the Hippo/YAP pathway,
  14-3-3 interacts with phosphorylated Yap/TAZ or YAP1, influencing downstream transcription
  expression and participating in the regulation of cell proliferation. Figure created
