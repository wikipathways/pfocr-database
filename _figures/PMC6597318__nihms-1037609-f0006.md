caption: Prolonged alcohol exposure (1 h) to isolated axonemes results in the transient
  production of oxidants, which drive desensitization of protein kinase A (PKA) to
  cAMP, preventing stimulation of dynein. The oxidant-generating (OGS) system (hypoxanthine
  + xanthine oxidase; XO) produces H2O2, which mimics the effect of prolonged alcohol
  exposure to blunt cAMP-dependent stimulation of CBF. The antioxidant thioredoxin
  (Trx) reverses both alcohol-mediated and OGS-mediated blunting of cAMP-dependent
  CBF stimulation.
