caption: Hypothetical scenario how conformation of fibronectin (FN) fibrils affect
  integrin signalling. (A) The α5β1 integrin can recognise its binding motif in a
  highly coiled FN fibril, which activates a GTPase signalling pathway that controls
  ECM formation and phagocytosis. (B) If the fibril is stretched, the α5β1 integrin
  binding motif in the fibril is conformationally altered preventing the α5β1 integrin
  from binding FN. The stretching of the fibril, however, reveals a binding motif
  for the αvβ3 integrin. Activation of the αvβ3 integrin alters the GTPase signalling
  pathway that controls ECM formation and phagocytosis
