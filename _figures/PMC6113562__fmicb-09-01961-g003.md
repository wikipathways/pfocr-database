caption: Overview of the regulation of inflammatory arachidonic acid (AA) derivatives
  after Leishmania infantum or Leishmania donovani infections. Visceral leishmaniasis
  causative parasites (L. infantum and L. donovani) induce the production of AA derivatives
  with opposite roles, depending of the Leishmania species. L. infantum infection
  (right panel) induce the release of TNF-α, IL-12, MCP-1 and PGE2 from macrophages.
  PGE2, in turn, induce the production of NO•, enhancing parasite killing. In contrast,
  L. donovani (left panel) increase the production of PGE2 by magrophages, through
  the activation of the PI3K pathway and the NFAT2 transcription factor. This augmented
  PGE2 activates the EP2 receptor in macrophages, inducing the release of regulatory
  cytokines such as TGF-β and IL-10, thus impairing the immune response against the
  parasite.
