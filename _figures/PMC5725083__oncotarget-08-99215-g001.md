caption: Non-leukemic truncation mutations in CSF3R constitutively activate both STAT3
  and STAT5 in the absence of ligand. In contrast, membrane proximal mutations fail
  to activate STAT3, but fully activate STAT5, while compound mutations show full
  activation of both STAT3 and STAT5. Interestingly, both membrane proximal and compound
  mutations of CSF3R constitutively activate MAPK pathway by inducing the adaptor
  protein KSR1. In contrast, truncation mutations do not overexpress KSR1, therefore
  fail to activate MEK and ERK in the absence of ligand. Thus, CSF3R induced leukemia
  is dependent on enhanced MEK/ERK signaling.
