caption: A. Domains of IGF-1. The N-terminus includes a signal peptide directing secretion.
  This is followed by the mature IGF-1 peptide, and a C-terminal extension celled
  the E-peptide. In the Igfla isoform, the E-peptide can undergo N-glycosylation.
  B. Signaling cascade of IGF-1. IGF-1 is normally sequestered by IGFBPs in the ECM.
  Upon release, it can bind to and activate the IGF-IR, which is a tyrosine kinase.
  The two arms of the pathways include the ras/raf/MEK/ERK pathway that can amplify
  satellite cell proliferation, and the Pl3K/Akt/mTOR pathway that increases protein
  translation. Figure was generated in BioRender.
