caption: 'The Wnt signaling pathway regulated by SFRPs. In the inactive state of Wnt,
  SFRPs sequestered Wnt proteins via either CRD or NTR domain and prevent them from
  binding to the frizzled receptor. SFRPs can also bind to Frizzled receptor via CRD
  domain to block Wnt signal transduction (left). In the active state of Wnt, SFRPs
  form inactive complexes by their own and Wnt proteins directly bind to Frizzled
  receptor; or SFRPs form complex with Wnts and interact with Frizzled receptor to
  promote Wnt signal transduction (right). The Wnt proteins then activate at least
  three different downstream signaling pathways: the canonical Wnt-β-catenin, the
  non-canonical planar cell polarity (PCP) and the Wnt-Ca2+ pathways.'
