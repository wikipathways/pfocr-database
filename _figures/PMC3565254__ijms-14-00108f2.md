caption: Pathways involved in endocrine resistance. (a) While tamoxifen (T), aromatase
  inhibitors (AIs), or fulvestrant (F) inhibit estrogen (E) signalization, GFR pathways
  promote ER phosphorylation, transcription factors (TFs), and their coactivators
  (CoA) in a ligand-independent manner. E-ER complex outside the nucleus can interact
  with GFRs, Src, CoA and matrix metalloproteinases that release heparin-binding-EGF;
  (b) Stress may trigger signalization leading to ER and its coregulators phosphorylation;
  (c) Notch regulates the migration and invasion of breast cancer cells. E inhibits
  this pathway while T activates it; (d) High levels of CoA, low levels of corepressors
  (CoR) and altered expression of miRs (e) have been implicated in endocrine resistance
  development.
