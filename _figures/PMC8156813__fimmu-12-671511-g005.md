caption: 'Schematic summarizing the signaling pathways modulated by Sildenafil to
  improve EAE pathology. Sildenafil increases the levels of p-AMPK and reduces the
  levels of mTOR, which leads to an increase in the levels of LC3, promoting autophagy.
  Furthermore, Sildenafil enhances the expression of p-eNOS, leading to the production
  of NO, which further activates AMPK. Moreover, NO triggers the activation of the
  NO-sGC-cGMP-PKG pathway, which leads to increased levels of p-CREB and BDNF, further
  promoting autophagy. Furthermore, Sildenafil inhibits iNOS, which when activated
  produces NO in excess, thus inhibiting nitrosative stress. Altogether, the activation
  of the aforementioned signaling pathways improves EAE pathology. Red bars: inhibition;
  Green arrows: activation.'
