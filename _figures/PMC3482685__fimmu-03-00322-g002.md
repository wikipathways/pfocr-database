caption: Regulatory function of TIR8/SIGIRR in signaling. TIR8/SIGIRR is a receptor
  composed by a single extracellular Ig domain (amino acids 17–112), a transmembrane
  domain (amino acids 117–139); an intracellular conserved TIR domain (amino acids
  166–305), and a 95 amino acid-long intracellular tail. Two conserved amino acid
  (Ser, Tyr in TIR domain) necessary for IL-1R-signaling are replaced in TIR8/SIGIRR
  (Cys 222, Leu 305) potentially leading to a non-conventional activation. TIR8/SIGIRR
  is an orphan receptor, but IL-36Ra has been proposed as a TIR8/SIGIRR ligand in
  glial cells. TIR8/SIGIRR inhibits ILR (IL-1RI, IL-18R, T1/ST2) and TLR (TLR2, TLR3,
  TLR4, TLR5, TLR7/8, TLR9, and possibly others) signaling and NF-κB activation. In
  T cells and epithelial cells, TIR8/SIGIRR inhibits IL-1-dependent activation of
  the Akt-mTOR pathway and of JNK.
