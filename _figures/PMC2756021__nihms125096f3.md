caption: Insertion of the sublytic C5b-9 into the cell membrane leads to G-protein
  activation, followed by the activation of PI3K and Akt. Activation of PI3K induces
  up-regulation of FLIP and inhibition of caspase-8 processing, thereby inhibiting
  Fas-induced apoptosis in OLGs. Inhibition of caspase-8 processing is also responsible
  for the inhibition of Bid cleavage. Akt is able to induce the phosphorylation of
  Bad and Bim and protects the mitochondria from pore formation by Bax/Bak and cytochrome
  c release. All these signaling pathways result in the inhibition of apoptosis and
  OLGs survival.
