caption: 'Mitochondrial physiology and mitochondrial Ca2+ handling. Ca2+ enters in
  the mitochondrial matrix via a low affinity uniporter (MCU) and through a high electronegative
  potential (− 180 mV). Extrusion of Ca2+ takes two major routes, one driven by the
  Na+/Ca2+ exchanger, and another pathway which involves H+–Ca2+ exchanger. In the
  matrix, Ca2+ stimulates the activity of three Ca2+-sensitive dehydrogenases of the
  Krebs cycle. The gradient across the inner membrane is important for the mitochondrial
  state and is established by the activity of the mitochondrial respiratory chain.
  VDAC: voltage-dependent anion channel, ANT: adenosine nucleoside transporter, HK:
  hexokinase, CD: cyclophilin D, CK: creatine kinase, BR: benzodiazepine receptor,
  PTP: permeability transition pore.'
