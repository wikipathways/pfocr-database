caption: Schematic representation of the molecular mechanisms involved in PAC1R mediated
  anti-apoptotic effect. The stimulation of PAC1R activates protein kinase A (PKA)
  cascade signaling, leading to extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK) phosphorylation.
  Activated ERK induces the transcription of neuroprotective genes such as c-fos and
  Bcl-2, which finally inhibits the mitochondrial apoptotic pathway and blocks pro-caspase-3
  activation. The inhibitory effect of the PACAP-PAC1R system on pro-caspase-3 cleavage
  can be induced via additional phosoholipase C (PLC)/protein kinase C (PKC) pathway.
  Moreover, PAC1R can also activate phosphatidylinositol 3 kinase (PI3K)/Akt system
  and promote neuronal survival to counteract the apoptotic process.
