caption: Smad3 signaling and crosstalk pathways in renal fibrosis. After binding to
  TβRII, TGF-β1 activates the TβRI-kinase which phosphorylates Smad3. The phosphorylated
  Smad3 translocates into the nucleus and regulates the target gene transcription.
  Smad7 is an inhibitory Smad that functions to block Smad3 activation by degrading
  the TβRI and preventing phosphorylation of Smad3.Ang II, AGEs and CRP can activate
  TGF-β1-independent signaling via the ERK/p38/ MAPK crosstalk pathway. Red arrows/
  symbols represent pathogenic or positive regulation pathway, while blue lines/ symbols
  indicate protective or negative regulation pathways in fibrosis.
