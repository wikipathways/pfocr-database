caption: Signaling pathways regulating mating of S. pombe. Similar to S. cerevisiae
  diploid pseudohyphal growth, mating in S. pombe is regulated by two parallel signaling
  pathways. One responds to pheromones and involves a conserved MAP kinase (MAPK)
  signaling pathway. The second is a nutrient-sensing pathway that, in the presence
  of abundant nutrients, stimulates cAMP production to repress ste11 expression and
  inhibit mating. CAP, cyclase-associated protein.
