caption: Hypertonic stress signaling via PGE2. The diagram depicts the signaling pathway
  proposed by Kuper et al. for PGE2-mediated inactivation of the pro-apoptotic protein,
  BAD. Hypertonicity increases cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) activity which catalyzes the
  production of unstable prostaglandin H2 (PGH2) from arachidonic acid. PGH2 is rapidly
  converted to prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) by PGE synthase (PGES). PGE2 binds to its receptor,
  EP2 which is coupled to Gsα. Gsα activates adenylyl cyclase which produces cAMP,
  leading to activation of protein kinase A (PKA). PKA then phosphorylates and inactivates
  BAD.
