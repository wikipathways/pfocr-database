caption: Different scenarios of IL-38 signaling pathways. (a) Binding of IL-1 pro-cytokines
  (IL-1α and IL-1β) to IL-1R1 employs IL-1RAcP and recruits MyD88 adapter protein.
  Then, the secretion of IRAKs to switch the pro-inflammatory cascade activates both
  NF-κB and MAPK pathways. Major IL-38 can block these pathways after interaction
  with IL-1R1. (b) Interaction of IL-36 pro-cytokines (IL-36α, IL-36β, and IL-36γ)
  with IL-36R activates the pathway like IL-1R1. These responses can be precluded
  by major IL-38 since it collocated with IL-36R. (c) Both major and truncated forms
  of IL-38 can bind to IL-1RAPL1 and consequently prohibit the JNK/AP1 pathway. TIR,
  Toll-Interleukin 1 receptor; MyD88, myeloid differentiation primary response 88;
  IRAKs, interleukin-1 receptor-associated kinases; NF-κB, nuclear factor “kappa-light-chain-enhancer”
  of activated B-cells; AP-1, activator protein 1. IKK, inhibitory kappa B kinases;
  MAPK, mitogen-activated protein kinases; JNK, Jun N-terminal kinase
