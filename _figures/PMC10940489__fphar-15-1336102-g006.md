caption: IL-6 causes myocardial fibrosis through the JAK/STAT3 signaling pathway.
  IL-6 binds to its receptor, IL-6R, forming a complex that activates the gp130 receptor.
  This activation triggers the JAK family of tyrosine kinases. Once activated, these
  JAKs phosphorylate STAT3, a crucial step in the signaling pathway. Phosphorylated
  STAT3 dimerizes and translocates into the nucleus. There, STAT3 dimers bind to specific
  DNA sequences, promoting the transcription of genes that are pivotal in mediating
  inflammation and fibrosis. (By Figdraw)
