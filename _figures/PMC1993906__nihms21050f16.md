caption: (A) Adenylyl cyclases are targets of multiple regulatory signaling pathways
  and respond differently, depending on which group they belong to. The figure shows
  these differences as summarized in 1994 (adapted from Taussig et al., 1994 []. (B)
  The β-type phosphoinositide-specific phospholipases Cβ are targets of three different
  signaling pathways (for G protein triggered signals see text; for signals impinging
  on the Rac-Cdc42 GTPases see Cerione (2004 []) and references therein), the Gqα
  family of α subunits and Gβγ dimers. (C) Example of cross talk from the Gq (Gqα),
  Gi (Gβγ) and Cdc42-Rac signalling pathways to the Gs signaling pathway may generate
  increases of decreases in cAMP levels depending on the subset of adenylyl cyclases
  expressed in the target cell.
