caption: Physiological regulation and pharmacological inhibition of the STAT3 signalling
  pathway. STAT3 is a key player in cell signalling and transcription in response
  to a wide range of stimuli and in various ways. The most relevant STAT3 signalling
  pathways in vascular diseases are indicated with continuous lines; less well-investigated
  STAT3 signalling pathways are indicated with broken lines. GTP, guanosine triphosphate;
  JAK, janus kinase; JNK, c-Jun N-terminal kinase; MAPK, mitogen-activated protein
  kinase; miRNA, microRNA; NFκB, nuclear factor ‘kappa-light-chain-enhancer’ of activated
  B-cells; P, phosphate residue; PIAS3, protein inhibitor of activated STAT; PLC-γ2,
  phospholipase C, γ2 isoform; PTP, protein tyrosine phosphatase; SOCS3, suppressor
  of cytokine signalling; Syk, spleen tyrosine kinase; TF, transcription factor.
