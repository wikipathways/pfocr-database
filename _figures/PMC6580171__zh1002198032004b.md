caption: Pathway analysis of human intermuscular adipose tissue (IMAT) revealed a
  downregulation of key pathways associated with insulin resistance. Green indicates
  a positive correlation whereas red indicates a negative correlation between the
  patient’s gene expression and insulin sensitivity. Key genes like the insulin receptor
  (INSR), the serine-threonine protein kinase (AKT), and the glucose transporter GLUT4
  (SLC2A4) were positively associated with insulin sensitivity, and therefore were
  downregulated in patients with low insulin sensitivity (A). The MAPK (B) and JAK/STAT
  (C) signaling pathways were inversely related to insulin sensitivity. Oxidative
  phosphorylation (D) was associated with insulin sensitivity, and therefore is consistent
  with mitochondrial dysfunction in insulin-resistant individuals. [KEGG pathways
