caption: Hypothetical model of cAMP-PKA signaling during prey sensing and trap development
  in A. oligospora. Nematode-derived signals activate unidentified GPCRs of A. oligospora,
  leading to dissociation of the heterotrimeric G-protein complex and release of the
  Gα Gpa2. Gpa2 then activates the downstream adenylate cyclase Cyr1, leading to an
  increase in cAMP levels. cAMP binds to regulatory Bcy1 subunits of PKA to activate
  its Tpk2 catalytic subunits that phosphorylate the downstream substrates required
  for trap morphogenesis.
