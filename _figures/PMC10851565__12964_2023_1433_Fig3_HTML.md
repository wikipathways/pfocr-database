caption: Autophagy’s dual-edge effect on NF-KB and PI resistance. In a physiological
  context, autophagy can impede the progression of malignancies by eliminating accumulated
  oncoproteins and inducing autophagic cell death. NF-KB, on the other hand, is primarily
  activated through canonical and non-canonical pathways, promoting cell survival
  during cellular stress conditions. PIs have the ability to hinder both of these
  NF-KB activating pathways, leading to apoptosis induction. However, various mechanisms
  that stimulate autophagy can give rise to resistance against PIs and cell death
  by degrading IKK-B and activating NF-KB
