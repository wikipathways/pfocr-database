caption: Activation of the Dlx5 gene triggers T-cell lymphoma. Expression of a constitutively
  activated form of Akt2 (MyrAkt2) in immature mouse thymocytes induces T-cell lymphoma
  via activation of Dlx5 gene expression, due to a recurrent chromosome rearrangement
  with Tcrb. Constitutive activation of the Akt pathway promotes survival in cells
  that undergo aberrant VDJ rearrangement and would otherwise undergo apoptosis, whereas
  the acquisition of T-cell-specific overexpression of Dlx5 gene provides a proliferative
  advantage that, together, result in lymphomagenesis.
