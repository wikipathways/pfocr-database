caption: Model of ion transport regulation by LXA4 and RvD1 enabling the restoration
  of ASL height in CF human bronchial epithelial cells. LXA4 and RvD1 induce a calcium-dependent
  airway surface liquid (ASL) layer height increase in human CF bronchial epithelial
  cell lines and primary cultures (; ; ; ; ). This effect involves binding to the
  FPR2 receptor, stimulation of an ATP secretion into the airway lumen via pannexin
  1 channels (Panx1), and subsequent activation of P2Yx purino-receptors, including
  P2Y11 which leads to a rise in both intracellular cAMP and calcium mobilization
  by stimulation of adenylate cyclase (AC) activity and inositol 3 phosphate (IP3)
  signaling pathway. Intracellular calcium and cAMP increases induce stimulation of
  calcium-activated chloride channel and inhibition of ENaC channel inhibition.
