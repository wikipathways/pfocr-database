  infection. Wnt3 plays a protective role in the M. tuberculosis infection by preventing
  bacterial dissemination. On one hand, Wnt3 promotes apoptosis in a caspase-dependent
  manner, while on the other hand inhibits necrosis by promoting glutathione synthase
  expression (GS) and thus increasing the levels of glutathione (GSH) that in turn
  results in reduced reactive oxygen species and by inhibiting the PARP/AIF pathway.
