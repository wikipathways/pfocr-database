caption: Antibactericidal mechanisms of C5a-TLR2 cross-talk induced by P. gingivalis
  in neutrophils. The C5aR-TLR2 cross-talk activated by P. gingivalis pili induced
  degradation of MyD88 in neutrophils. Without MyD88, the coassociation of C5aR-TLR2
  promoted P. gingivalis infection through activation of the TIRAP-dependent PI3K
  signaling pathway, not only producing the inflammatory cytokine TNF-α but also blocking
  RhoA activation and actin polymerization to inhibit the maturation of phagosomes,
  thus blocking P. gingivalis phagocytosis.
