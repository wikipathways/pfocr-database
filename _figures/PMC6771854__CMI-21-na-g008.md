caption: The neurochemicals TAs, DOP, and SER produced by SadA‐expressing staphylococci
  enhance bacterial internalisation into host cells. SadA‐expressing staphylococci
  convert aromatic amino acids (AAAs), L‐DOPA, and 5HTP into corresponding trace amines
  (TAs), dopamine (DOP), and serotonin (SER), respectively. TAs and DOP interact with
  α2‐adrenergic receptor (α2‐AR) while SER with 5HT receptors. The signaling of TAs
  and DOP via α2‐AR leads to a decrease of cytoplasmic cAMP level and actin polymerisation.
  SER signals via interaction with 5HT receptors leading also to actin polymerisation,
  however, without affecting the cytoplasmic cAMP level. In the presence of SadA or
  neurochemicals internalisation into host cell is boosted about two to three fold
