caption: TLR and NF-κB signaling disrupted by PID. The canonical NF-κB pathway is
  utilized by numerous immunologic receptors, including the IL-R family, TLRs, and
  TACI. Signal transduction is mediated through an IKK complex that results in phosphorylation
  and degradation of IκBα, allowing for nuclear localization of NF-κB. TLR3 signals
  through TRIF to both activate the canonical NF-κB pathway and induce type I interferon
  production through IRF3. Genes in which mutations cause known PID are in bold type.
