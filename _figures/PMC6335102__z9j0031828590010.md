caption: Renal ion regulation in response to ANG II. In the thick ascending limb (TAL),
  ANG II-induced Ca2+ elevation via a c-Src or PLC/PKC/ERK pathway leads to NKCC2
  activity which can be inhibited through NO-dependent cGMP elevation. ANG II-dependent
  IL-1R induction in intra-myeloid cells inhbitis NO secretion. Furthermore, ANG II-induced
  PDE5 converts cGMP to GMP. In the PCT, AT1R induced PLC and resultant Ca2+ elevation.
  Ca2+ can induce NHE3 through a JAK2/CaM pathway or a CaMKII/NHERF1/IRBIT pathway
  leading to NHE3 trafficking to the cell membrane. DCT ANG II signaling leads to
  WNK-4/SPAK-mediate NCC activation. Furthermore, AT1R stimulated PKC induces Nox
  and resultant ROS leading to ENaC activation.
