caption: Schematic model of the signalling pathway mediating cardioprotection against
  myocardial ischemia/reperfusion injury by ethanol consumption. Regular ethanol exposure
  increases extracellular adenosine through the inhibition of adenosine reuptake.
  This initiates intracellular signalling pathways that ultimately protect against
  ischemia/reperfusion injury (see Discussion for more detail). Inhibition of A1 with
  the adenosine A1 receptor antagonist 8-cyclopentyl-1,3-dipropylxanthine (DPCPX),
  phospholipase C (PLC) with U-73122 and protein kinase C (PKC) with chelerythrine
  prevents ethanol-dependent cardioprotection. (−) Inhibition; (+) Activation; DAG
  Diacylglycerol; Gi Inhibitory G protein; IP3 Inositol 1,4,5-triphosphate; Pi Inorganic
  phosphate; PIP2 Phosphatidylinositol 4,5-bisphosphate
