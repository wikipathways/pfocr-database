caption: Schematic model of the KEAP1-NRF2 signalling pathway under basal conditions
  (a), OS (b), and after the recovery of cellular redox homeostasis (c). (a) Under
  basal conditions NRF2 is sequestered by the KEAP1-NEDD8-CUL3-RBX1 complex in the
  cytoplasm, transferring ubiquitin (Ub) proteins from E2 ligase to the Neh2 domain
  in NRF2. In addition, the UBX7-p97-UFD1/NPL4 complex interacts with ubiquitinated
  NRF2 and NEDD8-CUL3 complex and transfers NRF2 to 26S proteasome for its degradation.
  (b) OS causes the oxidation of cysteine residues in KEAP1, inducing a conformational
  change in its structure and preventing NRF2 ubiquitination. NRF2 is therefore stabilized
  and translocates to the nucleus, where it binds to sMaf proteins activating ARE-driven
  genes. (c) Upon the recovery of redox homeostasis, KEAP1 translocates into the nucleus
  and induces NRF2 nuclear export. In the cytosol, NRF2 is ubiquitinated and degraded,
  and its level returns to be physiologically low
