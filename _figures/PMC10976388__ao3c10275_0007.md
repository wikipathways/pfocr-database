caption: |-
  In healthy noncancerous
  cells,
  a destruction complex comprised of APC, Axin, GSK-3β, and CK1α
  degrades β-catenin in the cytoplasm via ubiquitin-mediated proteolysis.
  This prevents β-catenin movement to the nucleus, therefore leaving
  downstream Wnt genes off. In TNBC cells, extracellular Wnt ligands
  bind to overexpressed Frizzled7 (FZD7) receptors, which recruits disheveled
  to the receptor to inhibit the destruction complex. This allows β-catenin
  to accumulate in the cytoplasm before translocating to the nucleus
  to activate the transcription of Wnt target genes that promote oncogenesis.
