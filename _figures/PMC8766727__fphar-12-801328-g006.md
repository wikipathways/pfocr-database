caption: USP14 is involved in several canonical signaling pathway. USP14 deubiquitinates
  NLRC5, then inhibits TNFα release and NF-κB signaling activation. USP14 deubiquitinates
  K63-linked RIG-I and decreased the RIG-I-induced activation of NF-κB signaling.
  USP14 deubiquitinates K48-linked I-κBα and promote the NF-κB signaling. USP14 deubiquitinates
  and stabilize β-catenin and then activated the Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway.
  In contrast, inhibition or knockdown of USP14 attenuated the Wnt/β-catenin signaling.
  Treatment of USP14 with B-AP15 suppressed the Notch1 signaling and also inhibited
  the phosphorylation of Akt and Erk1/2.
