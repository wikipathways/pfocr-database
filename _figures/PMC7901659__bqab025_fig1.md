caption: Pathway synthesis of prostaglandins (PGs), their receptors, and downstream
  secondary messengers. Phospholipase A2 cleaves fatty acids, releasing arachidonic
  acid. PTGS1 (COX-1) and PTGS2 (COX-2) then subsequently convert arachidonic acid
  to PGH2. Lipocalin type PGDS (L-PGDS) and hematopoietic PGDS (H-PGDS) are responsible
  for conversion of PGH2 to PGD2, PGES for PGE2, PGFS for PGF2α, PGIS to PGI2, and
  TXAS (thromboxane A synthase) to TXA2. PGD2, PGE2, PGF2α, PGI2, and TXA2 exert their
  biological function through binding to PGD2 receptor 1 and 2 (DP1 and DP2), PGE2
  receptors (EP1-4), PGF receptor (FP), PGI2 receptor (IP), and TXA2 receptor (TR),
  respectively.
