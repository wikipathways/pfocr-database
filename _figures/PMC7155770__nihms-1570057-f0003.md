caption: Diverse cellular stresses such as DNA damage, ER stress, and metabolic stress
  activate the intrinsic pathway leading to mitochondrial outer membrane permeabilization
  (MOMP). MOMP allows for the release of cytochrome c, second mitochondria-derived
  activator of caspases/ direct IAP binding protein with low pI (Smac/DIABLO), and
  Omi/ high temperature requirement protein A2 (HtrA2) from the mitochondrial intermembrane
  space. Cytochrome c binds to the WD repeats (red rectangle) of apoptotic peptidase
  activating factor 1 (APAF1), inducing a conformational change that opens up the
  molecule. This allows multiple molecules of APAF1 to oligomerize and assemble into
  the APAF1 apoptosome. The apoptosome recruits and activates caspase-9, which then
  activates caspase-3 and caspase-7 leading to apoptosis. Smac/DIABLO and Omi/HtrA2
  bind to XIAP to prevent it from inhibiting active caspases.
