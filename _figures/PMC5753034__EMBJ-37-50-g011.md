caption: ETO/CTH‐induced DNA damage appears to promote a p53/p21‐dependent pathway.
  This is associated with cell cycle arrest, evidenced by decrease in MCM2 expression.
  Furthermore, CDK1—responsible for SAMHD1 phosphorylation—is downregulated. As a
  result, SAMHD1 is not phosphorylated and becomes active against HIV‐1 infection.
  Dephosphorylated, active SAMHD1 induced by DNA damage appears to block 2LTR and
  provirus formation.
