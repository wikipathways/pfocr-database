caption: Schematic showing the main organs that express kisspeptin (magenta) and/or
  its receptor (blue) in mammals (left panel) and sea cucumbers (right panel). The
  binding of kisspeptin (Kiss) to its receptor (KissR) triggers an intracellular pathway
  which sequentially activates the signaling molecules Ca2+/Gq/PLC/PKC/MAPK. This
  stimulates a molecule called ERK1/2 which regulates a number of cellular processes,
  including gene expression (center panel). Wang et al. showed that the signaling
  cascade triggered by kisspeptin regulates similar biological processes in mammals
  and sea cucumbers, such as reproduction, glucose metabolism and body mass.
