caption: Model of TNFSF15 regulation of antimicrobial pathways. Upon PRR stimulation
  of macrophages, TACE is activated and leads to the release of soluble TNFSF15. Soluble
  TNFSF15 then feeds back to interact with DR3 on macrophages to initiate TRAF2/RIP1/RIP3
  and FADD/MALT1/caspase-8 signaling pathways. TRAF2/RIP1/RIP3 pathway activation
  leads to PDK1-dependent bacterial uptake and MAPK- and NF-κB–dependent up-regulation
  of ROS (p40phox, p47phox, p67phox), RNS, and autophagy (ATG5, ATG16L1) pathways,
  which promote intracellular bacterial clearance. MDMs from high TNFSF15-expressing
  rs6478108 TT IBD risk carriers in the TNFSF15 region show increased efficacy in
  inducing these antimicrobial pathways, and, conversely, MDMs from low TNFSF15-expressing
  rs6478108 CC IBD risk carriers clear bacteria less effectively.
