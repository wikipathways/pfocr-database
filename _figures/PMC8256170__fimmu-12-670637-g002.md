caption: OX40 signaling pathways mediating Tfh differentiation. Both TCR and OX40
  can activate PI3K, including P110 and P85 subunit, further leading to phosphorylation
  of AKT. pAKT then phosphorylates the FOXO1 transcription factor, which can subsequently
  be exported out of the nucleus and degraded. FOXO1, which represses Bcl6, is recognized
  as an inhibitor of Tfh differentiation. In addition, OX40 synergizes with TCR to
  allow Ca2+ influx and nuclear accumulation of NFATc1 and NFATc2. Overall, OX40 may
  mediate Tfh cell activity by augmenting TCR signaling via the NFAT or PI3K-Akt pathway.
