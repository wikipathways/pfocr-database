caption: A) Carbon and energy utilization of various intracellular pathogens relies
  on host metabolites to feed the TCA cycle. For L. pneumophila and C. burnetii host
  amino acids, particularly serine are the primary source of carbon and energy. Whereas,
  L. monocytogenes uses host glycerol and C. trachomatis utilizes host malate to feed
  the TCA cycle. B) Anabolic processes for these intracellular pathogens utilize host
  carbohydrates, primarily by the pentose phosphate pathway for cell wall biosynthesis.
  L. pneumophila also converts host glucose into the carbon storage molecule, poly-3-hydroxybutyrate.
