caption: In normal cells, hypomethylated miR-148a promoter allows the binding of transcription
  activator USF1, resulting in the activation of miR-148a expression. Mature miR-148a
  represses the protein expression of the integrin pathway downstream targets such
  as ITGB8, VAV2, ROCK1 and WASL, and thereby inhibits cell migration. Conversely,
  in NPC cells, hypermehtylated miR-148a promoter prevents the binding of USF1 and
  causes silencing of miR-148a. In the absence of miR-148a, NPC cells overexpress
  oncogenic integrin pathway targets and, in turn, trigger cell migration.
