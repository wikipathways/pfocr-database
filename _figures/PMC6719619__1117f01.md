caption: 'Schematic of T-cell development. Stages of αβ T-cell development are shown,
  with approximate timing of cell identity choice (gold fill), TCR gene rearrangement
  and selection (red–brown fill), and effector type choice (blue–gray fill; arrows).
  Developmental stages dependent on continued Notch signaling are indicated. For conventional
  αβ T cells, invariant natural killer T (iNKT) cells, and innate lymphoid cells (ILCs;
  lymphoid non-T cells), the distinct killer and various “helper” effector types (TH,
  NKT, or ILC types 1, 2, and 3 or 17) are shown with the key transcription factors
  that define them. T-lineage stage surface markers are as follows: (DP) CD4+ CD8+;
  (DN) CD4− CD8−; (ETP) Kit+ CD25− CD44+ DN; (DN2a) Kit++ CD25+ CD44+ DN; (DN2b) Kit+
  CD25+ CD44+; (DN3a) Kit− CD25+ CD44−; (DN3b) Kit− CD25+ CD44− CD28+; (DN4) Kit−
  CD25− CD44− CD28+. TCRβ gene rearrangement occurs in DN3a stage. TCRα gene rearrangement
  occurs in DP stage. γδ T cells diverge from the αβ T-cell pathway at the stages
  indicated and undergo fast intrathymic effector specialization (effector type 1
  and effector type 17; not shown) concomitant with TCR expression.'
