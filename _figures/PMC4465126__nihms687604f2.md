caption: Adrenergic receptor signaling and cross-talk with the renin-angiotensin aldosterone
  system. Activation of β-adrenergic receptor signaling increases cAMP levels and
  activates protein kinase A (PKA) to promote vasodilation and an increase in cardiac
  contractility. These effects are opposed by α2-AR signaling. PKA also activates
  the MAP kinase signaling pathway, which is downstream from the angiotensin type
  1 receptor (AT1R). AT1R also activates protein tyrosine kinases (PTK), which have
  been implicated in pulmonary hypertension, and similar to α1-AR, activates phospholipase
  C (PLC). Both AT1R and mineralocorticoid receptor (MR) activate NADPH oxidase to
  increase reactive oxygen species formation (ROS), which decrease bioavailable nitric
  oxide (NO•).
