caption: 'Interferon signaling in HPV-containing keratinocytes. Pathogen detection
  proteins such as TLRs or cytoplasmic nucleic acid sensors detect the presence of
  viral infection and trigger the activation of IRF and NFκB signaling pathways. Type
  I IFNs are produced and bind in an autocrine or paracrine manner to IFNAR1/2, which
  activates the JAK/STAT1/2 pathway and induces ISGs. Type II IFN (IFNγ) binds to
  the IFN gamma receptor (IFNGR) to activate JAK/STAT1 pathway and induce a distinct
  set of genes. For reviews, see,. EGFR inhibits IFN in part through suppressing IRF1.
  Unknown signals induce IFNκ expression in an IRF1 dependent manner. Targets of HPV
  oncogenes are shown. Color scheme in online version similar to . NFκB BS: NFκB binding
  site; IRF-E: IRF1 element; GAS: gamma activation site.'
