caption: The role of autophagy in regulation of intestinal stem cell proliferation
  and colorectal cancer progression. Blockages in the autophagy pathway stabilize
  Egfr, which autonomously activates ISC proliferation. Excess ISC divisions trigger
  general epithelial stress, EC-localized yki and rho (Egfr ligand splicer) activation,
  which promote the production of upd3 and the activation of Egfr ligands, respectively.
  This catalyzes a feedforward reaction and massive ISC hyperplasia. ISC hyperproliferation-generated
  local mechano-stress could potentially magnify proliferation signals. This process
  mimics carcinogenic processes (from early-stage cancer stem cells to cancer), and
  suggests a tumor suppressive role for autophagy in early stages of colorectal cancer
  progression. ISC, intestinal stem cell; EB, enteroblast; EC, enterocyte; VM, visceral
  muscle; yki, yorkie; upd3, unpaired 3; rho, rhomboid.
