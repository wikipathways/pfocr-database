  domain (green) that binds IMP (gold) and NAD+ (salmon) in the active site, and a
  regulatory domain (pink) with three allosteric nucleotide binding sites that bind
  GTP (dark blue) and ATP (light blue). The monomer is shown in the GTP-bound inhibited
  state. (C) In solution IMPDH is a tetramer, binding of ATP (sites 1 and 2) or GTP
  (sites 2 and 3) promotes octamer assembly. In the presence of ATP, octamers are
  extended and when GTP is bound in site 3, octamers are compressed (D) IMPDH octamers
  can assemble into filaments of stacked octamers. Figure adapted from [].
