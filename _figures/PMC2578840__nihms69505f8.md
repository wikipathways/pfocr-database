caption: Model of proposed signaling pathway. In mature osteoclasts, TGF-β rapidly
  activates both SMAD and TAK1. TAK1 activation leads to sequential activation of
  MEK1/2, AKT, NIK, and IKKα/β, leading to phosphorylation of IκB. This causes targeted
  degradation of IκB, allowing subsequent NFκB nuclear localization to promote osteoclast
  survival. TGF-β-dependent SMAD2/3 activation and nuclear localization also occurs
  in parallel. NFκB activates transcription of BCLXL and Mcl-1 whereas SMAD2/3 activate
  transcription of Mcl-1.
