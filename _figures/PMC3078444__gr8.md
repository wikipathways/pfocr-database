caption: The proposed mechanism of homocysteine-induced inhibition of hepatic glycogen
  synthesis. Homocysteine induces an increase in the intracellular levels of cAMP
  and the phosphorylation (P) of CREB in hepatocytes. Activation of this pathway promotes
  the expression of PGC-1α, which, in turn, up-regulates the expression of TBR3. The
  TRB3 inhibits the phosphorylation of Akt and GSK3β. The GSK3β induces the phosphorylation
  of glycogen synthase (GS), leading to reduced glycogen synthesis.
