caption: The biomechanical mechanism of ovarian cell canceration and metastasis. (A)
  Focal adhesion signals, Rho/Rock activity, KRAS, nonmuscle myosin II (Myo-II), histone
  acetyltransferase (HBO1) could reduce cell elasticity through signal transduction,
  thereby increasing the risk of metastasis; Under high shear environment, the epithelial-mesenchymal
  transformation and tumor stem cell markers are significantly expressed. (B, C) The
  abnormal activation of the PI3K/Akt pathway significantly increases the malignancy
  of cells. While in hematogenous metastasis, high shear stress destroyed circulating
  tumor cells and prevented cancer metastasis; Local adhesion formation, myosin light
  chain phosphorylation, enhanced cell traction, and cellular pseudopodia produced
  by extracellular matrix tissue increase substrate stiffness, gradually stretch and
  elong the cells, thereby making ovarian cancer cells have a high risk of metastasis.
  (D) Proliferation of ovarian cancer cells will produce tension stress and compressive
  stress on the cells around the developing mass and the increase of these two stress
  will further increase the invasion ability of tumor cells. EMT, epithelial-mesenchymal
  transformation.
