caption: SMAD pathway. a TGF-β1 binds to the type II receptor to activate the type
  I receptor. The activated type I receptor phosphorylates Smad2/3 which, once phosphorylated,
  interact with Smad4 to form the Smad2/3/Smad4 complex. The complex migrates to the
  nucleus to bind to DNA and activate transcription. b The phosphorylation of Smad3
  is prevented through the interaction of Smad7, a TGF-β1 type I receptor inhibitor,
  that binds to and blocks the TGF-β1-associated Smad signaling pathway shown in a.
