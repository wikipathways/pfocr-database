caption: Relationship between the downregulation of PTEN and autophagy. In most cancer
  cells, the expression of PTEN is either downregulated or absent. PTEN downregulation
  is the result, in part, of the hyperactivation of NFκB and the downstream overexpression
  of the PTEN repressor YY1. The inhibition of PTEN results in the failure to inhibit
  the PI3K/AKT pathway and in the activation of Beclin-1 and the induction of autophagy
  (solid lines). In contrast, the overexpression of PTEN, as a result of the inhibition
  of NFκB activity and the PTEN repressor target YY1, results in inhibition of the
  PI3K/AKT pathway, inhibition of Beclin-1, and failure to induce autophagy (dashed
  lines).
