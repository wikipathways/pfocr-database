caption: Schematic presentation of the dual mechanisms of the BH4/NOS axis in acquired
  capabilities along with cancer progression. BH4 pro-tumor activity comprises proliferation
  induction through TGF-β pathway activation, apoptosis abrogation by Bax inhibition
  and Bcl-2 increase, p70S6K-dependent promotion of migration, and angiogenesis stimulation
  by Ras/PI3K/Akt signaling. As anti-tumor, BH4/NOS axis induces apoptosis due to
  caspase-3 activation and iron uptake decrease, reduces proliferation by decreasing
  polyamine concentration, and impairs migration and invasion by reducing VEGF-A/p70S6K
  pathway and MMP-2 and MMP-9 activity, respectively.
