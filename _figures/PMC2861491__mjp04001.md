caption: Destruction of the β-catenin (β-Cat) destructor Axin. (A) β-catenin is phosphorylated
  by the Axin complex containing GSK3, CKIα and APC. Phosphorylated β-catenin is recognized
  by β-Trcp, an ubiquitin ligase, and degraded by the ubiquitination/proteasome pathway.
  (B) Wnt stimulation inhibits β-catenin phosphorylation/destruction, leading to increased
  β-catenin protein (which then enters the nucleus to activate transcription). (C)
  TNKS binds to and promotes the destruction of Axin, likely through Axin PARsylation.
  As such, β-catenin stabilization occurs. (D) XAV939 and IWR-1 bind to and inhibit
  TNKS, and therefore stabilize Axin and enhance β-catenin destruction. IWR-1 may
  also bind to and stabilize Axin.
