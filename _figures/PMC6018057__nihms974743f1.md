caption: The mevalonate (MA) pathway is a ubiquitous biochemical pathway present in
  all cells. It is essential for many diverse and basic cellular functions, and is
  necessary for cell survival. The downstream metabolites of MA include the sterol
  (e.g. squalene, cholesterol) and isoprenoid (IPP, FPP, GGPP) metabolites. The isoprenoids
  are essential for the function of the small GTPases Rho, Rab, and Ras families.
  The statins directly inhibit HMG-CoA reductase which depletes intracellular pools
  of MA and downstream metabolites important in cell cycle, survival, proliferation,
  regeneration, signaling, and homeostasis.
