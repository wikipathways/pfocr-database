caption: Proposed model for how mTORC1 activity controls pro-IL-1β expression. Physiological
  activation of the Akt-mTORC1, MAPK, and NF-κB signaling pathways by LPS stimulation
  allows for transient and inducible IL-1β expression in macrophages. Activated mTORC1
  inhibits C/EBPβ expression, then brakes IL-1β expression later. Constitutive or
  aberrant activation of mTORC1 impairs the ability of macrophages to synthesize IL-1β
  in response to LPS stimuli. A critical mediator of this process is C/EBPβ, whose
  expression is downregulated by increased mTORC1 activity.
