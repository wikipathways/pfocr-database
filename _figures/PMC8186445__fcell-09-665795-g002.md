caption: 'The canonical pathway: Wnt binds to the transmembrane receptor Frizzled
  protein (Fz) and to the low-density protein co-receptor related to the lipoprotein
  receptor (LRP5/6) to activate the intracellular Dishevelled protein (Dvl), which
  therefore inhibits the activity of the complex composed of cytoplasmic glycogen
  synthase kinase 3β (GSK-3β), adenomatous polyposis coli (APC), and Axin, leading
  to dephosphorylation, aggregation and nuclear translocation of β-catenin. Within
  the nucleus, β-catenin combines with T cell transcription factor (TCF)/lymphoid
  reinforcement factor (LEF) to form a complex, leading to transcriptional activation
  and expression of specific genes. Wnt/β-catenin signaling enhanced neurogenesis
  by regulating proneural genes (Nurr-1, Pitx-3, Ngn-2, and NeuroD1).'
