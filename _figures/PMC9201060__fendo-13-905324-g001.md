caption: The canonical AMH signaling pathway. AMH assembles a receptor signaling complex
  composed of type I and type II receptors. The kinase domain of AMHR2 is constitutively
  active and phosphorylates and activates the kinase domain of the type I receptor
  (ALK2 or 3), which phosphorylates Smad1, 5, and 8. The phosphorylated Smads form
  a heterotrimer with Smad4, which translocates to the nucleus, and binds to other
  transcription factors (TF). The Smad/TF complex binds to promoter or enhancer regions
  of target genes to regulate transcription.
