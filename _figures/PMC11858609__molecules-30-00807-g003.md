caption: Illustrates the NF-κB signaling pathway, highlighting the role of pro-inflammatory
  stimuli such as LPS, IL-1, IL-36, TNF-α, and PAMPs, which activate receptors (TLR4,
  IL-1R, TNFR) to initiate intracellular signaling cascades. Activation of adaptor
  proteins (MyD88, TRAF6, IRAK1/2) leads to phosphorylation and degradation of IκB,
  freeing the NF-κB complex (p50/p65) to translocate into the nucleus and activate
  immune response genes. Curcumin acts as an inhibitor at multiple points, preventing
  IκB degradation and blocking NF-κB nuclear translocation, thereby reducing inflammation
  and modulating immune responses. Created with BioRender.com
