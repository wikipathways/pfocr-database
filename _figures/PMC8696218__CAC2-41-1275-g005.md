caption: 'The Notch signaling pathway. The Notch single‐stranded pathways undergo
  proteolytic processing in the Golgi complex, which is furin protease‐mediated (S1
  cleavage). The receptor is transported to the cell surface membrane. The extracellular
  domain of the Notch receptor in the signaling cell binds to the Notch ligands (Delta
  and Jagged) expressed by the adjacent cell. This induces the second proteolytic
  step by ADAM/TACE metalloproteases and γ‐secretase, which releases the intracellular
  Notch domain that translocates to the nucleus and then binds to the CSL transcription
  factor and activates the expression of Notch target genes. Abbreviations: ADAM,
  a disintegrin and metallopeptidase; CSL, CBF1 suppressor of hairless LAG1; MYC,
  MYC proto‐oncogene, bHLH transcription factor; SOX9, SRY‐box transcription factor
  9; TACE, tumor necrosis factor‐alpha converting enzyme'
