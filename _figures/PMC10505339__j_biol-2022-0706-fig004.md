caption: BMP signalling pathway. In the canonical BMP signalling pathway, BMP signalling
  molecules bind and activate the type I receptor and type II receptor. This binding
  leads to the phosphorylation of Smad1/5/8, with the involvement of Smad6/7. Phosphorylated
  Smad1/5/8 then forms a complex with Smad4 and translocates to the nucleus, where
  it regulates gene expression. In the non-canonical BMP signalling pathway, BMP signalling
  molecules can regulate gene expression by activating various signalling pathways,
  such as MAPK, PI3K, and PKC.
