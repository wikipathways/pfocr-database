caption: A schematic illustration showing the proposed mechanisms by which L. sakei
  LB-P12 ameliorates OA pathogenesis. In macrophage, L. sakei LB-P12 may inhibit the
  production of proinflammatory cytokines by suppressing NF-κB signaling pathway.
  In chondrocytes, L. sakei LB-P12 may also inhibit NF-κB signaling pathway, thereby
  reducing HIF-2α-mediated MMP-13 expression. Collectively, L. sakei LB-P12 may improve
  joint health by attenuating inflammation and preventing cartilage degradation in
  osteoarthritis
