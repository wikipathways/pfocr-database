caption: TREM-2 ligand binding activates DAP12 and downstream Syk and ZAP-70 (Zeta-chain-associated
  protein kinase 70) which in turn activate PI3K (phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate
  3-kinase) pathway resulting in Ca2+ influx. Ca2+ activates NFAT (nuclear factor
  of activated T cells) by phosphorylation through calmodulin kinase. NFAT translocates
  to nucleus and triggers the expression of anti-inflammatory genes. PI3K inhibits
  TLR (toll-like receptor) signaling via Akt pathway which in turn inhibits inflammation.
