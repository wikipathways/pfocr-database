caption: Role of autophagy in the antiviral effect of kaempferol. (A) Effect of kaempferol
  on the formation of autophagic vacuoles in Vero cells. (B) Reverse effect of autophagy
  inhibitor SP600125 on the antiviral activity of kaempferol in ASFV-infected Vero
  cells. (C) Regulation of autophagy signaling pathway. Kaempferol causes AMPK activation,
  which can lead to autophagy induction through downstream signaling. These signaling
  pathways eventually result in phagophore formation and subsequent stages involve
  autophagosome maturation, lysosomal fusion, and finally cargo degradation. The SP600125
  inhibitor can prevent Beclin-1 upregulation as part of repressing the JNK-Beclin1
  signaling pathway, which can in turn inhibit autophagy induction (). Results in
  (A) and (B) graphs represent the mean (± SD) of three independent experiments (n=3).
  Significant differences compared to control are denoted by *p<0.05.
