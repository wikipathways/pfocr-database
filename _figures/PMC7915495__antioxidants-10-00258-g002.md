caption: The protective mechanism via the Nrf2-HO-1 pathway on CKD. When Keap1, which
  targets Nrf2 for ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation, is decreased or inactivated,
  phosphorylated Nrf2 is translocated into the nucleus, and transcription of downstream
  antioxidant enzymes is activated. In particular, by HO-1, fibrosis, apoptosis, and
  inflammation are attenuated either directly or indirectly through inhibition of
  OS, thus improving kidney damage. CKD, chronic kidney disease; CO, carbon monoxide;
  GPx, glutathione peroxidase; GST, Glutathione-S-transferase; HO-1, Heme oxygenase-1;
  Nqo1, NADPH quinone oxidoreductase; Nrf2, nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor
  2; SOD, superoxide dismutase; ROS, reactive oxygen species.
