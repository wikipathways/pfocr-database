caption: Autophagy signaling overview. The illustration identifies the proposed autophagy
  mechanism that occurs in Beas-2B and B-Cr cells after Cr(VI) treatment. An increase
  in NO levels can trigger a Bcl-2-mediated autophagy or apoptosis pathway. High levels
  of NO expression can lead to S-nitrosylation of Bcl-2; a process that stabilizes
  the Bcl-2-Beclin-1 complex thereby preventing the release of the Beclin-1 protein
  which is needed for autophagy initiation. This in turn will prevent the fusion of
  the LC3-II-containing autophagosome and the lysosome; a terminal step in the autophagic
  process.
