caption: p53 dependent and independent tumor suppressive functions of ARF. ARF plays
  a key role in cellular senescence and tumorigenesis through activation of the p53
  pathway. ARF binds and sequesters MDM2 in the nucleolus, leading to the activation
  of p53 in the nucleoplasm. p53 transcribes numerous target genes and activates cellular
  senescence or apoptosis. ARF also functions in a p53-independent manner through
  interaction with numerous target proteins. ARF regulates sumoylation, transcription
  activities, or protein turnover of target proteins, which lead to tumor suppression
  and senescence.
