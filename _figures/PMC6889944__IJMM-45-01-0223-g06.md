caption: The proposed antifibrotic mechanism of G. acuta in ISO-induced myocardial
  fibrosis in rats. Myocardial injury activates the TGF-β/Smads pro-fibrotic signaling
  pathway. G. acuta inhibits TGF-β1 expression, downregulates TβRI expression and
  TβRI and II phosphorylation levels, suppresses Smad expression and phosphorylation
  activation, and causes a decrease in ISO-induced α-SMA, collagens I and III expression
  in heart tissues. The results indicate that G. acuta improves ISO-induced myocardial
  fibrosis via inhibition of the TGF-β1/Smads signaling pathway. P-Smad, phosphorylated-mothers
  against decapentaplegic homolog; ISO, isoproter-enol; G. acuta, Gentianella acuta;
  P-TβR, phosphorylated- TGF-β receptor.
