caption: The hexosamine biosynthesis pathway (HBP) produces the metabolite, UDP‐GlcNAc,
  that is used for N‐ and O‐glycosylation of proteins, lipids and nucleic acids. The
  de novo synthesis of UDP‐GlcNAc is catalyzed by GFAT, which utilizes glutamine and
  the glycolysis metabolite, fructose‐6‐phosphate, to produce glucosamine‐6‐phosphate.
  Metabolites from the TCA cycle (acetyl‐CoA) and pyrimidine biosynthesis (UTP) are
  also utilized by the HBP to produce UDP‐GlcNAc. Salvage metabolites, such as glucosamine
  (GlcN) and N‐acetyl‐glucosamine (GlcNAc) can also be used to produce UDP‐GlcNAc
