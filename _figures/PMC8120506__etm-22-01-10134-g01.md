caption: A sensor network regulating mitochondrial biogenesis. AMPK activation promotes
  the expression of PGC-1α and its downstream signals and serves a key role in mitochondrial
  biogenesis. The activation of AMPK by phosphorylation of SIRT1 enhances PGC-1a dependent
  transcription. PGC-1α interacts with nuclear receptors and stimulates Nrf1/2 to
  participate in the expression of various nuclear coding genes and TFAM, leading
  to an increase of mtDNA replication transcribed on TFAM. These processes ultimately
  affect mitochondrial biogenesis, promoting mitochondrial mass, ATP synthesis, protein
  synthesis and glucose absorption. AMPK, AMP-activated protein kinase; SIRT1, sirtuin
  1; PGC-1α, proliferator-activated receptor γ coactivator 1α; TFAM, transcription
  factor A; mtDNA, mitochondrial DNA.
