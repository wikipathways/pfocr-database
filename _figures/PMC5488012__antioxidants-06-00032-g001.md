caption: Role of ROS in VEGF-mediated angiogenesis. Stabilization of HIF1α by hypoxia
  or by an increase of ROS leads to the expression of several proangiogenic factors
  such as VEGF and angiopoietin. VEGF stimulates multiple signaling pathways in ECs,
  with a simultaneous ROS production by NADPH oxidases. ROS would help to maintain
  the VEGF signaling pathway activated through the inhibition of PTPs. Some Ca2+ channels
  can be regulated by ROS. Finally, ROS can modulate the activity of transcription
  factors involved in the regulation of angiogenesis. More details in the text. (Figure
