caption: Growth factor stimulation leads to activation of receptor protein tyrosine
  kinases, which in turn phosphorylate adaptor proteins such as IRS1. Phospho-IRS1
  then recruits the PI3K heterodimers (p85 regulatory and p110 catalytic subunits)
  and activates the lipid kinase activity. The activated PI3Ks convert PIP2 to PIP3.
  The second messenger PIP3 recruits PDK and AKT to cytoplasmic membrane, where PDK
  phosphorylates and activates AKT. Activated AKT phosphorylates an array of downstream
  targets that control cell proliferation, apoptosis and cell migration.
