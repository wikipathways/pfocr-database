caption: Diagram of the proposed molecular mechanism of action of PMF-induced intrinsic
  apoptosis in human MCF-7 breast cancer cells. In the mitochondria, PMF induces the
  loss of MMP, inhibits BCL-2 protein levels, and increases BAX and Cyt c, which correlates
  with overproduction of ROS, expression and activity of caspases and upregulation
  of PARP-1. PMF also increases protein levels of p53 and p21, resulting in G1 phase
  cell cycle arrest. Also, PMF inhibits protein expression of the NF-κB p65 subunit
  and this could be related to the inhibition of cell migration.
