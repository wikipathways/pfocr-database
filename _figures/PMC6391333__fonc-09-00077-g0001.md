caption: Model of anchorage-dependent regulation of cell survival and glucose metabolism.
  Adhesion of cells to the ECM activates integrins and receptor tyrosine kinase (RTK)
  signaling which triggers different pro-survival and proliferation pathways like
  AKT and MAPK. AKT inhibits BIM, a pro-apoptotic protein preventing anoikis and increases
  glucose uptake and glycolysis by upregulating the transcription of glucose transporters
  (GLUT1) and hexokinase 2 (HK2) respectively. Oxidative phosphorylation (OXPHOS)
  of pyruvate derived from glucose yields ATP for cellular function. Glucose shunted
  through the PPP pathway generates NADPH which prevents ROS induced cell death.
