caption: Regulation of NPR1 phosphorylation by the rapamycin-sensitive TOR signaling
  pathway. (A) Under good nutrient conditions, TOR promotes the association of TAP42
  and SIT4, thereby inactivating the phosphatase activity of SIT4. NPR1 is, therefore,
  maintained in a highly phosphorylated state. (B) Rapamycin treatment leads to inactivation
  of TOR and to dissociation of the regulatory subunit TAP42 from SIT4. In turn, activated
  SIT4 dephosphorylates NPR1. Arrows indicate activation; bars indicate inhibition.
