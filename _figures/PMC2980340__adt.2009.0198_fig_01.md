caption: Signaling to GCN4 via eIF2B. (Left) In wild-type cells amino acids (aa) repress
  kinase Gcn2p, maintaining low eIF2 phosphorylation (eIF2αP) and active eIF2B. This
  represses GCN4 translation. (Middle) Starvation for one or more aa activates Gcn2p,
  inhibiting eIF2B and activating translation of GCN4 and transcription of its target
  genes. (Right) Mutations in eIF2B (eIF2BM) override this signaling pathway and activate
  GCN4 constitutively.
