caption: TIR8/SIGIRR, a negative regulatory receptor of ILR and TLR signaling. TIR8/SIGIRR
  is composed by a single extracellular Ig domain, a transmembrane domain, an intracellular
  conserved TIR domain and a long intracellular tail. Two replaced amino acid in the
  TIR domain (Cys 222, Leu 305) are potentially involved in non-conventional activation.
  TIR8/SIGIRR is an orphan receptor, but IL-36Ra has been proposed as a TIR8/SIGIRR
  ligand in glial cells. TIR8/SIGIRR inhibits ILR and TLR signaling by competing with
  MyD88 and IRAK recruitment at the TIR domain thus dampening the signaling pathway
  leading to NF-kB activation. In T cells and epithelial cells, TIR8/SIGIRR inhibits
  IL-1-dependent activation of the Akt- mTOR pathway and of JNK, thus controlling
  cell proliferation and survival. In vivo studies in gene-targeted mice demonstrate
  that Tir8/Sigirr acts as a non-redundant negative regulator in different inflammatory
  conditions dependent on ILRs or TLRs.
