caption: Sex differences in the signal transduction pathways of endothelial nitric
  oxide (NO) synthase (eNOS) activation in endothelial cells by estradiol and testosterone
  and the subsequent vascular response. Estradiol is generally thought to increase
  NO availability through genomic and nongenonimc pathways in both men and women,
  resulting in vasodilation. Testosterone has similar effects in men. Testosterone
  works through a separate pathway in women, resulting in reduced NO availability
  and impaired vasodilation. The dashed line indicates effects that are uncertain.
  ER, estrogen receptor; AR, androgen receptor; PI3K, phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase;
  GPER, G protein-coupled estrogen receptor 1.
