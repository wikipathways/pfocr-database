caption: Viral replication triggers Dcr-2 processing of dsRNA intermediates into siRNA
  duplexes. Virus-derived siRNAs are then loaded into the RISC, a guide strand acting
  as a specificity determinant for the targeting and degradation of cognate viral
  mRNAs. VSRs bind long dsRNAs (YFV C, FHV B2, DCV 1A, H1N1 NS1 or TCV P38) preventing
  processing by Dicer, and/or the smaller siRNA duplexes (FHV B2, H1N1 NS1, TCV P38
  or CymRSV) preventing loading into the RISC. VSRs may in some cases also interact
  directly with Ago2 (CrPV 1A or TCV P38) to inhibit slicing activity.
