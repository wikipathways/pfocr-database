caption: Classic AHR signaling pathway. Prior to ligand binding, AHR remains in cytosol
  bound to HSP90, P23, and ARA9. When a ligand binds, a conformational change occurs,
  exposing the nuclear localization sequence (NLS) in AHR’s N-terminus. Presentation
  of NLS permits the translocation of AHR to the nucleus and subsequent dimerization
  with ARNT. The AHR–ARNT heterodimer recognizes and binds to AHREs in the genome
  and initiates transcription of select genes. This interaction can be inhibited by
  AHRR, CYP1 metabolism of ligands, and post-translational modification of the receptor.,,,,,,,−,−,,,,,,
