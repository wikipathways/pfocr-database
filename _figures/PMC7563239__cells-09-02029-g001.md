caption: Possible mode of action of matrikines on pulmonary arterial endothelial cells.
  Type IV collagen matrikines alter cell survival in endothelial cells. Arresten and
  canstatin activate Fas-L-mediated apoptosis (caspase 8 activation) via an unknown
  mechanism [,]. Upon integrin binding, arresten, canstatin, and tumstatin induce
  caspase-9 dependent mitochondrial apoptosis via inhibiting FAK phosphorylation [,,,].
  Canstatin and tumstatin attenuate proliferation by inhibiting the FAK/PI3K/AKT/mTOR
  signaling pathway [,]. BM HSPGs influence cell survival and cell motility. Endostatin
  and endorepellin activate autophagy in a Beclin1-dependent manner [,]. In addition,
  endostatin and endorepellin trigger the disassembly of actin stress fibers by inhibiting
  caveolin-associated Src-mediated RhoA kinase [] and by activating the FAK/p38/HSP27
  signaling pathway [], respectively. Hence, matrikines can interfere with crucial
  EC functions involved in PH pathophysiology. Receptors and signaling proteins involved
  in the depicted pathways, but not directly investigated in the indicated studies,
  are represented in gray. Fas-L, Fas ligand; FAK, focal adhesion kinase; PI3K, Phosphoinositide
  3-kinases; AKT, alpha serine/threonine-protein kinase; mTOR, mammalian target of
  rapamycin; BM HSPGs, basement membrane heparan sulfate proteoglycans; HSP27, heat
  shock protein 27; EC, endothelial cells; PH, pulmonary hypertension.
