caption: 'The role of lactic acid in macrophages during inflammation as a signaling
  molecule. Lactic acid communicates with macrophages by two main mechanisms: transporter-mediated
  (McT) or sodium-dependent co-transport (SLC5A8, SLC5A12), and receptor-mediated
  (GPR). Lactic acid employs a negative feedback mechanism to impede glycolysis. Lactic
  acid stimulates the Wnt/β-catenin and Yap/Taz signaling pathways and suppresses
  the NF-κB signaling pathway via binding to GPR receptors on the cell membrane. Lactic
  acid penetrates the nucleus and attaches to histone lysine residues, so directly
  stimulates the activation of M2 genes by histone lactylation. The figure was created
  using BioRender.com'
