caption: Schematic molecular mechanisms of EGCG and curcumin activity. EGCG was shown
  to inhibit cell growth in NF639 breast cancer [], in BT-74 breast cancer, and in
  YCU-H891 head and neck squamous carcinoma cell lines with ErbB2 overexpression [];
  downregulate the phosphorylation of ErbB proteins in A-4311 epidermoid carcinoma
  cell line [], in colon cancer cells, in head and neck squamous cancer cells [, ,
  ], in BT-74 breast cancer, and in YCU-H891 head and neck squamous carcinoma cell
  lines []; and reduce the activity of downstream MAPK cascade [], PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway
  in pancreatic cancer cells [, ], and JAK/STAT3 pathway in pancreatic cancer cells
  with ErbB expression []. In cancer cells with ErbB protein expression, curcumin
  was able to inhibit the phosphorylation of ErbB2 in breast cancer cells [], downregulate
  ErbB2 by ubiquitination in SK-BR3 breast cancer cells and COS-7 fibroblast transfected
  with ErbB2 [], inhibit tumor proliferation and induce apoptosis in Ntera-2 human
  malignant testicular cell line [], modulate the activity of STAT [, ], and inhibit
  the phosphorylation of Akt and MAPK in BT-474 and SK-BR-3 breast cancer cell lines
  [].
