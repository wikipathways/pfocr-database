caption: Diagram of the hypothesized auditory CS pathway for delay eyeblink conditioning
  in adult rats. Auditory CS information initially projects to the cochlear nucleus
  (CN). The CN, superior olive (SO), nucleus of the lateral leminiscus (LL), and inferior
  colliculus (IC) then send converging inputs to the medial auditory thalamic nuclei
  (MATN). Auditory information is then conveyed through a direct projection to the
  basal pontine nuclei (PN). The PN sends mossy fiber projections into the cerebellar
  cortex (CTX) and interpositus nucleus (IPN). Finally, the IPN sends output for conditioned
  response (CR) expression.
