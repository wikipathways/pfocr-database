caption: JNK signaling delays aging in Drosophila fat body. IIS inhibits FOXO function.
  The transcription factor Relish (Rel) of the IMD pathway can be activated in old
  fat body cells to activate the expression of downstream target antimicrobial peptide
  (AMP) genes. Elevated expression of AMP can induce apoptosis, elicit depolarization
  of mitochondria and significantly shorten lifespan. JNK signaling blocks the effects
  of IIS, enabling expression of FOXO target genes. JNK activates the transcription
  factor AP-1, which forms a repressosome complex in the nucleus together with Stat92E
  and Dsp1. This complex competitively blocks Rel binding to immune effector gene
  promoters and recruits ambient histone deacetylase to inhibit AMP gene transcription.
