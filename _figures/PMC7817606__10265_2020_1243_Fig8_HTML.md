caption: Models for rapid signaling mechanisms in plants. a Schematic representation
  of ion channel activities that generate action potentials in animals and their hypothetical
  counterparts in plants. b Classic action potential model in plants. Depolarization
  of the plasma membrane activates neighboring voltage-gated channels, leading to
  further depolarization. c Ligand-gated Ca2+ influx through GLRs (olive green). GLR
  ligands come directly from distal sources such as wounded cells or are secreted
  from cells constituting the signaling pathway through unknown channels or transporters
  (green). d ROS-Ca2+ wave. RBOHD is a ROS-producing enzyme whose activity is upregulated
  by increases in cytoplasmic Ca2+ levels. Ca2+ influx into the cytoplasm occurs through
  as-yet-unidentified ROS-activated Ca2+ channels on the plasma membrane, and the
  cytoplasmic Ca2+ concentration further increases due to Ca2+-induced Ca2+ release
  (CICR) mediated by the vacuolar Ca2+ channel TPC1
