caption: 'Proposed mechanisms mediating the effect of the (pro)renin receptor (PRR)
  in metanephric kidney development. (a): PRR interacts with Wnt receptor frizzled
  (Fz) to regulate polarization and intercalation of collecting duct (CD) cells via
  planar cell polarity (PCP)/convergent extension (CE) Wnt signaling pathway [, ,
  ]. (b): PRR may regulate UB branching by (1) inhibiting c-kit transcription via
  promyelocytic zinc finger transcription factor (PLZF) [, , ] (2) induction of Erk1/2,
  PI3K/Akt or epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) phosphorylation [, , , , –],
  and (3) interaction with LRP5/6 Wnt coreceptor leading to activation of β-catenin
  and Ret gene expression [, , ]. (c): PRR interacts with LRP5/6 and V-ATPase to form
  a complex at the plasma membrane. Following endocytosis, V-ATPase generates a gradient
  of H+ ions that is essential for LRP5/6 phosphorylation and activation of β-catenin
  []. V-ATPase stimulates Notch signaling []. Notch acts to define the podocyte and
  proximal tubular cell fates [] and regulates differentiation of the CD cells [].'
