caption: The protective role of TBHQ related to Nrf2/HO-1 and PI3K/AKT. METH chronic
  exposure induced the body to produce excessive ROS. On the one hand, it leads to
  OS disorder by reducing immunocontent of PI3K, AKT, and p-AKT; on the other hand,
  it promotes apoptosis through increasing caspase-3. However, TBHQ activates the
  Nrf2/HO-1 pathway, thereby reducing OS and protecting the normal signal transduction
  of the PI3K/AKT pathway to exert antioxidative stress and antiapoptotic effects.
