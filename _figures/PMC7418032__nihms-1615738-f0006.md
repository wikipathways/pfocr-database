caption: The removal of active and damaged mitochondria through autophagy maintains
  HSCs in a low metabolic, quiescent state. Active mitochondria with high ATP production
  and ROS levels are cleared through macroautophagy. Mitochondria with impaired function,
  such as deletion of Atad3a, may provoke Pink/Parkin pathway for mitophagy. Phosphorylated
  Parkin promotes the ubiquitination of mitochondrial substrates, recruits p62, and
  links mitochondria to LC3 for mitophagy. The presence of Atad3a on healthy mitochondria
  promotes the degradation of Pink1 via protease cleavage in the mitochondria matrix.
  Autophagy and mitophagy are regulated through a cascade of reactions involving Atg
  (Atg5, 7, 10, 12, 13, 16L1), Ulk1, FIP200, and other autophagy-related proteins.
  Autophagic pathways are interlinked with mitochondria biogenesis (mTOR, AMPK, LKB1,
  TSC2, and PGC1a) along with lysosomal pathways (FLCN, TFEB, TFE3, and MITF).
