caption: Dysregulated Wnt-FoxM1/ β-catenin signaling pathway regulates self-renewal
  and tumorigenecity of GSCsWnt activates its receptor Frizzled and co-receptor LRP5/6
  to disrupt or functionally inactivate the destruction complex containing Axin/APC/CK1/GSK3β,
  leading to aberrant accumulation of β-catenin in cytosol. FoxM1 can bind to free
  β-catenin in cytosol and subsequently translocate into nucleus. In nucleus, FoxM1/β-catenin
  complex is associated with TCF4 on Wnt target-gene promoters, such as c-myc and
  cyclinD1, to promote the expression of the Wnt target-genes hence self-renewal and
  tumorigenecity of GSCs.
