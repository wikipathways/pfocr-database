caption: The activation of BIM by cellular stress in normal cells. The expression
  of BIM is regulated at multiple levels for normal homeostasis. Bim mRNA can be induced
  by the inhibition of AKT, leading to FOXO3a nuclear translocation and transcriptional
  activation of Bim. Activation of the Ras/Raf/MEK/ERK/RSK pathway leads to phosphorylation
  and to proteosomal degradation of BIM. Conversely, BIM can be stabilized by dephosphorylation
  on growth factor deprivation. Alternatively, JNK-mediated phosphorylation of BIM
  renders the protein more active. Once activated, BIM can neutralize antiapoptotic
  BCL-2 family proteins at mitochondria and/or activate BAX by direct protein-protein
  interaction. See details in the text.
