caption: mTOR signaling. The evolutionary conserved core of mTOR complexes 1 and 2
  activated via PDK1, Akt, TSC2, and Rheb. In humans and other mammals, nutrients,
  insulin and TNF signaling pathways (light blue) provide additional upstream inputs.
  The TNF pathway is integrated with the starvation sensor AMPK via the TSC complex
  (bold dark blue); insulin via the PI3K/PDK1/Akt axis. The Ragulator/Rag complex
  provides spatial control over mTORC1 that is dependent on mitochondrial oxidative
  stress, nutrient sufficiency and traffic to the lysosome. Additional innovations
  in animals include PRAS40 [,] through which Akt provides a disinhibitory signal
  to Raptor, thus favoring assembly of mTORC1, which provides an additional regulatory
  axis for mTORC1 activation.
