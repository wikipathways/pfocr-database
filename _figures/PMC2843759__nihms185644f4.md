caption: 'The pikromycin polyketide synthase is comprised of a single loading module
  and six elongation modules that span four polypeptide chains (PikAI-PikAIV). Chain
  elongation through PikAIII followed by thioesterase catalyzed termination results
  in the 12-membered ring macrolactone product, 10-deoxymethynolide 2, while continued
  elongation of the polyketide chain produces the 14-membered ring macrolactone, narbonolide
  3. Both products undergo further processing via a glycosyltransferase and P450 hydroxylase
  to yield the methymycin and pikromycin suite of antibiotics. PikAV is a type II
  thioesterase that functions to remove prematurely decarboxylated extender units
  from ACP domains. Abbreviations: KS, ketosynthase; AT, acyl transferase; ACP, acyl
  carrier protein; KR, ketoreductase; DH, dehydratase; ER, enoyl reductase; TE, thioesterase.'
