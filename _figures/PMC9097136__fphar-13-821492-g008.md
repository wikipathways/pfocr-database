caption: Schematic illustration of molecular mechanism underlying inhibition of PgLPS-induced
  periodontitis by treatment of KMUP-1. PgLPS triggers pro-inflammatory reactions
  and thus promotes maturation of osteoclasts. Amplification of osteoclastogenesis
  leads to imbalance of bone remodeling which aggravates periodontitis progression.
  KMUP-1, through elevation of the cGMP-PKG pathway and reduction of pro-inflammatory
  signaling pathways, suppresses PgLPS-induced osteoclast differentiation thereby
  diminishing functional osteoclast maturation.
