caption: Innate receptor signaling and stefin B influence NF-κB activation and caspase-11
  expression in macrophages. Stefin B could act an inhibitor of cysteine cathepsins
  in cytosol (1) and nucleus (2) in mitochondria (3) prevents the excessive ROS formation.
  Toll-like receptor (TLR) signaling is activated by TLR ligands. Signaling through
  TLR4 which is located in plasma membrane activate NF-κB via MyD88 dependent pathway.
  Moreover, caspase-11 expression is regulated by NF-κB signaling. In addition, LPS
  stimulation results in increased mitochondrial ROS formation. LPS stimulation could
  lead to non-canonical inflammasome activation of caspase-11 and caspase-1 that results
  in IL-1β and IL-18 processing and secretion.
