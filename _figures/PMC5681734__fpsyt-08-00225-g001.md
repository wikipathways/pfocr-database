caption: NRG–ErbB signaling pathway. Neuregulin-1 (NRG1) and NRG2 bind to ErbB3 and/or
  ErbB4, which in turn undergoes homo or heterodimerization and activates PI3K. PI3K
  then activates AKT and subsequently mTOR causing initiation of protein synthesis
  via the mTOR signaling pathway. mTOR phosphorylates and activates P70S6K which facilitates
  phosphorylation of small ribosomal protein 6 (S6) and eukaryotic translation initiation
  factor 4B (eIF4B) and leads to initiation of protein synthesis. Activated mTOR also
  causes phosphorylation and inactivation of eIF4EBP1, which release eIF4E and facilitates
  translation.
