caption: Diagram describing the possibility for Roflumilast to increase DCX and MAP2
  expression. PDE4 can hydrolyse cAMP to 5’-AMP. This cAMP depletion leads to the
  inactivation of the cAMP-PKA signalling pathway. Roflumilast can inhibit the action
  of PDE4 and therefore stop the depletion of cAMP which leads to the persistent activity
  of the cAMP-PKA pathway. Through PKA phosphorylation of CREB, pCREB can upregulate
  the expression of neurogenesis and neuroplasticity proteins DCX and MAP2. Red arrows
  depict the (predicted) effects for roflumilast.
