caption: Schematic of the proposed mechanism by which riboflavin improves the immune
  response. Riboflavin first activated the MAPK signaling pathway, and then induced
  DC maturation, including through an activation marker (CD69), cytokine secretion
  (TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-12p70, and IL-10), phenotypic markers (MHCII, CD40, CD80, and
  CD86), a migration marker (CCR7), and the proliferation of allogeneic T cells. Furthermore,
  riboflavin enabled inactivated influenza nasal vaccine to trigger the downstream
  mucosal and systemic immune responses.
