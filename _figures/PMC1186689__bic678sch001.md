caption: IFNγ activation of the IFNGR in keratinocytes results in activation of STAT1
  via JAK2 phosphorylation and leads to antiviral defence response (left-hand side).
  IFNγ activation also leads to a flux of free calcium. Annexin II binding calcium
  contributes to Pyk2's calcium-dependent activation. MEKK4 is tyrosine-phosphorylated
  by Pyk2 and activates MKK6, which activates p38 MAPK and results in activation of
  transcription factors for prostaglandin responses (right-hand side).
