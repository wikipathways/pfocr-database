caption: FUS-DDIT3-dependent induction of IGF2 establishes an autocrine IGF-II/IGF-IR
  signaling loop, contributing to IGF-IR/PI3K/AKT pathway activation in MLS. IGF-IR
  and PI3K-transmitted signals interfere with the Hippo kinase LATS1, which is a direct
  negative regulator of YAP1, thereby promoting nuclear accumulation of transcriptionally
  active YAP1. In the nucleus, FUS-DDIT3 complexes with YAP1/TEAD. FUS-DDIT3 and YAP1
  co-regulate oncogenic gene expression programs affecting proliferation, cell cycle
  progression, apoptosis, and adipogenesis.
