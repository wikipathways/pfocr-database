caption: (A) Summary of the molecular dynamics of Fas signaling in the DG upon optogenetic
  Fas activation. Transient Fas activation in immature neurons of the DG induces activation
  of the mTOR pathway in these cells. Prolonged activation results increases pErk
  in neural stem cells through BDNF secreted from Fas-activated immature neurons.
  NSC, neural stem cell; IN, immature neuron; MN, mature neuron. (B) Summary of the
  histological and behavioral outcomes observed following repetitive activation of
  Fas signaling in the DG. Repetitive activation of the Fas–mTOR (immature neuron)–BDNF
  VI–pErk (neural stem cell) paracrine pathway in the DG induces the proliferation
  of neural stem cells and transiently increases spatial working memory.
