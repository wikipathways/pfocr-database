caption: 'A schematic diagram of ERS regulating autophagy through four pathways. ERS
  primarily regulates autophagy through four pathways: PERK, ATF6, and Ca2+, with
  the central regulator being CHOP. Upon activation of ERS, IRE1α interacts with Beclin1
  via the TRAF2 and JNK pathway to enhance autophagy. ATF6 initiates the activation
  of DAPK, which in turn triggers autophagy via Beclin1. All three UPR pathways possess
  the capacity to modulate autophagy through the induction of CHOP protein expression.
  Furthermore, Ca2+ can also participate in the regulation of autophagy by engaging
  PKC and CAMKK. This figure is created with BioRender.com. Abbreviations used: ATF6,
  activating transcription factor 6; ERS, endoplasmic reticulum stress; IRE1, inositol-requiring
  enzyme 1; PERK, protein kinase-like ER kinase'
