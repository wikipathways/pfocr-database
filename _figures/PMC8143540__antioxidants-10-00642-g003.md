caption: Role of ROS in apoptosis. Toxic ROS levels damage the mitochondrial membrane
  releasing cytochrome c to the cytoplasm which forms a complex with Apaf-1 and pro-caspase
  9. This induces the cleavage of caspase-3 and -7 resulting in apoptosis. Additionally,
  binding of TNFα ligand to TNFR1 death receptor triggers the activation of caspase
  8 leading to cleavage of caspase 3. Caspase 8 activation also cleaves Bcl-1 protein
  Bid to form tBid which further leads to the release of cytochrome c in the intrinsic
  apoptotic pathway.
