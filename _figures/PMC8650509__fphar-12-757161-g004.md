caption: Activation of the NADPH oxidase family members. Several components and domains
  make up the transmembrane-active enzyme complexes of NADPH oxidase isoforms. NOX1-5
  and DUOX1/DUOX2 are shown. Upon activation, an electron will be transferred from
  NADPH to O2 to form superoxide. NOX4-generated superoxide undergoes rapid conversion
  into hydrogen peroxide, which mediates many of its downstream effects. NOX5 and
  the DUOX enzymes are sensitive to cellular Ca2+ concentrations.
