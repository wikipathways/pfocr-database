caption: Molecular signaling pathway underlying NETs formation induced by different
  stimulus. PMA binds to PKC on the cell membrane to activate the Raf-MEK-ERK signaling
  pathway cascade and NOX, resulting in the production of ROS. ROS act as initiators
  of the azurosome to liberate NE from the protein complex composed of MPO, NE, and
  cathepsin G, among other granular proteins. After NE translocation to the nucleus,
  the core histones are proteolyzed, resulting in decondensation of the chromatin.
  Calcium ionophores can activate PAD4 directly and induce NETs release without ROS
  generation. III. After activated by LPS, GPIb on the surface of platelets can bind
  to CD18 on neutrophils, then activate Src kinase-PI3K-ERK pathway, resulting in
  proliferation.CD18, β2-integrin; GPIb, glycoprotein Ib; LPS, lipopolysaccharide;
  MPO, myeloperoxidase; NE, neutrophil elastase; NETs, neutrophil extracellular traps;
  NOX, NADPH oxidase; PKC, protein kinase C; PMA, phorbol 12-myristate 13-acetate;
  ROS, reactive oxygen species.
