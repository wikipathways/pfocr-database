caption: Wnts bind to their receptors Fzd/Lrp5/6, recruit Dvl and block the destruction
  complex from degrading cytosolic β‐catenin. Stabilized β‐catenin translocates to
  the nucleus where it interacts with LEF/TCF to activate Wnt target genes. Wnt antagonists
  such as Dkk, sFRPs, WIF1 and sclerostin block Wnt signalling by binding and blocking
  Wnt ligands or their receptors. Norrin and R‐spondins promote Wnt activity by binding
  or stabilizing membrane availability of Fzd/Lrp5/6. Pygo promotes Wnt signalling
  by activating transcription via interactions with the complex of proteins bound
  to the LEF/TCF transcription factors.
