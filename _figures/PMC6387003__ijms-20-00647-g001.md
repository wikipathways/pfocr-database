caption: Schematic illustration of myostatin signaling pathway in sarcopenia. Signaling
  activated by insulin positively regulates muscle mass, downstream of protein kinase
  Akt and mTOR. Myostatin first binds to the activin receptor (ActRIIB)/Alk 4/5 on
  skeletal muscle causing phosphorylation of Smad2 and Smad3, and the recruitment
  of Smad4 into a Smad complex, which leads to muscle atrophy. Second, Smad 2/3/4
  complex down-regulate the activity of Akt, thereby inhibiting protein synthesis.
  Akt blocks FOXO1 nuclear translocation to inhibit protein degradation.
