caption: A representative cascade of the TGF-β pathway. TGF-β1~3, TGF-β receptors
  (I & II), Smads, and transcriptional factor (TF) constitute the cascade. TGF-β ligands
  (including TGF-β1~3, activins, BMPs, etc.) with the assistance of accessory receptor
  (betaglycan) activate the formation of heterotetrameric complexes of type I and
  II serine/threonine kinase receptors which in turn activate the intracellular effectors
  of TGF-β signaling, Smad2/3 by phosphorylation. The action recruits Smad4 to the
  complex and translocate into the cellular nucleus where Smad/2/3/4, working together
  with a cellular context-specific transcriptional factor (TF), bind to DNA and trigger
  the expression of a battery of genes including collagens, fibronectins, laminins,
  etc. Eventually, the TGF-β signaling pathway leads to fibrotic responses.
