caption: Dph1 to Dph5 are required for the biosynthesis of diphthamide. Dph1 to Dph4
  are involved in the first step, the transfer of 3-amino-3-carboxypropyl to the His715
  (His699 in yeast) of eEF-2. Dph5 acts as methyl transferase in the next step, yielding
  diphthine. Amidation of diphthine is the last step of diphthamide biosynthesis.
  Arrowhead (N-1 of the histidine imidazole ring of diphthamide) indicates the site
  of ADP-ribosylation by ADP-ribosylating toxins. AdoMet, S-adenosyl methionine; Ado-S-Me,
  methylthioadenosine; Ado-Hcy, S-adenosylhomocysteine. In ADP-ribosyl diphthamide,
  A, adenine moiety; R, ribosyl moiety. The CHO cell mutants lacking corresponding
  functional Dph proteins used in this study are shown in parentheses.
