caption: PI 3-kinase/AKT-dependent pathways involved in IL-1 induction of  IL-6. The
  current model for the binding of IL-1 to its receptor,  IL-1R1, and co-receptor,
  IL-1 receptor accessory protein IL1-RAcP  (). Formation of the  signaling module
  containing MyD88, phosphorylated interleukin-1  receptor-associated kinase (IRAK),
  and TRAF6 (TNF receptor associated  factor), is essential for PI 3-kinase recruitment
  and AKT activation  (,  ,  ,  ). The kinase TAK1  (TGFβ-activated kinase) activates
  the NIK-IκB-NFκB pathway  and the MAP kinase cascade (MKK4/7 to JNK/AP-1 activation
  as well as the  MKK3/6 to p38 activation (not shown)  (,  ,  –).  Possible negative
  regulation in the Caco-2 cell line between AKT and JNK  pathways is shown as a broken
  line  (,  ). We have identified 2  separate PI-3 kinase-dependent pathways from
  the IL1-R1 complex to the  activation of IL-6 gene transcription. 1, a novel pathway  leading
  to AP-1-dependent induction of IL-6 via IKKαT23.  2, an NFκB-dependent pathway,
  which indirectly induces  IL-6, likely by inducing factors such as AP-1 family members,
  which  are necessary for IL-6 gene transcription in response to IL-1.
