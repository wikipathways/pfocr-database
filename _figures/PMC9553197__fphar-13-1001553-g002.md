caption: The main affected targets and pathways in anticancer effects of BV. In cancer,
  BV mainly affects the PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway (e.g., PI3K, Akt, and mTOR), apoptosis
  signaling pathway (e.g., EGFR and TNF-α, including downstream effectors such as
  Casp-3, Casp-7, Casp-8, Casp-9, Bcl-2, Bax and Bcl-xL), p38 MAPK pathway, and thus
  affect the growth, differentiation, invasion, autophagy or migration of cancer cells
  in lung, breast, cervical and other cancers. Green arrows or red cut-off lines represent
  the “promote” or “inhibit” effect of the target (gene or protein) by the upstream
  target factor, respectively. Bee Venom, known as BV, is dispersed on the surface
  of a phospholipid bilayer. The text shows the direct or indirect targets of BV.
