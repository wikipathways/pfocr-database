caption: 'Proximal B-cell receptor-mediated signalling pathways. After binding to
  antigen, the immunoglobulin (Ig)α and Igβ cytoplasmic tails are phosphorylated on
  the immunoreceptor tyrosine-based activation motif (ITAM) tyrosines by Src-family
  tyrosine kinases (SFTKs) and/or Syk. Syk then binds to the Igα ITAM, and the B-cell
  linker protein (BLNK) binds to tyrosine 204 of Igα. This activates multiple signalling
  pathways, including: Btk, which activates phospholipase C (PLC)γ2 and leads to calcium
  flux (blue) and protein kinase C (PKC) activation (green); Grb2, which activates
  the Ras/Raf/mitogen-activated protein kinase (MEK) extracellular signal-regulated
  kinase (ERK) pathway (green); and Vav, which activates the Rac/Rho/Cdc42 pathway
  and results both in cytoskeletal rearrangement (maroon) and c-Jun N-terminal protein
  kinase (JNK) activation (green). The SFTKs themselves activate nuclear factor-κB
  (NF-κB) (green).'
