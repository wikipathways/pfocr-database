caption: The protein C activation by the thrombin-thrombomodulin complex and the cytoprotective
  function of Activated Protein C (APC) in ischemic heart disease and heart failure.
  The Endothelial Protein C Receptor (EPCR)-dependent cleavage of Protease-Activated
  Receptor 1 (PAR1) by APC initiates β-arrestin-2 biased signaling that results in
  the activation of Rac1 GTPase, Akt, and AMPK by the Ca2+/calmodulin-dependent protein
  kinase-kinase β (CaMKKβ) pathway. The PAR1-biased signaling also inhibits the activation/nuclear
  translocation of NF-κB and mTORC1 signaling network. E1, E2… represent EGF-like
  domains. See the text for more details. The figure was prepared by software provided
