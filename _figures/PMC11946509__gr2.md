caption: The biosynthetic pathway of ergothioneine in eukaryotes.Ergothioneine biosynthesis
  in eukaryotes involves two key enzymes, Egt1 and Egt2. Egt1, with methyltransferase
  and 5-histidylcysteine sulfoxide synthase activities, catalyzes the conversion of
  deacetylcysteine to its sulfoxide and methylates either cysteine or histidine, resulting
  the formation of deacetylcysteine. Egt2, a hercynylcysteine S-oxide lyase, completes
  the process by forming ergothioneine after C–S bond formation
