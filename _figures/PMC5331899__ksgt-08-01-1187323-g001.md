caption: 'Schematic comparison of different oncogenic Ras signaling in RasMUT and
  RasGRP1-overexpressing T-ALL. Left: Leukemic cells with constitutively active Ras
  display high net levels of active RasGTP and very low nucleotide exchange rates
  due to the defective GTPase function of Ras. Middle: RasGRP1-overexpressing T-ALL
  show high levels of flux through the RasGDP/GTP cycle, yet low net levels of RasGTP,
  presumably due to the high compensatory activity of RasGAP family members. Right:
  After cytokine activation, RasGRP1-overexpressing T-ALL reveal elevated RasGTP levels
  and active downstream signaling, primarily through the PI3K-Akt pathway.'
