caption: Schematic representation of ITIM-mediated signaling function of IDO. Tyrosine-phosphorylated
  IDO ITIMs (P) act as docking sites for tyrosine phosphatases (SHPs) and SOCS3. Association
  of IL-6–induced SOCS3 promotes IDO proteasomal degradation. TGF-β, by inducing SHPs
  and activating the tyrosine kinase Fyn that phosphorylates IDO ITIM domains, promotes
  the association of IDO with SHPs. IDO/SHPs complexes, via inhibition of IRAK1, activate
  the noncanonical NF-κB pathway (p52/RelB), which in turn induces the production
  of type I IFNs (IFNα/β) and TGF-β, which synergize with noncanonical NF-κB in upregulating
  Ido1 expression.
