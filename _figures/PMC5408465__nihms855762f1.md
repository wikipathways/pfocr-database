caption: The role of calmodulin (CaM) with phosphorylated tyrosine 99 (pTyr99) in
  KRAS-driven adenocarcinoma. In physiological cell growth, receptor tyrosine kinase
  (RTK) signal activates phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K) through binding of the
  RTK’s pYXXM motif to the n/cSH2 domains of the p85 subunit of PI3Kα. In KRAS-driven
  adenocarcinomas, CaM with pTyr99 acts by fully activating PI3Kα through binding
  of the pTyr99 to the two SH2 domains of PI3Kα. The CaM’s pTyr99 binding site acts
  as the RTK’s pYXXM motif, substituting the missing RTK signal, thus stimulating
  the PI3Kα/Akt/mTOR pathway.
