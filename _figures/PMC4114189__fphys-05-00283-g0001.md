caption: Diagram of the molecular pathway of the mechano-induction of tenascin-C.
  Mechanical strain activates RhoA in fibroblasts, depending on fibronectin, integrin
  β 1 and integrin-linked kinase (ILK), which causes the reduction of monomeric G-actin
  by inducing actin assembly and stress fiber formation. Depletion of the G-actin
  pool frees MAL/myocardin-related transcription factor-A (MRTF-A)/megakaryoblastic
  leukemia-1 (MKL1) to enter the nucleus, which induces TNC expression partly depending
  on serum response factor (SRF). Meanwhile, TNC binds fibronectin at the syndecan-4
  binding sites and interferes with fibronectin-mediated RhoA activation, and finally
  suppresses TNC transcription. Adapted from Asparuhova et al. (), Imanaka-Yoshida
  et al. ().
