caption: The microbiota increases host cell genomic damage and suppresses genome repair.
  Primary bile acids, nitrate, and proteins metabolized by certain microbial communities
  produce substances such as DCA, N-nitrosamines, and heterocyclic amines, leading
  to DNA damage. F. nucleatum increases the expression of miR-205-5p through the TLR4
  and MyD88-dependent innate immune signaling pathway, suppressing the expression
  of MLH1, MSH2, and MSH6. H. pylori upregulates miR-150-5p, miR-155-5p, and miR-3163
  to suppress the expression of MSH2 and MSH3 proteins
