caption: p53 is activated in response to type I IFN signalling due to the presence
  of IFN-stimulated response elements (ISRE) within the p53 promoter sequence. Activation
  of p53 leads to the upregulation of genes that can promote cell cycle arrest, apoptosis
  and intracellular immunity. Several p53 target genes are in turn involved in driving
  IFN production and signalling, including TLR3, IRF5, ISG15 and IRF9. This provides
  a positive feedback loop on the type I IFN pathway with important implications in
  immune responses, in particular against viral infections.
