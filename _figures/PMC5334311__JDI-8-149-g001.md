caption: Schematic representation of the compensatory β‐cell proliferative signaling
  network mediated by insulin resistance. Insulin resistance stimulates β‐cell replication
  through glucose, insulin or other factors, including circulating molecules secreted
  by other tissues. Glucose‐induced β‐cell replication is dependent on insulin receptor
  substrate‐2 (IRS‐2). The role of insulin/insulin receptor signaling on the ability
  of β‐cells to replicate in response to insulin resistance remains controversial.
  Activation of protein kinase Cζ (PKCζ) requires phosphoinositide 3‐kinase (PI3K)
  activity; however, the precise mechanism remains unclear. Mechanistic targets of
  rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1) activity has also been associated with adenosine monophosphate‐activated
  protein kinase (AMPK), Akt, glycogen synthase kinase‐3β (GSK‐3β) and feedback signaling
  of p70S6K. The potential role of PKCζ in the induction of the mTORC1 pathway and
  cyclin D2 expression requires further investigation. 4EBP1, eukaryotic translation
  initiation factor 4E‐binding protein 1; CREB, cyclic adenosine monophosphate response
  element binding protein; ERK, extracellular signal‐regulated kinases; NFAT, nuclear
  factor of activated T‐cells; p70S6K, ribosomal protein S6 kinase beta‐1; Tsc2/Tsc1,
  tuberous sclerosis complex 2/1.
