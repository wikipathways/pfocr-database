caption: 'Insulin signaling. Insulin signaling in endothelial cells occurs after insulin
  binding to the insulin receptor. This causes activation of two separate and parallel
  pathways: (i) PI3K–Akt and (ii) Ras/Raf/MAP kinase. (i) Akt kinase phosphorylates
  eNOS at S1177, resulting in increased NO production and vasodilation. (ii) The MAP
  kinase pathway results in endothelin-1 production and vasoconstriction. In other
  tissues, these pathways have effects (shown in dark blue lettering). In skeletal
  muscle (left), the PI3K–Akt pathway results in translocation of GLUT4 and glucose
  uptake. In vascular smooth muscle (right), the MAP kinase pathway results in growth
  and mitogenesis. Abbreviations: eNOS, endothelial nitric oxide synthase; ET-1, endothelin-1;
  GLUT4, insulin-dependent glucose transporter 4''; MAP kinase, mitogen-activated
  protein kinase; PI3K, phosphatidylinositol-3-kinase.'
