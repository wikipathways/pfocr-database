caption: T cell senescence‐related signaling pathways. Hypoxia‐driven cAMP can be
  transferred to T cells, inducing DNA damage via PKA–CREB pathway activation. Glucose
  competition could trigger ATM‐related DNA damage, induce ERK1/2 and P38 pathways,
  and interacts with STAT1/3, leading to T‐cell senescence. The downregulation of
  TCR signaling can trigger the activation of P38 pathway while inhibiting the PI3K‐AKT–mTOR
  signaling pathway. This leads to the inactivation of autophagy and the induction
  of mitochondrial dysfunction in senescent T cells. The cGAS–STING pathway also modulates
  the secretion of SASP factors. Treg cell, regulatory T cell; TCR, T cell receptor;
  SASP, senescence‐associated secretory phenotype
