caption: DSeA first indues intracellular ROS overproduction in a rapid time. The accumulation
  of intracellular ROS induces DNA damage-mediated p53 activation and dephosphorlation
  of Akt, which is negative regulated by PTEN. Inactivation of Akt and phosphorylation
  of p53 co-regulate the expression of Bcl-2 family proteins, resulting in depletion
  of Δψm and release of apoptogenic factors from the mitochondria into cytosol. Furthermore,
  the activation of p53 increases the expression of death receptors, which accompany
  with mitochondrial pathway to promote TRAIL-induced apoptosis through activation
  of caspase cascade and PARP cleavage.
