caption: (A) Illustration of the transcellular phosphate absorption pathway. The sodium-dependent
  phosphate cotransporter 2b (NaPi2b) is responsible for transcellular phosphate absorption.
  This phosphate transporter saturates at phosphate concentrations well below those
  associated with conventional Western diets., There is evidence that NaPi2b plays
  a larger role in intestinal phosphate absorption when luminal phosphate concentrations
  are low, which is likely to occur during dietary privation. (B) Illustration of
  the paracellular phosphate absorption pathway,, , , , ,  Paracellular phosphate
  absorption is characterized by passive diffusion along concentration gradients through
  tight junction complexes of claudins and occludins between cell membranes. The paracellular
  route does not saturate and is the dominant intestinal phosphate absorption pathway.,
  (C) Illustration of the paracellular phosphate absorption pathway with tenapanor.
  Tenapanor blocks paracellular absorption of phosphate in the GI tract by local inhibition
  of the sodium/hydrogen exchanger isoform 3 (NHE3). NHE3 inhibition directly reduces
  sodium absorption, leading to modest intracellular proton retention that is proposed
  to induce conformational changes in tight junction proteins. These changes directly
  reduce permeability specific to phosphate through the paracellular pathway.
