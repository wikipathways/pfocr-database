caption: Tissue-specific insulin signaling. The insulin receptor is autophosphorylated
  on multiple tyrosine residues, allowing the docking and activation of multiple signaling
  molecules, most notably insulin receptor substrate (IRS) proteins. This in turn
  activates phosphatidylinositol-3-kinase (PI3K) and Akt to mediate the increases
  in glucose uptake and metabolism as well as changes in protein and lipid metabolism.
  While the general pathway is similar in all tissues, the final biological effects
  are specialized to the roles of insulin in muscle (a), liver (b), and adipose tissue
  (c).
