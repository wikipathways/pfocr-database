caption: Intracellular peptidoglycan (PGN) is detected by intracellular receptors
  and activates the NF-κB signaling pathway in Drosophila melanogaster and human enterocytes.
  Intracellular PGN is detected by the PGRP-LE receptor in Drosophila melanogaster
  (A) or by NOD receptors in human (B). PGN-receptor interaction triggers the NF-κB
  signaling cascade (Imd pathway in Drosophila), which results in the production of
  antimicrobial peptides (AMPs). The orthologous proteins are represented by the same
  shape, color and position in the pathways.
