caption: Mitogenic signals are transduced by Ras which inhibits the retinoblastoma
  (Rb) protein allowing E2F and Myc to promote cell cycle progression. p16INK4a is
  a tumour suppressor whose mutation permits cyclin D dependent kinase (CDK) to inhibit
  Rb. High levels of E2F or Myc activates p14ARF and thereby p53 which has multiple
  outputs. A series of proapoptotic bcl-2 family members are stimulated and via PTEN
  the antiapoptotic actions of AKT are inhibited. Note that p53 can still respond
  to DNA damage, and therefore chemotherapy, if the Ras/Rb pathway is disabled.
