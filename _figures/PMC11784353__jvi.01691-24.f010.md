caption: Schematic diagrams illustrate how EIAV negatively regulates RLR pathway activation
  by degrading MAVS. Virus infection results in activation of the RLR pathway and
  production of type I IFN. To escape such innate immunity of the host, in the late
  period of infection, EIAV core structure protein Gag binds to MAVS to recruit E3
  ubiquitin ligase Smurf1, which can catalyze their K48-linked polyubiquitination
  for proteasome-dependent degradation. All above processes lead to the inhibition
  of the RLR signaling pathway and downregulation of IFN-β
