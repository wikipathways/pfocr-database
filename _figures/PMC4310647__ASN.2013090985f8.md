caption: Working model of GIV’s role in early podocyte injury in PAN nephrosis. (A)
  In normal podocytes, Akt survival signaling is mediated through the well defined
  nephrin-dependent PI3K/Akt pathway. (B) At early time points (7 days) after PAN
  injury, increased GIV expression coincides with decreased nephrin expression and
  activation of VEGFR2 and Akt. GIV binds to activated VEGFR2 and is tyrosine phosphorylated
  by VEGFR2. Activated GIV binds and activates Gαi3 (Gαi3-GTP). A VEGFR2/GIV/Gαi3
  signaling complex is assembled and prolongs podocyte survival in PAN via activation
  of PI3K, Akt, mTORC1, and mTORC2. Thus, after PA injury when nephrin levels decrease,
  GIV takes over the task of enhancing Akt/mTOR survival signaling in response to
  VEGF.
