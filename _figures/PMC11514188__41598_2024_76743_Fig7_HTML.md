caption: Proanthocyanidins regulate ovarian oxidative stress in mice with POF through
  the SESTRIN2-NRF2 pathway. 3-NPA induces excessive accumulation of ROS, which impairs
  mitochondrial function and eventually leads to POF. On the one hand, PCs directly
  remove ROS; on the other hand, they activate SESTRIN2 and then promote NRF2 and
  Keap1 separation and entry into the nucleus. In the nucleus, NRF2 promotes the expression
  of a variety of cytoprotective genes, which in turn cleans excessive ROS, protects
  mitochondrial function, and protects ovarian function
