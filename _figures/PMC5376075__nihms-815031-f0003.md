caption: The TRL2 pathway is activated by Tollip and results in IRAK suppression.
  Upon stimulation with IL-1, the Tollip-IRAK-1 complex is recruited to the IL-1 receptor
  complex causing IRAK-1 phosphorylation, dissociation from Tollip and TRAF6 activation.
  Tollip overexpression leads to the suppression of NF-kβ inhibition. Negative regulation
  of TLR signaling by Tollip may therefore serve to limit the production of pro-inflammatory
  mediators during the inflammation process following an infection.
