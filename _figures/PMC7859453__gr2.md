caption: The MST activation center is the Hippo path. This pathway is stimulated through
  signals from the NF2/Expanded/KIBRA complex to SA or through the interaction of
  RAS with RASSF. Activation of MST results in activation of LATS kinase and phosphorylation
  of YAP, which prevents association with oncogenic TEAD growth-promoting transcription
  factors. In addition to increasing cytoplasmic levels, YAP phosphorylation decreases
  the transcription factor association under tumor suppression and differentiation
  conditions. Increased MST activity inhibits MEK activation and also, LATS inhibits
  RAF activity through S259 phosphorylation and silencing of RAF.
