caption: 'Figure 2. Concept of the link between choline metabolism and intracellular
  signaling. A proposed concept of how EDI3 links choline metabolism to altered signaling
  network activities. Choline is further metabolized to the major membrane lipid PtdC.
  Tumor cells have a higher demand for this lipid to facilitate restructuring of the
  cell membrane for division and migration. G3P fuels the G3P-LPA-PA-DAG pathway which
  creates anchoring points for GTPases and PKC and activates intracellular signaling
  (from Marchan et al., 2012). Abbreviations: CDP-Cho, cytidine 5′-diphosphocholine;
  CHK, choline kinase; CT, CDP, phosphocholine cytidyltransferase; DAG, diacylglycerol;
  DAGK, DAG kinase; G3P, glycerol-3-phosphate; LPL, lysophospholipase; GPAT, G3P acyltransferase;
  LPAAT, LPA acyltransferase; PAP, PA phosphatase; PCT, diacylglycerol choline phosphotransferase;
  PLA, phospholipase A; PLB, phospholipase B; PLC, phospholipase C; PLD, phospholipase
  D; PtdC, phosphatidylcholine (from Marchan et al., 2012).'
