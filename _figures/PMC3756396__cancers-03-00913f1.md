caption: A simplified scheme of the possible involvement of cAMP/PKA pathway in cancer.
  Adenylyl cyclase (AC) may be activated in several ways, including G-protein-coupled
  receptors (GPCR), leading to the formation of cAMP, that binds to the regulatory
  (R) subunits of PKA, thus releasing the catalytic subunits (C). In turn, these phosphorylate
  several target proteins, leading to apoptosis, arrest of cell growth and block of
  the cell motility.
