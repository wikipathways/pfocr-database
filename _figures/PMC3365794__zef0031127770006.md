caption: Model depicting COUP-TFII functions in tumor angiogenesis. In endothelial
  cells, the absence of COUP-TFII results in the elevated expression of VEGFR-1, a
  decoy receptor that sequesters VEGF ligand. This leads to attenuation of VEGFR-2
  signaling and endothelial cell sprouting in the COUP-TFII-deficient mice. Additionally,
  endothelial cell proliferation is impaired due to decreased expression of E2F1.
  In pericytes, the absence of COUP-TFII results in reduced Ang-1 that serves as a
  paracrine signal to activate Tie2 receptors on the endothelial cells. Thus, COUP-TFII
  regulates angiogenesis through growth signaling, VEGF/VEGFR-2, and Ang-1/Tie2 signaling
  pathway within tumor microenvironment.
