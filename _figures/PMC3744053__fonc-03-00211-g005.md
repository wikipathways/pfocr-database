caption: Signaling pathways of anti-permeability factors. The most relevant anti-permeability
  factors are angiopoietin-1 (Ang1), sphingosine-1-phosphate (S1P), and fibroblast
  growth factor (FGF). Ang1 activation of its cognate receptor, Tie2, elicits a signaling
  pathway and promotes Src sequestration; thus hindering VEGF-A signaling and VE-cadherin
  internalization. S1P signaling maintains vascular integrity by modulating VE-cadherin
  internalization, cytoskeletal rearrangement, barrier enhancement and integrity,
  through its cognate receptor S1P1R. FGF maintains the integrity of the VE-cadherin/p120-catenin
  complex, thus stabilizing VE-cadherin at the membrane.
