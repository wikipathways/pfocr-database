caption: 'Hsp90 is involved in cancer development in pathways critical for the cell
  growth, invasiveness, and survival. Hsp90 regulates the hypoxia-inducible factor
  (HIF)-1α activity [] (A), and together with chaperone p23, binds the human telomerase
  reverse transcriptase (hTERT) [] (B), causing evasion from apoptosis and metastasis.
  (C) The components of the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway are Hsp90 clients, indicating the
  chaperone’s role in the regulation of autophagy. Therefore, autophagy has a pro-survival
  function [,]. (D) The tumor necrosis factor receptor-associated protein 1 (TRAP1)
  is involved in TCs: it is thought to enhance ERK phosphorylation and cell cycle
  progression, leading to a dampening of apoptosis by activation of the RAS/RAF/ERK
  pathway [].'
