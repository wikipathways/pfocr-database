caption: 'Simplified scenario model of the necroptotic pathway. Necroptosis can be
  triggered by engagement of TNF to TNF receptor superfamily (e.g., TNFR1), Toll-like
  receptors (e.g., TLR3 and TLR4), or the DNA receptor DAI. In TNF-induced necroptosis,
  TNF binds to the TNF receptors and engages a complex formation, which includes TRAF2,
  cIAP, and RIPK1. RIPK1 recruits and activates RIPK3 that oligomerizes resulting
  in the formation of the necrosome. RIPK3 phosphorylates MLKL which functions as
  a necroptosis inducer by causing plasma membrane rupture. The concise mechanisms
  of plasma membrane rupture have yet to be defined. In parallel, phosphorylated MLKL
  translocates to the nucleus and results in transcription of CXCL1, IL-33, and IL-1β
  which are actively secreted. Engagement of the necrosome can also be a consequence
  of TLR signalling through TRIF/RIPK3 or DAI signalling through DAI/RIPK3. cIAPs
  cellular inhibitor of apoptosis protein, DAI DNA-dependent activator of IFN regulatory
  factors, dsRNA double-stranded RNA, HMGB1 high mobility group box 1, IL interleukin,
  MLKL mixed lineage kinase domain-like protein, RIPK1/3 receptor-interacting serine/threonine-protein
  kinase 1/3, TLR Toll-like receptor, TNF tumor necrosis factor, TNFR1 tumor necrosis
  factor receptor 1, TRADD tumor necrosis factor receptor type 1-associated death
  domain, TRAF2 tumor necrosis factor-receptor-associated factor 2, TRIF Toll/IL-1(TIR)
  domain-containing adaptor-inducing interferon-β. Sources: Refs. [, , –]'
