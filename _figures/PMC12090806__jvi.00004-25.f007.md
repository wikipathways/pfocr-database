  TRIM22, and MAVS form a feedback loop in which ELF3 translocates to the nucleus
  following RNA virus infection, leading to the transcriptional regulation of TRIM22
  expression. Subsequently, TRIM22 catalyzes K63-linked polyubiquitination of MAVS,
  activating the RIG-I antiviral signaling pathway and promoting the dissociation
  of MAVS from NLRX1
