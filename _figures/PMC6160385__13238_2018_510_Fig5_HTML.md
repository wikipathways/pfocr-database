caption: Proposed models for the context-dependent roles of β-TRCP in regulating the
  Wnt signaling. (A) Under Wnt off conditions, β-TRCP formed a degradation complex
  to recognize and degrade CKIα/GSK3-mediated phosphorylated form of β-catenin, to
  repress β-catenin targeted genes, and maintain Wnt in silent status. (B) Under Wnt
  on conditions, the activated FZD/LRP5/6 receptor destructs β-catenin degradation
  complex and releases β-catenin downstream targeted genes. Simultaneously, as a feedback
  regulator of Wnt pathway, ZNRF3 degrades FZD to alleviate Wnt signal. Meantime,
  β-TRCP1 could promote the degradation of CKI-mediated phosphorylated form of ZNRF3,
  to sustain the Wnt pathway
