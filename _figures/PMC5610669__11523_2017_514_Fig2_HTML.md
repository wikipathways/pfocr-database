caption: IGF-1R downstream signaling. Ligand binding to IGF-1R or IGF-1R/INSR hybrid
  receptors leads to phosphorylation of tyrosines that create binding sites for docking
  proteins including IRS 1–6 and Shc. Recruitment of IRS and Shc activates signaling
  via the PI3K/Akt and Ras/Raf/MAPK pathways, which regulate cellular proliferation,
  survival, migration, and metabolism. In addition to these pathways, interactions
  between IGF-1R and integrins, via scaffolding with RACK1 and FAK proteins, regulate
  cellular adhesion and motility []. Blue arrows indicate activation. Red arrows indicate
  inhibition. P indicates major sites of phosphorylation. Akt protein kinase B, ERK
  extracellular-signal-regulated kinase, IGF-1R type 1 insulin-like growth factor
  receptor, INSR insulin receptor, IRS INSR substrate, MAPK mitogen-activated protein
  kinase, MEK mitogen-activated protein kinase/Erk kinase, mTORC1 mammalian target
  of rapamycin complex 1, PI3K phosphatidylinositol-3-kinase, PTEN phosphatase and
  tensin homolog, Shc Src homology and collagen domain protein
