caption: Activated CD47 is a proximate regulator of multiple cell survival and cell
  death pathways. CD47 is a potent and redundant inhibitor of the canonical nitric
  oxide (NO) pathway, limiting endothelial NO synthase (eNOS) activation (), soluble
  guanylyl cyclase (sGC) stimulation (), and the downstream cGMP target PKG (). Thrombospondin-1
  (TSP1) activation of CD47 decreases VEGF signaling by altering CD47 and VEGF receptor
  (VEGFR2) coassociation (), to limit both NO-dependent and -independent effects of
  VEGF. TSP1, presumably via CD47, inhibits adenylyl cyclase to limit cAMP production
  (). CD47 also limits mitochondrial energetics and promotes reactive oxygen species
  (ROS) and programmed cell death ().
