caption: The modified Fearson and Volgenstein Model. Colorectal cancer (CRC) progression
  can occur through either the chromosomal instability (CIN) or microsatellite instability
  (MSI) pathway. Early adenomatous changes are secondary to loss of APC. KRAS loss
  initiates the formation of larger adenomas in the CIN pathway followed by 18qLOH.
  Mutations in TP53 are a late change. Sporadic MSI tumors are commonly part of the
  serrated neoplasia pathway and BRAF mutations are more common finding.
