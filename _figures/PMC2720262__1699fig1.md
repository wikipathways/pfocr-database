caption: Summary of consequences on signal transduction and brain architecture of
  conditional Pten or Pdk1 deletion, alone or in combination. (A) Under normal levels
  of PI3K/Akt/mTOR pathway activation, PTEN and PDK1 activities are in balance. (B)
  Conditional deletion of Pten with nearly complete activity in the hippocampus “hp-cre”
  results in hyperactivation of Akt/mTOR signaling with macrocephaly, increased neuronal
  cell size, and cerebellar neuronal migration defect. (C) Conditional deletion of
  PDK1 suppresses Akt/mTOR signaling and causes microcephaly. Up-regulation of PI3K
  via derepression of mTORC1 is detected in glia but not in neurons. (D) Pdk1 deletion
  rescues Pten deletion-mediated macrocephaly and prevents Pten deletion-mediated
  increase in neuronal cell size. However, Pdk1 deletion cannot rescue the effect
  on abnormal neuronal migration.
