caption: Mitochondrial antiviral response. The recognition of viral nucleic acids
  involves mitochondria and intact membrane potential (ψm). RIG-I and MDA5 recognize
  cytoplasmic viral nucleic acids, leading to the oligomerization of MAVS. MAVS then
  sets in motion a signaling pathway that eventually leads to the phosphorylation
  (*P) of IRF3/7 with subsequent induction of IFNα to offer antiviral cellular protection.
  RIG-I = retinoic acid inducible gene I, MDA5 = melanoma differentiation-associated
  protein 5, MAVS = mitochondrial antiviral-signaling protein, TRAFs = TNF receptor
  associated factors, TBK1 = TANK binding kinase 1, IKKε = inhibitor of nuclear factor
  kappa-B kinase subunit epsilon, IRF 3 = interferon response factor 3, IRF 7 = interferon
  response factor 7, INFα = interferon alpha.
