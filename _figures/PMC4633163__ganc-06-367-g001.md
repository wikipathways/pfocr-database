caption: Upon treatment, BRAF inhibitors have a direct effect on both melanoma cells
  and fibroblasts. In fibroblasts, paradoxical ERK activation leads to activation
  of fibroblasts and secretion of HGF. In melanoma cells, BRAF inhibition leads to
  secretion of TGF-β, deposition of fibronectin and upregulation of integrin signaling.
  Melanoma-derived TGF-β stimulates fibroblasts to secrete NRG1 and deposit more fibronectin.
  Taken together, activated adhesion signaling amplifies receptor tyrosine kinase
  activity in the context of fibroblast-secreted growth factors, ultimately converging
  on the PI3K/AKT pathway.
