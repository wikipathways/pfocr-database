caption: Signaling pathway of (+)-brevipolide H in PC-3 cells. The (+)-brevipolide
  H displays anticancer activity through mitochondrial stress in which the interplay
  between ROS production and increased cytosolic Ca2+ levels takes place. The inhibition
  of both Bcl-2 and Bcl-xL plays a crucial role on mitochondrial dysfunction. Akt/mTOR/p70S6K
  pathway was suppressed followed by the downregulation of cyclin D1 and cyclin E
  in (+)-brevipolide H-induced G1 checkpoint arrest. The cellular stress may ultimately
  induce apoptosis.
