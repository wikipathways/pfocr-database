caption: Activation of Wnt signaling during muscle regeneration. (A) In quiescent
  satellite cells, Wnt signaling (blue line) is inactive; (B, C) Canonical Wnt signaling
  suppresses the Notch pathway, leading the satellite cell to switch from proliferation
  to differentiation. (D) Wnt signaling induces myogenic gene expression via Barx2
  activation, stimulates Follistatin, inhibits myostatin, and translocates the negative
  regulator Setdb1 into the cytoplasm, promoting myoblast development and fusion.
  (E) Finally, Wnt activity returns to low levels. Overall, Wnt/β-catenin signaling
  must be regulated in a timely manner for muscle regeneration to occur.,,-)
