caption: (A) RNA viruses, such as VSV and Sendai virus, and transfected poly(I:C)
  stimulate TRAF3 degradation, likely via RIG-I and MAVS, thereby causing activation
  of noncanonical NF-κB. RIG-I and MAVS also activate the canonical NF-κB and the
  IRF3 pathways. Noncanonical NF-κB may negatively regulate the induction of Ifnb
  gene expression by competing with RelA for binding to the Ifnb promoter and inhibiting
  transcriptionally active histone modification. (B) Noncanonical NF-κB pathway is
  also activated by M-CSF along with macrophage development, which negatively regulates
  Ifnb gene induction by TLR4.
