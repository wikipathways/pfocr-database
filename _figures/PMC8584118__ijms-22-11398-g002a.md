caption: NLRP12 negatively regulates innate immune signaling in a pathogen-dependent
  manner. (A) NLRP12 inhibits IRAK1 and TRAF3/NIK to further inhibit canonical and
  noncanonical NF-κB signaling and the MAPK/ERK inflammatory signaling pathway in
  bone marrow-derived macrophages (BMDMs). (B) NLRP12 participates in the recruitment
  of ASC for the processing of caspase 1 and maturation of IL-1β and IL-18 to positively
  regulate innate host defense during Yersinia pestis and Plasmodium chabaudi. (C)
  NLRP12 associates with TRIM25 to reduce polyubiquitination of RIG-I and inhibits
  the RIG-I-mediated IFN response during VSV infection in bone marrow-derived dendritic
  cells. RIG-, Retinoic acid-inducible gene I; MAVS, Mitochondrial antiviral-signaling
  protein; TBK1, TANK-binding kinase 1.
