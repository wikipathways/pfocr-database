caption: TGFβ stimulates cardiac resident fibroblast-to-myofibroblast transition Stress-induced
  TGFβ release from the latent complex leads to the activation of membrane-bounds
  TGFβ receptor complexes, which induces Smad2/Smad3 and multiple non-Smad signaling
  pathways, including TAK1, MAPK/p38, and Rho-GTPase/Rock for activation of myofibroblast
  specific genes, such as α-SMA, Col-I, Col-III, Postn. Smad2/Smad3 also induce the
  expression of Nox4, which generates ROS to maintain myofibroblast phenotypes. Additionally,
  GPCRs activated by angiotensin or endothelin activate the Rho-GTPase/Rock to promote
  myofibroblast formation. Mechanical force directly activates TRPC6 channel to induce
  fibroblast-to-myofibroblast transition via Ca2+/Calcineurin/NFAT pathway.
