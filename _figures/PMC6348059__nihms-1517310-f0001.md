caption: High level representation of the NF-κB signaling pathway and SC-514 mechanism
  of action. Stimuli IL-1β and TNF-α bind IL-1R and TNF-R, respectively, triggering
  downstream phosphorylation. NF-κB is sequestered in the cytoplasm by IkBα until
  activation of the IKK complex, which consists of three isoforms, IKKα, IKKγ, and
  IKKβ (a). IKKβ phosphorylates IkBα, allowing NF-κB to translocate to the nucleus
  for gene transcription (b) until eventually translocating back to the cytoplasm
  (c). SC-514 is a selective inhibitor of IKKβ action.
