caption: Canonical and non-canonical TGFβ signaling. In the canonical pathway, TGFβ
  ligands bind to serine/threonine kinase type II and type I receptors and phosphorylate
  R-SMADs, which form heteromeric complexes with SMAD4 and translocate into the nucleus
  to regulate gene transcription. The non-canonical pathway generally refers to the
  SMAD-independent pathway such as PI3K-AKT, ERK1/2, p38, and JNK pathways. Recent
  studies have identified an “R-SMAD-dependent but SMAD4-independent” non-canonical
  pathway that regulates miRNA maturation.
