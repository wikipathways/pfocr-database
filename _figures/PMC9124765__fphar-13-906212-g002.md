caption: Main Mechanotransduction Signaling Pathways in keloid. TGF-β/Smad, integrin/FAK,
  YAP/TAZ signaling pathways and Calcium ion signaling are the main mechanotransduction
  signaling pathways that mediate mechanoresponsiveness. TGF-β senses mechanical forces
  and signals through Smads, where Smad2/3 binds to Smad4 and enters the nucleus,
  where they bind DNA and initiate trans-activation of target genes. Integrin signaling
  induces gene expression through FAK/Erk pathway, and also interferes with TGF-β/Smad
  signaling pathway. Mechanical stimulation can promote YAP to nuclear transfer and
  function. The Gq-coupled receptor activated by stretch stimulation can activate
  PLCβ and produce DAG and IP3. IP3 acts on the intracellular calcium pool to release
  Ca2+, resulting in the increase of intracellular free calcium ion concentration
  and the subsequent intracellular reaction of Raf/MEK/Erk pathway.
