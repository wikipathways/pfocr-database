caption: TLR4 promotes neoplasia through activation of the β-catenin pathway. Under
  normal conditions, TLR4 expression is low in the colon crypts, with few proliferating
  cells at their base expressing Lgr5 (green cells) and nuclear β-catenin. The majority
  of the cells along the crypt have β-catenin undergoing proteosomal degradation (membrane
  β-catenin). TLR4 activation results in activation of β-catenin, increased expression
  of Lgr5, and increased proliferation. In a third of CRCs, TLR4 is over-expressed,
  promoting tonic stimulation of cell proliferation and an expansion of cancer cells.
