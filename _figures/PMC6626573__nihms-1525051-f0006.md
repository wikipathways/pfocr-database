  at specific residues by the GSK3β and CK1 kinases. Phosphorylated β-catenin undergoes
  proteosomal degradation, therefore does not translocate to the nucleus. B. In the
  presence of Wnt ligands (on-state), LRP5/6 and Fz, allowing Axin-Dv1 binding and
  the disassembly of the β-catenin degradation complex. β-catenin is released in the
  cytoplasm and translocated to the nucleus. In the nucleus β-catenin binds to TCF/LEF
  and activates several downstream genes. The figure is reproduced from Bello et al
