caption: ER stress is involved in NLRP3 inflammasome activation. ER stress promotes
  expression of IL-1β and NLRP3 via the NF-κB pathway, contributing to NLRP3 inflammasome
  activation []. In response to ER stress, IRE1α reversibly dissociates from the endoplasmic
  reticulum chaperone immunoglobulin-binding protein (BiP) [], induces thioredoxin
  interacting protein (TXNIP) translocation to mitochondria, ROS release, and NLRP3
  association with mitochondria, promoting release or externalization of mitochondrial
  contents, which bind and activate NLRP3, provoking secretion of proinflammatory
  cytokines such as IL-1β and IL-18 []. Protein kinase-(PKR)-like eukaryotic initiation
  factor 2α kinase (PERK) contributes to apoptosis by sustaining the level of C/EBP-homologous
  protein (CHOP) and facilitating the propagation of ROS signals after dissociation
  from BiP [,,], and apoptosis acts as the second signal to activate NLRP3 [].
