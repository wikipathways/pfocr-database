caption: The mechanism for 46, XY DSD as caused by the MAP3K1R186G variant. (A) In
  the wild-type, MAP3K1 tends to be degraded by auto-ubiquitination. (B) In the mutant,
  MAP3K1R186G enhances its binding to RhoA and stability, causing hyperphosphorylation
  of p38 and GSK3β, activating the Wnt4/β-catenin/FOXL2 pathway, and in turn resulting
  in 46, XY DSD.
