caption: 'The involvement of numerous bioactive substances in the TLR-dependent MAPK
  signaling cascade in RA. The TLR receptor attracts (MyD) 88 and other related adaptor
  proteins are activated (ERK1/2, JNK, P38). The MyD88-dependent system controls the
  expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines and other immune-related genes, signaling
  through the MAPK pathway, which in turn leads to the activation of the transcription
  factor AP-1 in RA. ERK: extracellular regulated kinase; IRF: interferon regulatory
  transcription factor; My D88: myeloid differentiation primary response 88; TLRs:
  Toll-like receptors; LPSs: lipopolysaccharide receptor; MAPK: mitogen-activated
  protein kinase; JNK: Jun N-terminal kinases; NF-κB: nuclear factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer
  of activated B cells; miR: microRNA; AP1: activator protein 1; p38: a MAPK.'
