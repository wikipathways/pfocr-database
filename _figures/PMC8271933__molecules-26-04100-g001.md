caption: Summary of the phosphoinositide 3-kinase (PI3K)/protein kinase B (AKT)/mammalian
  target of rapamycin (mTOR) signaling pathway (created with BioRender.com). The PI3K
  pathway is activated when receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs) or G protein-coupled
  receptors (GPCR) bind to growth factors and lead to AKT activation. Upon activation,
  AKT triggers the phosphorylation of mTOR, p70S6 kinase 1 (p70S6K) and eukaryotic
  translation factor 4E-binding protein 1 (4EBP1), resulting in the modulation of
  gene transcription related to cancer aggressiveness. AKT also stimulates cell death
  resistance by upregulating mouse double minute 2 homolog (MDM2) and prosurvival
  proteins such as Bcl2 and X-linked inhibitor of apoptosis protein (XIAP) and downregulating
  Forkhead box O3 (FOXO3) and proapoptotic proteins. mTORC2 phosphorylates AKT and
  induces actin reorganization, resulting in cell motility.
