caption: Type I IFN signaling starts by activation of the IFN-α/β receptor (IFNAR)
  and subsequent activation of the IFNAR1-associated TYK2 and IFNAR2-associated JAK1,
  with consequent recruitment of STAT2. Phosphorylation of STAT2 enables reruitment
  of STAT1 and release of the phosphorylated STAT1-STAT2 heterodimer bound to IRF-9.
  This complex binds to the IFN stimulated response element (ISRE) and in concert
  with recruited transcriptional co-activators such as p300/CBP then drives IFN-stimulated
  gene (ISG) transcription.
