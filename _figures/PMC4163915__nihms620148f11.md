caption: A model is presented whereby serine phosphorylation of IRS-1 serves as a
  mediator of Ang II to inhibit insulin-induced Akt Thr308 phosphorylation. When C9
  cells are stimulated with Ang II, the endogenous AT1R is activated leading to PKC/MMP-dependent
  release of the HB-EGF ligand, which in turn activates the EGFR. Such EGFR transactivation
  leads to activation of the PI3K/ERK1/2/mTOR/S6K-1 pathway, where S6K-1 has an important
  role in the phosphorylation and degradation of IRS-1, which impairs insulin-induced
  PI3K and Akt activation.
