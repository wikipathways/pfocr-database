caption: S-nitrosylation in NF-κB and STAT3 signaling pathways. (A) IKKβ and NF-κB
  subunits (p65 and p50) are S-nitrosylated in the NF-κB signaling pathway. S-nitrosylation
  of IKKβ at Cys179 prevents the phosphorylation of IκB and subsequent proteasomal
  degradation. This results in the inactive NF-κB-IκB complex sequestered in the cytosol.
  Furthermore, S-nitrosylation of NF-κB subunits p65 (Cys38) and p50 (Cys62) inhibits
  their DNA binding, in turn, preventing the transcription of NF-κB target genes including
  a number of pro-inflammatory cytokines. (Red arrows show the impact of S-nitrosylation.)
  (B) In tumor cells, when STAT3 is phosphorylated, it leads to the expression of
  genes related to proliferation and angiogenesis that promote tumor progression.
  However, S-nitrosylation of STAT3 at Cys259 leads to its inactivation by preventing
  phosphorylation. This could lead to improved immunogenicity in the TME. (1 and 2
  represent the site of S-nitrosylation in the pathway).
