caption: Induction of type I IFNs and ISGs by influenza virus. Innate immune cells,
  such as macrophages and lung epithelial cells, produce type I IFNs after sensing
  IAV genomic RNA using various PRRs. In infected and neighboring cells, type I IFN
  signaling activates the JAK-STAT pathway, leading to transcription of ISGs, the
  products of which initiate intracellular antiviral effectors that limit the spread
  of the viruses. IFN, interferon; ISG, IFN-stimulated gene; PRR, pattern recognition
  receptor; JAK, Janus kinase; STAT, signal transducer and activator of transcription;
  TLR, Toll-like receptor; RLR, RIG-I like helicase; NLR, nucleotide-binding domain
  and leucine-rich-repeat-containing protein.
