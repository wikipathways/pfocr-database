caption: Proteolytic processing of amyloid precursor protein. In the nonamyloidogenic
  pathway, transmembrane APP is cleaved by α-secretase followed by γ-secretase generating
  a nontoxic P3 fragment and α C-terminal fragments (α-CTFs), thus preventing the
  generation of toxic Aβ. Alternatively, the amyloidogenic pathway involves sequential
  cleavages of APP by β-secretase followed by γ-secretase complex, thus generating
  toxic Aβ peptides in addition to the β-CTFs and amyloid precursor protein intracellular
  domain (AICD). The accumulation of Aβ peptides promotes oligomerisation and formation
  of insoluble plaques.
