caption: Effect of GPCRs on RTK transactivation. GPCRs for BB and PACAP couple to
  Gq11 and causes metabolism of PIP2 to DAG (activates PKC) and IP3 (elevates cytosolic
  Ca2+). GPCRs for VIP and PACAP interact with Gs activating adenylyl cyclase and
  increasing cAMP. cAMP activates PKA leading to CREB phosphorylation and altered
  gene expression. GPCRs activate Src and MMP, resulting in the production of EGFR
  ligands such as TGF-α. When TGF-α binds to the EGFR, tyrosine kinase activity is
  increased leading to phosphorylated EGFR homodimers or EGFR–HER2 heterodimers. RTK
  activates the Ras–Raf–MEK–ERK pathway, leading to increased cellular proliferation.
  RTK also activates the PI3K–PKD–Akt–mTor pathway, leading to increased cellular
  survival. Phosphorylated EGFR–HER2 is dephosphorylated by protein tyrosine phosphatase
  (PTP), which is impaired by ROS.
