caption: Signaling pathways in fibrotic processes involving macrophages. In the typical
  TGF-β/smad signaling pathway, upon release of TGF-β binds to TGFR2, which recruits
  and activates TGFR1. Active TGFR1 phosphorylates Smad2 and Smad3, complexes with
  Smad4 and translocates to the nucleus. The Smad3 component of the complex binds
  directly to gene promoters and induces molecules such as α-SMA. In addition, Smad3
  affects miRNAs and lncRNAs, influencing epigenetic modifications of DNA and histones
  to encode α-SMA, vimentin and E-cadherin. In the non-Smad signaling pathways, activation
  of the PI3K–AKT–mTOR and JAK–STAT pathways, can affect pro-fibrotic factor gene
  coding. The wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway can contribute to the accumulation of
  β-catenin in the cytoplasm leading to the induction of MMT by nuclear translocation.
  Wnt3a-treated macrophages promote polarization toward M2 macrophages via IL-4 or
  TGF-β, which facilitates the development of fibrosis. Cytokines such as VEGF and
  CTGF bind to the receptors and regulate fibrosis
