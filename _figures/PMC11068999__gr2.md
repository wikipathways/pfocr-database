caption: The Wnt/β-catenin pathway. In the absence of Wnt ligands, the AXIN, GSK-3β,
  and APC degradation complex phosphorylates β-catenin and β-TrCP ubiquitinates β-catenin,
  resulting in proteasome-mediated degradation. A repressive complex (TCF/LEF and
  Groucho) represses target genes in the absence of β-catenin. When Wnt ligands bind
  to the Wnt receptor (FZD) and its co-receptor (LRP), β-catenin is liberated from
  the degradation complex and translocates to the nucleus, where it replaces Groucho
  and forms an active complex with TCF/LEF to activate the target genes. Ginsenosides
  (Rg1, Rg3, Rh2) decrease the protein level of β-catenin
