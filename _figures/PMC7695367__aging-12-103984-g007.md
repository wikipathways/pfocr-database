caption: Schematic illustration of the proposed mechanism of radiation-induced tumor
  repopulation in NSCLC. Radiation-induced DNA double-strand breaks (DSBs) activate
  the DNA damage response (DDR) and caspase-3. Activated caspase-3 regulates the EndoG
  nuclear translocation and thus participates in the DDR by regulating ATM/p53 signaling,
  which activates the Cox-2/PGE2 axis in dying NSCLC cells, consequently enhancing
  the proliferation of living tumor cells.
