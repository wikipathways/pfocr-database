caption: Anandamide as an intracellular messenger inducing Ca2+ influx via TRPV1.
  Stimulation of G-protein-coupled receptors (1) coupled to Gq/11 activates the PLC/IP3pathway
  (2) and results in Ca2+ mobilization from the ER (3). The increase in intracellular
  Ca2+ activates the biosynthetic pathway of anandamide (AEA) (4), which, alone or
  together with concomitant sensitizing stimuli, signals to TRPV1 (5) to gate Ca2+
  entry in the cytoplasm (6). Anandamide action is terminated either by its extrusion
  from the cell or hydrolysis by FAAH (7).
