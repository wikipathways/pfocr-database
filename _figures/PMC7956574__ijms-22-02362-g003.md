caption: Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway in osteogenesis. Wnt ligands interact with
  FZD and activate Wnt signaling pathway. FZD binds Dsh that inhibits GSK3-β activity,
  and thus the phosphorylation of β-catenin. β-catenin translocates into the nucleus,
  where it binds to TCF/LEF and CBP and induces the expression of RUNX2. Interaction
  between Wnt ligands and SFRP-1/2 and the binding of LRP5/6 and Keremen with DKK-1
  antagonist switch off Wnt signaling pathway. In this case, GSK3-β, together with
  Axin, CK and APC, phosphorylate β-catenin, inducing proteasome-mediated degradation.
