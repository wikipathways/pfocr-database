caption: Negative regulation of interferons (IFNs) by autophagy. Upon recognition
  of viral pathogen-associated molecular patterns (PAMPs) by pattern recognition receptors
  (PRRs), several signaling pathways are activated to produce IFNs. This response
  is amplified by the activation of the Janus kinase signal transducer and activator
  of transcription (JAK-STAT) pathway. Some coronaviruses negatively regulate the
  IFNs’ response via the autophagic degradation of key molecules downstream of IFN
  signaling cascade (e.g., IRF3, MAVS, NLRX1, mitochondria, MyD88 and STAT).
