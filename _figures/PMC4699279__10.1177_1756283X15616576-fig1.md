caption: Gliadin peptides pass through the epithelial barrier paracellularly or transcellularly.
  In the transcellular pathway, the gliadin peptides bind to the secretory IgA at
  the apical membrane of intestine. Then transferrin receptor CD71 facilitates the
  delivery of the gliadin peptides to the lamina propria. In the paracellular pathway,
  the gliadin peptides bind to CXCR3. At the same time, zonulin is released and subsequent
  transactivation of EGFR by PAR2 leads to disorganization of the tight junction and
  entrance of gliadin peptides to the lamina propria.APC, antigen presenting cells;
  CXCR3, chemokine CXC motif receptor 3; EGFR, epidermal growth factor receptor; HLA,
  human leukocyte antigen; IgA, immunoglobulin A; IFN-γ, interferon-γ; MYD88, myeloid
  differentiation factor 88; PAR2, protease activated receptor 2; TCR, T-cell receptor;
  TG2, transglutaminase 2; TJ, tight junction; TNF-α, tumour necrosis factor-α.
