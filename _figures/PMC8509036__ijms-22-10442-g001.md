caption: The intrinsic and extrinsic pathways of apoptosis. The two pathways that
  initiate apoptosis are the extrinsic (or death receptor initiated by ligand binding
  and subsequent activation of caspase-8) pathway and intrinsic pathway (driven by
  Bcl-2 family proteins, release cyt-c and activation of caspase-9) of apoptosis.
  Both pathways lead to a common apoptosis pathway by activating caspases-3, -6, and
  -7, triggering apoptosis. Bcl-2 family proteins are primarily localized to mitochondria
  and present on the ER and the perinuclear membrane in hematopoietic cells.
