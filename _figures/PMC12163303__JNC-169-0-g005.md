caption: Overview of mechanisms of translation regulation. Activation of extracellular
  receptors leads to increases in mitogen‐activated protein kinases/kinase‐interacting
  serine/threonine‐protein kinase 1/2 (MNK1/2) and mammalian target of rapamycin complex
  1 (mTORC1) activity. MNK1/2 directly phosphorylates eukaryotic initiation factor
  4E (eIF4E) to facilitate translation. mTORC1 phosphorylates both eIF4E binding proteins
  (4E‐BP) and P70 S6 kinase (P70/S6K), increasing both translation initiation and
  elongation rates. P70/S6K may also activate fragile X messenger ribonucleoprotein
  (FMRP), leading to stalling of the elongation process. In response to cellular stress,
  the integrated stress response pathway is activated and prevents translation initiation
  by blocking the association of eukaryotic initiation factor 2 (eIF2) with the Met‐tRNA.
