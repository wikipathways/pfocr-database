caption: Molecular mechanisms linking ROS, antioxidants, and the insulin signalling
  pathway. To allow a response to insulin stimulation, ROS are relieving insulin receptor's
  inhibition by phosphatases such as PTP1B. When the cellular environment shifts to
  an oxidative one, because of increased mitochondrial respiration and ROS release
  for example, the stress-sensitive kinases are activated upon redox-sensitive phosphatases
  inhibition. Theses kinases are inhibiting signal amplification by inhibitory phosphorylation
  of IRS proteins. Mitochondrial ROS can be regulated by PGC-1α-dependent detoxification
  system (Sirt3, SOD, GPx) or by specific mitochondrial antioxidants (SS31, MitoQ).
