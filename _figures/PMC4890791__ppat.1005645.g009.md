caption: (A) As temperatures increase (e.g., from 30°C to 37°C), unknown sensor(s)
  trigger the RIP pathway bind σE to RseA on the inner membrane. σE is then incorporated
  into RNAP to activate (solid lines) the expression of luxR and QS. (B) When temperatures
  increase to higher, unpermitted conditions (e.g., 42°C), RIP signaling releases
  the inner membrane bound σE to activate the expression of rpoH or other factors,
  which initiates the response to heat stress to improve survival and switch off the
  expression of LuxR and QS (dashed lines). The rpoE-resABC and luxR loci are shown.
  The temperature-dependent σE switch, which can turn luxR expression on/off by interacting
  with AphA/LuxR, is highlighted with red arrows and bar-ended lines. Factors that
  may be involved in RIP signaling are shown and discussed in the text.
