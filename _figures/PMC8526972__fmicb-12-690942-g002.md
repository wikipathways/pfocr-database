caption: Activating pathways of adrenergic sensors. Catecholamines activate various
  cellular signal transduction by binding to α 1-, α 2-, and β-adrenoreceptors (yellow).
  The α1-receptor is coupled with Gq protein, allowing activation of kinase protein
  C (PKC) and increase of intracellular concentration of Ca2+, through the triphosphate
  inositol (IP3)/diacylglycerol (DAG) pathway. Activation of this pathway results
  from the cleavage of phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate (PIP2) in IP3 and DAG
  thanks to phospholipase C (PLC). α-2 and β-receptors are coupled to Gi and Gs, respectively.
  In both cases, cAMP (cyclic adenosine monophosphate) is increased or decreased depending
  on the stimulation (Gs) or inhibition (Gi) of adenylate cyclase (AC), leading to
  the activation of kinase protein A (PKA). Adapted from .
