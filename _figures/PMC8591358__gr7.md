caption: 'NEU1 disrupts the MUC1-CD/PI3K/Akt pathway: A proposed model.A, the p85
  subunit of PI3K binds to phosphorylated Tyr-20 of the MUC1-CD. The PI3K p110 catalytic
  subunit promotes the conversion of phosphatidylinositol-(4,5)-bisphosphate (PIP2)
  to phosphatidylinositol-(3,4,5)-bisphosphate (PIP3) in the plasma membrane. Phosphoinositide-dependent
  kinase-1 (PDK1) is recruited to membrane-bound PIP3 where it stimulates the phosphorylation
  of Akt, leading to downstream signaling pathways that stimulate cell growth and
  survival and angiogenesis. B, NEU1 sialidase disrupts the MUC1-CD-PI3K-Akt signaling
  cascade by binding to the MUC1-CD and competitively displacing its interaction with
  PI3K. MUC1-CD, mucin-1 cytoplasmic domain; NEU1, neuraminidase-1.'
