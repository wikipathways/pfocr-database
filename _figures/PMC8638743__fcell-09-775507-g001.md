caption: Regulation of the mTOR pathway. In mammalian cells, mTOR forms two complexes
  with distinctive structure and functions, namely mTORC1 and mTORC2. mTORC1 promotes
  cell growth and proliferation via phosphorylation of S6K and 4E-BP1. In contrast,
  mTORC2 regulates cell survival by phosphorylating Ser473 of AKT. mTORC1 responds
  to various signals, including nutrients, energy, growth factors, and oxygen levels.
  Upon negative feedback from mTORC1/S6K to IRS1/PI3K signaling, hyper-activated mTORC1
  inhibits the activity of mTORC2. Various components of the mTOR pathway are directly
  transactivated by p53, including REDD1, LKB1, AMPKβ, TSC2, PTEN, Sestrin1/2, mTOR,
  Rictor, and DEPTOR (highlighted in red color in the figure).
