caption: Typical TCS signaling pathway. First, a membrane-bound HK homodimer senses
  the signal and is auto-phosphorylated by ATP at the histidine residue. Second, the
  phosphoryl group on the phosphorylated HK is transferred to the conserved aspartate
  at the receiver domain of the cognate RR. Third, the phosphorylated RR (P-RR) interacts
  with target genes or proteins, and intracellular responses are triggered accordingly.
  Finally, P-RR is dephosphorylated by modulation of intrinsic or HK-induced P-RR
  autophosphatase activity (; ).
