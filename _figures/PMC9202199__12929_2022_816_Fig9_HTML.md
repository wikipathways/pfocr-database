caption: Effect of growth factors on heteronemin-induced anti-proliferation in human
  cancer cells. Growth factors bind with particular receptors and stimulate signal
  transduction pathways, mainly PI3K and ERK1/2. In addition, STAT3 activation involves
  cancer cell proliferation. The signals of growth factors may engage in crosstalk
  with integrin αvβ3. The working model for heteronemin suppresses the tumorigenicity
  of cancer cells through the inactivation of PI3K, ERK1/2, and STAT3. Heteronemin
  perhaps not only inhibited downstream of integrin αvβ3, ERK/MAPK pathway, but also
  crosstalk with growth factor receptor to suppress the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway
