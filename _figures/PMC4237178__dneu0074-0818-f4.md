caption: Canonical and non-canonical Wnt pathways exert opposite effects on the formation
  of the vertebrate NMJ. The noncanonical Wnt cascade (left panel) has a positive
  role on postsynaptic development. Wnt3 activates Rac1 and induces the formation
  of AChR microclusters, which are then converted to stable and large clusters in
  the presence of Agrin. In contrast, activation of the canonical Wnt pathway (right
  panel) has a negative effect on NMJ formation. Wnt3a, through β-catenin, inhibits
  the expression of Rapsyn resulting in the dispersal of AChR clusters.
