caption: Schematic representation of survival signaling vs. proliferative signaling
  from the EGF receptor based on surface restriction of EGFR activation. The TNC EGF-like
  repeats provide for less efficient activation of the Ras-Raf-ERK and PI3kinase-Akt
  pathways in a persistent staccato manner; this leads to survival signaling. However,
  soluble EGF ligands drive EGFR internalization with the endosomal active EGFR efficiently
  signaling via the Ras-Raf-ERK pathway to drive mitogenesis. Not shown are the motogenic
  pathways (PLCgamma, ERK-calpain II, and PKCdelta) that are preferentially signaled
  from surface restricted EGFR.
