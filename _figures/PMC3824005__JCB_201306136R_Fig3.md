caption: Canonical and noncanonical functions of the apoptotic machinery. (A) The
  apoptotic machinery is not only involved in eliminating cells destined to die, but
  is also a central player in refining neuronal connectivity, by regulating synaptic
  transmission and by generating the adult connectivity through axon pruning (; ).
  But how the canonical and noncanonical roles of the apoptotic machinery are interlinked
  and spatially restricted is not well understood. (B) In the adult nervous system,
  the pro-apoptotic proteins BAX, caspase 9, and caspase 3 promote weakening of synapses
  (long-term depression [LTD]; ; ; ), while the anti-apoptotic proteins Bcl-Xl and
  the IAP survivin promote synaptic strengthening (long-term potentiation [LTP]; Li
  et al., 2008a; ). It is unclear how the activation of these pathways is restricted
  to a single synapse, but a recent review suggested that the proteasomal degradation
  of activated caspases may prevent their diffusion (). (C) Caspase activation is
  now known to be required for axon pruning during development to generate the adult
  refined connectivity (; ). Different pathways are activated depending on the stimulus
  leading to degeneration. Growth factor deprivation during development leads to activation
  the executor caspases 3 and 6 () through the intrinsic apoptotic pathway, although
  its core protein Apaf1 does not seem to be required for this process (). On the
  other hand, a traumatic injury leads to reduced influx of NMNAT2 into the axon,
  which negatively affects the stability and function of mitochondria and leads to
  an increased calcium concentration (). The effector caspase, caspase 6, is dispensable
  for this form of axonal degeneration (; ). Regulatory proteins such as the IAPs
  and also the proteasome seem to play a role in limiting the extent of activation
  to the degenerating part of the axon (; ; ). (D) Simplified schematic of the main
  pro- and anti-apoptotic components. DISC, death-induced signaling complex. IAP,
  inhibitor of apoptosis protein. See  for details.
