caption: Peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor gamma (PPARγ) translocates to
  the nucleus to bind with retinoid X receptor (RXR) and then activates PPAR response
  elements (PPREs), such as superoxide dismutase (SOD) to inhibit reactive oxygen
  species (ROS) generation. ROS inhibition does not stimulate the β-catenin nuclear
  transcription and therefore does not activate proliferation processes and the NFκB
  pathway. PPARγ, through its anti-inflammatory role, inhibits NFκB and decreases
  inflammation. Through a catenin domain, PPARγ directly inhibits the TCF/LEF/β-catenin
  nuclear activity. In parallel, PPARγ can activate DKK, a WNT inhibitor.
