caption: The signalling pathway of type III interferons (IFNs) compared with type
  II and type I IFNs. IFNλ binds the IFNλR1 chain leading to a conformational change
  in the receptor. It recruits a second short receptor chain (IL10R2). JAK 1 and Tyk
  2 transphosphorylate the receptor chains and form phosphorylated tyrosine peptides
  on the IFNλR1 receptor chain. STAT proteins bind and are phosphorylated. They can
  then form homo- or heterodimers and migrate to the nucleus to bind gene regulatory
  elements (gamma-activated sequences, GAS). In the case of type I and type III IFNs,
  they can also bind interferon regulatory factor 9 (IRF9) in the cytoplasm and then
  migrate to the nucleus to bind interferon stimulated regulatory elements (ISREs)
  to regulate gene transcription.
