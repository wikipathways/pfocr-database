caption: Trafficking pathways of E-cadherin. E-cadherin can undergo either clathrin-dependent
  or independent endocytosis. Internalized E-cadherin traffic through different endosomal
  compartments. Numerous molecules and endocytic machineries determine the fate of
  E-cadherin either to endosomal recycling back to the plasma membrane or to lysosomal
  degradation. Polarized sorting in both the biosynthetic pathway from the trans-Golgi
  network and recycling endosomes requires vesicle carriers such as AP-1 as discussed
  in the main text.
