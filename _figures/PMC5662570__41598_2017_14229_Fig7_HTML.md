caption: Illustration of L. infantum LPG-induced PGE2-associated inflammatory response.
  Intact LPG is recognized by TLR-1/2 (i) on the membranes of IFN-γ−primed macrophages
  (ii), triggering the MAPs kinase signaling pathway, leading to the phosphorylation
  of PKC, ERK and JNK (iii). This process induce thes PGE2 production and lipid droplet
  formation, as well as NO and MCP-1 production in addition to a proinflammatory cytokine
  storm (iv). PPARγ regulates the LPG-induced PGE2-associated inflammatory response
  in a feedback mechanism (v). Illustration by Théo Araújo-Santos.
