caption: Mechanistic diagram for microglial IRF5/4 activation. Upon DAMPs’ Stimulation,
  a Myddosome is formed in the cytoplasm to phosphorylate IRF5. P-IRF5 then translocates
  into the nucleus and bind to ISRE to regulate the expression of pro-inflammatory
  mediators. IL4 signaling, however, triggers activation of STAT6-Jmjd3-IRF4 pathway.
  IRF4 competes with IRF5 for MyD88 binding and the IRF5-IRF4 regulatory axis regulates
  microglial activation in a balanced way.
