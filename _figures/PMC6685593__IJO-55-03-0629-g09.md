  channel and its activation may induce Ca2+ influx. Subsequently, intracellular Ca2+
  increase directly or indirectly activates Akt, mTOR, upregulating the expression
  of cyclin D1 and CDK4. Phosphorylation of Akt and mTOR, followed by activation of
  the cyclin D1/CDK4 complex, may facilitate cell survival, cell cycle progression,
  cell proliferation and migration, which in turn may promote the tumorigenesis of
  prostate cancer.
