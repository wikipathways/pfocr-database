  glucose to pyruvate ultimately producing lactate, or acetyl-CoA in the TCA cycle
  for mitochondrial oxidative phosphorylation. Anabolically, glycolysis generates
  metabolic precursors for serine biosynthesis and supports redox potential and nucleotide
  biosynthesis via the pentose phosphate pathway. Most leukemias are highly dependent
  on glycolysis and can be targeted using glycolytic inhibitors.
