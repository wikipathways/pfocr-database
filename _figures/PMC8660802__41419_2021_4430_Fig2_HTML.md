caption: Exosome biogenesis begins with early endosome formation through endocytosis
  at the plasma membrane. The membrane receptors are internalized through clathrin-coated
  pits and delivered to early endosomes. By ESCRT dependent or independent pathway,
  the endosomal membrane buds inward to form exosomes, and then early endosomes mature
  into MVBs. Through this process, exosomes contain a variety of factors including
  DNA, RNA, and intracellular proteins. Exosomes in MVBs are delivered to lysosomes
  for degradation or fused with the plasma membrane via the Rab proteins for release
  to the extracellular space. After secretion, the recipient cell mediates uptake
  of exosomes through endocytosis, fusion with the plasma membrane, or ligand/receptor
  interaction. ESCRT Endosomal sorting complexes required for transport, MVB multivesicular
  body.
