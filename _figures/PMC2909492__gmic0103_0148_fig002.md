caption: Probiotics modulate key signaling pathways in intestinal epithelial cells.
  Various probiotics prevent NFκB activation by inhibiting IκBα phosphorylation, ubiquitination,
  proteasomal degradation, or translocation of NFκB into the nucleus (suppression
  is indicated by a block sign “??”). Probiotics can also enhance RelA export from
  the nucleus via PPARγ. Other probiotics increase NFκB activation through enhanced
  translocation into the nucleus (activation is indicated by an arrow sign “→”). Apoptosis
  of intestinal epithelial cells can be prevented by probiotic modulation of the PI3K/Akt
  pathway. Probiotic-induced changes in phosphorylation levels of p38, JNK, and ERK1/2
  MAPKs can affect cytokine secretion and apoptosis. ERK, extracellular signal-regulated
  kinases; IκBα, inhibitor of NFκB α; IKK, IκB kinase; IRAK, interleukin-1 receptor-associated
  kinase; JNK, c-Jun N-terminal kinase; P, phosphorylation; PPARγ, peroxisome proliferator
  activated receptor-γ; RXR, retinoid X receptor; TLR, Toll-like receptor; Ub, ubiquitin.
