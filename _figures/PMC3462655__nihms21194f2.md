caption: Schematic representation of the signaling pathways involved in T-cell activation.
  Antigen recognition by the TCR and coreceptor (CD4 or CD8) ligation releases Lck
  from Csk inhibition, leading to phosphorylation of the TCR ξ-chains and recruitment
  of ZAP-70. ZAP-70 phosphorylates LAT, which ligates PLCγ-1 and thereby amplifies
  and sustains the activation signal. PLCγ-1 can activate different signaling cascades
  via cleavage of PIP2, such as the PKC and calcineurin pathways leading to cellular
  activation and differentiation. The function of PLCγ-1 is further enhanced by CD28
  ligation, via interaction with Ltk. Additionally, LAT can interact with several
  linker molecules, leading to activation of the MAPK-pathway via activation of Ras
  or the Rho A pathway leading to actin reorganization.
