caption: 'CCL5/CCR5 signaling pathway. CCR5 is a 7-transmembrane G protein-coupled
  receptor with 3 extracellular loops and 3 intracellular loops. The disulfide bonds
  formed by 4 cysteine residues in the extracellular domain are vital for stabilizing
  the extracellular structure. Three cysteine residues on the carboxyl terminal domain
  can anchor the tail to the membrane. DRYLAVHA sequence on the second intracellular
  loop can interact with G protein. Following CCL5 binding to CCR5, G protein dissociates
  into the αi and βγ subunits; the former inhibits adenylate cyclase (AC), whereas
  the latter activates phospholipase Cβ (PLCβ) and phosphatidylinositol 3 kinase (PI-3K).
  The following mediators are then activated in turn: diacylglycol (DAG), protein
  kinase C (PKC), inositol-1,4,5-triphosphate (IP3), intracellular Ca2+, calcineurin
  (CaN), nuclear factor of activated T cells (NFAT), extracellular signal regulated
  kinase 1 and 2 (ERK1/2), p38, c-Jun N-terminal kinase (JNK), protein kinase B (PKB/Akt),
  and Rho GTPases, which eventually affect cell migration, survival, cytoskeleton
  rearrangement, cell polarization, and T-cell proliferation.'
