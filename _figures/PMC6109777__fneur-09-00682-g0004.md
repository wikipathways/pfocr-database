caption: Schematic representation of cellular protection mechanism conferred by Nrf2-ARE
  pathway after intracerebral hemorrhage (ICH). Under normal conditions, Nrf2 forms
  a complex with actin-bound Keap1 and Cullin 3, which are isolated in the cytoplasm.
  Heme released after ICH, along with LPS and oxidative stress, can induce reactive
  oxygen species (ROS), which can induce and mediate dissociation of the Nrf2-Keap1
  complex. Then, Nrf2 translocates to the nucleus and heterodimerizes with Maf. Subsequently,
  Nrf2 binds to antioxidant response elements (ARE) in the promoter regions of HO-1
  and other target genes, activating transcription. Bach1 is an inhibitor of the Nrf2-ARE
  signaling pathway; thus, the removal of Bach1 is a prerequisite for transcription
  of this pathway.
