caption: 'The transforming growth factor (TGF)-β signaling cascade. TGF-β binds to
  the type II receptor that recruits, phosphorylates and activates type I receptor
  (TβRI). TβRI in turn phosphorylates SMAD2/3 which then associate with the co-SMAD
  SMAD4 to form a heterocomplex that accumulates in the nucleus to regulate target
  gene transcription. Inhibitory SMAD7 binds activated TβRI to prevent SMAD2/3 phosphorylation,
  or recruits E3 ubiquitin ligases (SMURF1/2, WW1) or phosphatase GADD34/PP1 to the
  receptor complexes, inducing proteasomal degradation or dephosphorylation of the
  latter, respectively. C-SKI and SNON act as transcriptional co-repressors to repress
  TGF-β-induced, SMAD-driven, transcription. TβRII: TGF-βreceptor type II.'
