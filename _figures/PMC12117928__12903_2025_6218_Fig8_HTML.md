caption: Oxidative damage induced by lipid droplet accumulation impairs the physiological
  osteogenesis-osteoclasis balance on titanium surface in hyperlipidemia. ERS and
  mitochondrial damage induced by lipid droplet accumulation in hyperlipidemia tends
  to form a vicious cycle network and promotes the expression of inflammatory factors
  in peri-implant tissues, which further dramatically drives osteoclastogenesis and
  inhibits osteogenesis on titanium implant surface. After local application of NAC,
  the ROS level is downregulated, which effectively activates the Wnt/β-catenin signaling
  pathway and inhibits the RANKL/NF-κB signaling pathway to alleviate the osteogenesis-osteoclasis
  imbalance, thereby ultimately facilitating the Ti implant osseointegration in hyperlipidemia.
