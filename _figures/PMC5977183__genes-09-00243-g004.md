caption: The oncojanus effect of CI encoding genes on tumorigenesis. Mild functional
  alterations of CI cause mitochondrial ROS overproduction and subsequent activation
  of the oncogenic Akt/mTORC1 (mammalian Target Of Rapamycin Complex 1) pathway that
  leads to cell proliferation and survival, epithelial–mesenchymal transition (EMT),
  and HIF1α stabilization, thus favoring tumor progression. Conversely, severe CI
  dysfunction provokes the accumulation of tricarboxylic acids (TCA) cycle products
  NADH and α-ketoglutarate (KG), which impedes Hypoxia Inducible Factor (HIF) 1α stabilization,
  hypoxic adaptation, ultimately arresting tumor growth. Moreover, the energetic crisis
  stimulates apoptotic cell death.
