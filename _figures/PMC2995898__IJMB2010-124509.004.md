caption: Predicted pathways of LPS O-antigen and core molecule biosynthesis. Brucellae
  derive all sugars from glucose. Green arrows indicate the reactions leading to the
  synthesis of the monomeric O-antigen subunits, which are polymerized onto bactoprenol
  (pathway indicated in blue). The complete O-antigen is then transported to the periplasmic
  face of the inner membrane by the ABC transporter system Wzm/Wzt and ligated to
  lipid A molecule by the O-antigen ligase WaaL. Pathways leading to the biosynthesis
  of the core molecule are indicated in red. Hexagons indicate whether mutants in
  the respective genes are affected in the biosynthesis of the O-antigen (OAg) or
  the sugar core molecule (Core). Dotted arrows indicate abbreviated pathways involving
  the defined enzymes.
