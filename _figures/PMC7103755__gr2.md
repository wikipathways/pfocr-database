caption: Schematic representation of the biosynthetic pathway for non-methylene-interrupted
  PUFAs in plants. The ubiquitous essential fatty acids linoleic acid and a-linolenic
  acid can undergo either Δ5-desaturation to yield the C18 NMI-PUFAs pinolenic acid
  (PNA; n-6) or coniferonic acid (CON; n-3), or undergo C2 Δ9-elongation to generate
  a methylene-interrupted intermediate eicosadienoic acid (EDA; n-6) or eicosatrienoic
  acid (ETA; n-3) which then undergo Δ5-desaturation to yield the NMI-PUFAs sciadonic
  acid (SCA; n-6) or juniperonic acid (JPA; n-3). The same enzymes (Δ9-elongase, Δ5-desaturase)
  recognise both omega-6 and omega-3 substrates.
