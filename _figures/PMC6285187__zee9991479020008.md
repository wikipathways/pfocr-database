caption: Illustration of the working mechanisms for FGF21 prevention of spontaneous
  and diabetes-induced germ cell apoptosis. Diabetes adversely affects glucose metabolism
  (AKT/GSK-3β/GS signaling) and fatty acid oxidation (AMPK/Sirt1/PGC-1α) in testis,
  leading to the accumulation of metabolic intermediates that cause testicular oxidative
  damage and germ cell apoptosis. FGF21 deficiency exacerbates these defects, leading
  to testicular cell apoptosis. In nondiabetics, FGF21 preserves almost normal spermatogenesis
  through the AKT-signaling pathway to inhibit p53 via AKT1/MDM2.
