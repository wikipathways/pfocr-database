caption: 'Canonical transforming growth factor beta (TGF-β) signaling is a process
  that involves ligands (TGF-β1, TGF-β2, TGF-β3), receptors (TGFBRI/II/III), receptor-activated
  SMADs (R-Smads: Smad2/3; I-Smads: Smad7), and the common Smad (co-Smad: Smad4).
  TGF-β superfamily ligands bind to a type II receptor, which recruits and phosphorylates
  a type I receptor. The type I receptor then phosphorylates R-Smads that can bind
  Smad4. R-Smads/co-Smad complexes accumulate in the nucleus where they act as transcription
  factors and participate in the regulation of target gene expression.'
