caption: Diagram of the role of RKIP in various signaling pathways. RKIP inhibits
  the ERK/MAPK pathway by preventing Raf1 from associating with MEK, and on phosphorylation
  via PKC inhibits activation of GRK2. It also prevents the phosphorylation-induced
  inhibition of GSK3β by p38 mitogen activated protein kinase. Finally, RKIP exerts
  an inhibitory effect on the NFκB signaling pathway by interacting with kinases responsible
  for the phosphorylation of IκB and subsequent dissociation from NFκB. This event
  leads to IκB degradation and relief of inhibition of NFκB.
