caption: Biosynthetic pathways for wyosine/wybutosine and derivatives. (A) The eukaryotic
  hydroxywybutosine (OHyW). (B) the proposed biosynthetic pathway of archaeal (specifically
  Crenarchaeota) wyosine (imG), also found in the cytoplasm of one eukaryote (Candida
  utilis) (). TAW1 and TAW3 are the archaeal homologs of eukaryotic TYW1 and TYW3.
  Eukaryotic TYW4 and TYW5 have never been found in Archaea. Most Euryarchaeota contain
  a TYW2 homolog that forms yW-86 derivatives. All steps other than hydroxylation
  of yW-72 via TYW5 require SAM ().
