caption: The Small GTPase non-Smad pathway. RhoA can be activated by TGF-β via either
  Smad-dependent or independent routes to induce actin stress fiber formation during
  EMT. TGF-β also induces the dissolution of tight junctions during EMT by recruiting
  Cdc42 to the receptor complex, and by triggering degradation of RhoA at cellular
  protrusions. TGF-β-induced RhoA degradation requires phosphorylation of Par6 by
  TβRII and the Smurf1 E3 ligase.
