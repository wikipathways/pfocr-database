caption: A model depicting the mode that hypoxia exacerbats acute lung injury (ALI)
  via the toll-like receptor 4 (TLR4) signaling pathway and the protective effect
  of hypoxia-inducible factor 1 alpha (HIF-1α). Acute hypoxia causes a drastic exacerbation
  of inflammation predisposing ALI. Hypxia potentiates lipopolysaccharides (LPS)-induced
  cytokine production via the TLR4 signaling pathway in alveolar macrophage cells.
  Moreover, LPS and combined hypoxia stimuli induce compensatory HIF-1α accumulation,
  in turn, to protect the lungs by supperssing TLR4 expression and attenuating macrophageal
  inflammatory responses.
