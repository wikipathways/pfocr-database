caption: The non-shivering thermogenesis activation pathway. Cold stress is sensed
  by the brain (A) and signals the sympathetic nervous system to release norepinephrine
  (NE) (B) that interact with b-adrenergic receptor (b-AR) on brown adipocytes (C).
  As a result, triglycerides (TAG) are broken down to free fatty acids (FFA) (D) that
  interact with uncoupling protein-1 (UCP-1) (E) to induce the mitochondria to generate
  heat (E). The thyroid hormone (TH) plays a role in the cold stress (F) sensor region
  of the brain (G).
