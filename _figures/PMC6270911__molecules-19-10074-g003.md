caption: The “Hinge and Latch” Hypothesis. Under basal conditions, Keap1 forms a homodimer
  and associates with Cullin-3 protein. At the same time, the DGR domains of two Keap1
  bind to the DLG (latch) and the ETGE (hinge) domains in a single Nrf2. In response
  to Nrf2 inducers, the DLG motif in Nrf2, but not ETGE motif in Nrf2, is released
  from the DGR domain in Keap1.
