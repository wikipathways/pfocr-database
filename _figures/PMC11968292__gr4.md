caption: MicroRNAs play a crucial role in fine-tuning the regulation of the Nrf2 signaling
  pathway. MiR-27a, miR-141, miR-144, and miR-432 repress the translation of Keap1
  mRNA, thereby promoting Nrf2 activation. In contrast, miR-144, miR-155, miR-28,
  and miR-148b inhibit Nrf2 activity by repressing Nrf2 mRNA. Additionally, miR-181a,
  miR-193b, miR-424, and miR-125b are positively correlated with increased Nrf2 production
  and indirectly activate Nrf2. Notably, miR-200a inhibits Nrf2 ubiquitination, contributing
  to Nrf2 stabilization
