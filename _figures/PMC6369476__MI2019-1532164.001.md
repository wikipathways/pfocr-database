caption: The potential proinflammatory role of resistin in the pathogenesis of osteoarthritis.
  Resistin possibly binds to the transmembrane Toll-like receptor 4 (TLR4) and activates
  TNF receptor-associated factor 6 (TRAF6) through the MyD88-dependent signaling pathway,
  which then activates the p38-mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK), C/enhancer-binding
  protein β (EBPβ), and nuclear factor- (NF-) κB signaling pathways, leading to the
  activation of the transcription of proinflammatory cytokine genes in the cell nucleus.
  Resistin can also stimulate the expression of proinflammatory cytokines and chemokines
  through the p38-MAPK and NF-κB pathways by binding to adenylyl cyclase-associated
  protein 1 (CAP1) receptors, which activates the cyclic AMP (cAMP)-protein kinase
  A (PKA) signaling pathway.
