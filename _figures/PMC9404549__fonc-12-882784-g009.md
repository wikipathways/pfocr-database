caption: Schematics illustrate the identified signaling cascade triggered by SCH treatment.
  In TNBC cells, SCH treatment causes DNA damage (increased phosphorylation levels
  of H2AX, ATM, p53, ATR, and CHK1), which leads to cell-cycle arrest. At the same
  time, SCH treatment suppresses DNA repair (reduced RAD51 and FANCD2), and DNA repair
  failure finally leads to apoptosis.
