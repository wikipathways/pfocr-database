caption: The interaction between nutrient-sensing pathways and mitochondrial oxidative
  stress and chronic inflammation. The interplay among IIS, AMPK, mTOR, and Sirt involves
  regulating the Nrf2 and NF-κB signaling pathways and mitochondrial function through
  cascading interactions. Dysfunction in the mitochondria releases mtDNA, triggering
  inflammatory responses through the cGAS-STING signaling pathway. The release of
  inflammatory markers and mitochondrial dysfunction further induce oxidative stress,
  thereby exacerbating chronic inflammation and mitochondrial damage. By modulating
  key components in the described signaling pathways, stimuli can facilitate the rejuvenation
  of senescent BM-MSCs
