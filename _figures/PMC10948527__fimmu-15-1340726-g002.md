caption: 'SHP2 signaling pathways: SHP2-dependent signaling in both developing and
  established cancer cells is illustrated, with arrows denoting positive regulators
  and T-bars indicating negative regulators. Functioning as a convergence node, SHP2
  exhibits diverse roles in cell survival-related signaling pathways. It not only
  positively influences the RAS-RAF-MEK-ERK pathway but also modulates the PI3K-AKT
  and JAK-STAT pathways, either enhancing or antagonizing based on extracellular stimuli
  and the cellular environment. Additionally, SHP2 is involved in Wnt/β-catenin and
  NF-κB pathways. Nuclear localization of SHP2 has been observed in various contexts,
  and in the Hippo signaling pathway, YAP and TAZ act as the rheostat for nuclear
  SHP2 function'
