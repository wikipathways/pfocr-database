caption: 'Functions of E-cadherin and N-cadherin. E-cadherin junctions form the stable
  adherens junction and enable strong cell–cell contact. As p120 catenin and β-catenin
  are strongly bound to the E-cadherin complex, they are not available to activate
  Wnt/β-catenin pathway and P13K pathway. N-cadherin junction enables the stabilization
  of fibroblast growth factor receptor (FGFR) which leads to activation of MAPK/ERK
  pathway and activates the PI3K pathway in association with PDGFR to enhance cell
  survival and migration. Abbreviations: PI3K = Phosphoinositide-3-kinase; RAC = Ras-related
  C3 Botulinum Toxin Substrate; GSK3 = Glycogen Synthase Kinase 3; TCF = T-cell Factor;
  PDGFR: Platelet-derived Growth Factor Receptor; FGFR: Fibroblast Growth Factor Receptor;
  MAPK = Mitogen-activated Protein Kinase; ERK = Extracellular Signal-regulated Kinases.'
