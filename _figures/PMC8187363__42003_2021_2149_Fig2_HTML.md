caption: Accessory proteins are involved in every step of the RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway
  and increase the connectivity between signalling components. Adaptor proteins and
  docking proteins interact with phosphorylated receptors, in contrast to anchoring
  proteins, which are directly associated with the membrane (a). Recruiter translocate
  binding partner towards the site of action, e.g., a GTPase activating protein to
  activated RAS at the plasma membrane (b). Scaffold proteins that induce nanoclustering,
  increase the local concentration of their binding partner in lipid rafts (c). RAS
  scaffold proteins bind RAS and other components of the RTK-RAS-MAPK pathway (d),
  whereas ERK binder (e), MEK/ERK binder (f) and RAF/MEK/ERK binder (g) can connect
  one, two, or all three members of the MAPKs, bringing them close together, regulate
  their activity and determine their localisation. See text for more details.
