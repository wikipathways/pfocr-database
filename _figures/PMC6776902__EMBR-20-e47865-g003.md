caption: Modifications in mitochondrial translation or mitoribosome composition trigger
  distinct transcriptional changes that impact aging. The absence of the mitoribosomal
  subunit Afo1 activates mitochondrial back‐signaling involving the transcription
  factor Sfp1, an essential regulator of cytosolic translation. The loss of mitochondrial
  translation control, e.g., in the absence of Sov1, impacts on PKA signaling and
  the Msn2/4 stress response and activates Sir2‐mediated gene silencing. Mutations
  that alter the accuracy of mitochondrial translation trigger transcriptional changes
  via the Msn2/4 stress signaling pathway that affect cellular proteostasis and aging.
