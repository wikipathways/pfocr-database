caption: Model demonstrating the specific apoptotic effect of SREBP-1 and FAS inhibition
  on GBM cells bearing EGFRvIII. (A) In cells with little EGFR and intact PTEN, the
  demand for lipogenesis is modest and is balanced by low levels of SREBP-1 pathway
  activation. (B) In GBM cells with abundant EGFR and PTEN loss, the SREBP-1-ACC-FAS
  pathway is upregulated to meet the demand for increased fatty acid synthesis for
  membrane biogenesis of rapidly dividing cancer cells. (C) The SREBP-1 pathway becomes
  essential for survival in GBM cells bearing EGFRvIII. Targeting SREBP-1 or FAS is
  therefore lethal to EGFRvIII-bearing GBM cells.
