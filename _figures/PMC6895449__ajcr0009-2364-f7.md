caption: Illustration of the NEK2/β-catenin/Myc/KDM5B/H3K4me3 pathway. NEK2 binds
  to and stabilized β-catenin [-], promotes β-catenin translocation into nucleus [,].
  β-catenin forms a complex with TCF/LEF to activate the transcription of c-Myc gene
  []. As a transcriptional factor, c-Myc may transcriptionally regulate the expression
  of NEK2 and KDM5B. KDM5B demethylates H3K4me3 to a transcriptionally inactive state
  and decreases the transcription of tumor suppressor genes, then promotes cancer
  cell proliferation, metastasis and tumorigenesis.
