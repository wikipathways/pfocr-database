caption: Proposed models how DACT2 affects the β-catenin/Cdc25c pathway in nasopharyngeal
  carcinoma. When β-catenin/LEF1 complex is activated, it promotes the development
  of nasopharyngeal carcinoma by targeting its downstream genes including Cdc25c,
  which plays a crucial role in regulating cell cycle G2/M stage. DACT2 inhibits the
  activity of β-catenin/LEF1 complex by competitively binding to β-catenin and downregulates
  the expression of Cdc25c and its downstream genes, which can suppress nasopharyngeal
  carcinoma growth
