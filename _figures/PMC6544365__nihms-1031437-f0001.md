caption: The PI3K/Akt signal pathway is crucial to SDF1/CXCR-mediated homing of endothelial
  progenitor cells (EPCs) after myocardiac infarction. Ischemia-associated cell death
  triggered by myocardiac infarction initiates an immune response by producing various
  cytokines, such as SDF-1. SDF-1 activates its corresponding receptor in circulating
  EPCs via activation of CXCR4. This binding in turn mediates the PI-3K/AKT signaling
  pathway that induces the progenitor cell migration toward myocardial infarcts to
  initiate the tissue repair by increasing CXCR4 and MMP-9 as well as cytoskeleton
  rearrangement. Either AMD3100, an inhibitor for CXCR4, or LY294002, an inhibitor
  for PI3K, can downregulate this signaling event.
