caption: Small-molecule compounds targeting apoptosis-related pathways in cancer.
  There are two core apoptosis pathways, intrinsic and extrinsic. The extrinsic pathway
  is initiated by multiple death receptors, such as TNFR1, Fas, and DR4/5. The intrinsic
  pathway is mediated by Bcl-2 family proteins. Activation of either pathway ultimately
  triggers a cascade of caspases, thus inducing caspase-dependent nucleosome fragmentation
  leading to cell death. In addition, NF-κB, JAK-STAT3, and MAPKs signaling pathways
  play an essential role in regulating cell apoptosis
