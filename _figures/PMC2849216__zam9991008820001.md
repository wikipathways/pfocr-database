caption: Biosynthesis pathway for ectoine. l-Aspartate-β-semialdehyde (ASA) is converted
  into l-2,4-diaminobutyrate (DABA) by the enzyme DABA aminotransferase, encoded by
  the ectB gene. DABA is converted into N-γ-acetyl-l-2,4-diaminobutyrate (ADABA) by
  DABA acetyltransferase, encoded by the ectA gene, and l-ectoine (Ect) is formed
  from ADABA by ectoine synthetase, encoded by the ectC gene (, , ). Ectoine can then
  be hydroxylated to form 5-hydroxyectoine (EctOH) by the enzyme ectoine hydroxylase,
  encoded by the ectD gene (, ). CoA, coenzyme A.
