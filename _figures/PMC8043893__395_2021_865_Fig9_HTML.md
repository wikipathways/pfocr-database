caption: Model of SKI-mediated regulation of Hippo signaling and cardiac fibroblast
  activation. When SKI is localized in the cytoplasm, LIMD1 can freely associate and
  inhibit the function of LATS2 kinase, thus allowing TAZ-dependent, pro-fibrotic
  signaling to occur. Conversely, when SKI is functioning in the nucleus, it inhibits
  LIMD1 which, in turn, de-represses LATS2 kinase. The result is the phosphorylation
  and proteasomal degradation of TAZ, and the inhibition of the activated myofibroblast
  phenotype
