caption: A schematic representation shows that several lncRNAs regulate the PI3K/AKT,
  MAPK/ERK and JAK2/STAT3 pathways in pancreatic cancer. Growth factor-driven RTK
  (e.g., EGFR) or cytokine (e.g., IL- 6) signaling can trigger the activation of PI3K/AKT,
  MAPK/ERK, and JAK2/STAT3 cascades. LncRNAs can affect the activity of these cascades.
  For instance, HOTAIR can trigger the activation of the JAK2/STAT3 pathway via down-regulating
  miR-34a expression, thus promoting invasion and migration of pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma
  []. In addition, GAS5 can up-regulate PTEN expression by down-regulating the expression
  level of miR-32-5p, therefore inhibiting pancreatic cancer metastasis []. LINC01559,
  through sponging miR-1343-3p, can up-regulate RAF1 expression that can further activate
  the ERK signaling pathway, thereby enhancing pancreatic cancer progression and metastasis
  []. Green arrows indicate the up-regulation of target genes modulated via lncRNAs;
  red arrows depict the inhibitory effects.
