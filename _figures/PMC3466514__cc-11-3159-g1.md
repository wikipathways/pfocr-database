caption: Figure 1. Reciprocal crosstalk between estrogen receptor (ER) α and growth
  factor receptor signaling pathways. Growth factor receptors activate phosphatidylinositol
  3-kinase (PI3K) and MAPK signaling pathways. The effectors of these pathways, including
  ERK, RSK and S6K1 can then phosphorylate and activate ERα. ERα can also become activated
  by binding of its ligand, estrogen. When activated, ERα dimerizes, translocates
  to the nucleus, binds DNA and activates transcription of genes encoding components
  of growth factor signaling pathways, promoting a positive feed-forward pathway activation
  loop.
