caption: 'Schematic representation of C-C chemoattractant cytokine (chemokine) receptor
  6 (CCR6) activation pathway. After binding with the ligand, G Protein Coupled Receptors
  (GPCR) are activated to release the Gβ and Gγ subunits which in turn activate PLC
  and PI3 kinase. PI3 kinase then converts PIP2 to PIP3 which activates calcium ion
  mobilization across IP3. IP3 with DAG stimulated by calcium ions activate PKC and
  other PKCs which trigger phosphorylation causing cell migration. Legend for abbreviations:
  Gα, Gβ, Gγ—Subunits of GPCR, PI3 kinase—Phosphoinositide 3 kinase, PLC—Phospholipase
  Cβ2, DAG—diacylglycerol, PIP2—Phosphatidylinositol 4,5-bisphosphates, PIP3—Phosphatidylinositol
  4,5-bisphosphates 3, IP3—Inositol triphosphate, PKC—Protein kinase C, ER—endoplasmic
  reticulum, Ca+2—Calcium ions. Reproduced from []. Copyright 2003, CRC Press: Boca
  Raton, FL, USA.'
