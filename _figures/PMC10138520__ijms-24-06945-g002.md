caption: Compounds secreted by adipocytes support tumor growth, angiogenesis and metastasis.
  Adipocyte-secreted adiponectin, resistin, IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1),
  and HGF (hepatocyte growth factor) induce tumor growth via activation of the PI3K/Akt
  (phosphoinositide 3-kinase/protein kinase B) and STAT3 (signal transducer and activator
  of transcription protein 3) pathway. IGF-1 and HGF also activate the MAPK/ERK (mitogen-activated
  protein kinase/extracellular signal-regulated kinase) pathway, together with leptin.
  HGF, Leptin and VEGF-A (vascular endothelial growth factor A) are involved in angiogenesis,
  while secreted collagen VI and MMP11 (matrix metalloproteinase 11) play a role in
  tumor metastasis.
