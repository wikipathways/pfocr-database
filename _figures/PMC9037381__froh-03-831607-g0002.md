caption: Signaling pathways that are related to F. nucleatum infection. F. nucleatum
  upregulates MIR4435-2HG, which binds miR-296-5p and weakens the inhibitory effect
  of miR-296-5p on SNAI1 via AKT2 []. F. nucleatum can also activate the PI3K/AKT—nuclear
  factor kappa B (NF-κB) signaling pathway which regulates cell proliferation, apoptosis,
  and the inflammatory response []. The infection with F. nucleatum promotes the capability
  of proliferation by leading to DNA damage through the Ku70/p53 pathway [].
