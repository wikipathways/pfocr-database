caption: Illustration of this study’s results and a working model. BMP signaling enhances
  the Rankl/Opg expression ratio in cells of the osteoblastic lineage to promote osteoclastic
  bone resorption in bone remodeling. Moreover, the BMP-Smad pathway directly induces
  Atoh8 in immature osteoblasts. Atoh8 interacts with Runx2 and inhibits Runx2 transcriptional
  activity to suppress autoregulation of Runx2 and subsequent osteoblastic differentiation,
  as well as the Rankl/Opg expression ratio, thereby regulating the osteoclast number
  negatively to prevent excessive BMP-mediated bone resorption (left panel). The enhanced
  Runx2 activity in Atoh8-KO mice likely limited osteoblast maturation to maintain
  bone formation, while Runx2 increased the Rankl/Opg expression ratio to promote
  bone resorption and loss (right panel)
