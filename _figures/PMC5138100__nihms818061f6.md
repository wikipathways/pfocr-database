caption: (A) PHD3/EglN3 -mediated prolyl hydroxylation on EPOR (erythropoietin receptor)
  Pro419 and Pro426 residues facilitates EPOR binding with pVHL complex, hereby causing
  its proteasomal degradation. PHD3/EglN3 depletion stabilizes EPOR and, therefore,
  stimulates JAK/STAT signaling pathway-driven erythropoiesis. (B) MAPK6 (Mitogen-activated
  protein kinase 6) can be hydroxylated by PHD3/EglN3 on proline 25 (Pro25), which
  potentially leads to its dissociation from HUWE1 (HECT, UBA and WWE domain containing
  1) E3 ubiquitin protein ligase, thus protecting it from proteasomal degradation.
  (C) RIPK4 hydroxylation by FIH affects RIPK4 activity, therefore contributing to
  Wnt signaling pathway activity modulation.
