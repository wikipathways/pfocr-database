caption: (A, B) Different viruses possess or generate RNAs that can be detected by
  RIG‐I and/or MDA5. These sensors transduce signals via the mitochondria‐associated
  MAVS protein that oligomerises and activates TBK‐1 and IKKα, IKKβ and IKKε to phosphorylate
  IRF transcription factor family members (e.g. IRF3 as depicted), as well as IκB
  to release NF‐κB family transcription factors p60 and p65. These transcription factors
  co‐ordinately induce expression of type I and type III IFN and other genes. IFNs
  are secreted and type I IFNs (IFNα/β) act in autocrine or paracrine manner via the
  type I IFN receptor (IFNAR) and STAT1/STA2/IRF9 signalling to induce expression
  of ISGs, the products of which restrict virus infection. P, phosphate group.
