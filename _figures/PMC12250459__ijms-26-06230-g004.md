caption: 'Neuroprotective mechanisms of AOF. Its components activate TREM2, recruit
  DAP12/DAP10, and inhibit the nuclear translocation of GSK3β and NF-κB by activating
  the PI3K/AKT pathway, and have anti-inflammatory responses by inhibiting the Ras/MEK/Erk
  pathway. Meanwhile, TREM2 mediates BDNF/TrkB to cause the following: inhibit the
  LPS-stimulated TLR4 signaling pathway; promote the M2 polarization of microglia;
  activate the IGF-1R/PI3K/AKT pathway; phosphorylate Bad; up-regulate Bcl-2 and Bcl-xL
  to resist apoptosis; regulate the cell cycle to promote cell proliferation; further
  activate Nrf2 to resist oxidation and inflammation; and activate ERK1/2/JNK/p38,
  promoting cell migration. Multi-pathway mechanisms synergistically exert anti-inflammatory,
  anti-oxidative, and anti-apoptotic effects, promoting cellular migration and proliferation.
