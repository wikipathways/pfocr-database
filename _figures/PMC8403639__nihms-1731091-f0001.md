caption: Under basal conditions, NRF2 is bound to KEAP1, which is a CUL3-based E3
  ubiquitin ligase adapter that regulates NRF2 ubiquitination and proteasomal degradation.
  When cells are exposed to oxidative or electrophilic stress, KEAP1 changes its conformation
  and releases NRF2. Then NRF2 translocates into the nucleus, forms a heterodimer
  with its obligatory partner (small MAF proteins), binds to the ARE, and activates
  the transcription of downstream genes, such as antioxidative genes and phase II
  detoxification enzymes.
