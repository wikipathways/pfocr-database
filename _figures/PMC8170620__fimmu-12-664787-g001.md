caption: A brief summary of miRNA regulation of signaling pathways during M. tuberculosis
  infection. A schematic diagram represents different miRNAs and their target genes.
  MiR-708-5p and miR-1178 negatively regulates the level of TLR4 in macrophages. MiR-27b
  are induced by TLR2/MyD88/NF-κB pathway. MiR-125a and miR146a negatively regulate
  the NF-κB pathway by directly targeting TRAF6. MiR-132 and miR-26a down-regulate
  the transcriptional coactivator p300 (a molecule involved in IFN-γ signaling), miR-Let-7f
  targets A20, a feedback inhibitor of the NF-κB pathway. MiR-99b inhibit the activation
  of NF-κB, miR-196b-5p activate STAT3 signaling pathway via targeting negative regulators
  SOCS3.  Direct stimulatory modification;  Direct inhibitory modification.
