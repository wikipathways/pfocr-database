caption: TNF-α-driven formation of necroptosis-specific protein complexes. Upon binding
  of tumor necrosis factor (TNF-α) to TNF receptor 1 (TNFR1), the presence of cIAP1
  and cIAP2 facilitates their association with TRADD, TRAF2, and other receptor-mediated
  protein kinases to form Complex I. This complex promotes the polyubiquitination
  of RIPK1, which then activates the NF-κB pathway, leading to the induction of anti-apoptotic
  and pro-inflammatory genes. Kinases such as IKKα/β/ϵ, TBK1, and TAK1 phosphorylate
  RIPK1. These phosphorylation events negatively regulate RIPK1 activity, thus inhibiting
  subsequent cell necrosis. Created in BioRender.com. Shi, Z. (2025) https://BioRender.com/k68l223
