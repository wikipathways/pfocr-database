caption: Mechanism of action of ZIP8 in macrophage polarisation and IDD. ZIP8 promotes
  M1 macrophage polarisation, leading to increased production of proinflammatory cytokines
  like IL‐1β, COX‐2 and iNOS. These cytokines induce IL‐1β in NP (nucleus pulposus)
  cells, which activates NF‐κB and Wnt/β‐catenin pathways. This results in increased
  expression of inflammatory mediators (IL‐6, TGF‐α), ECM‐degrading enzymes (ADAMTS5,
  MMP13) and apoptosis‐related proteins (Bax, caspase‐3), while reducing ECM components
  (aggrecan, COL II) and proliferation. These changes contribute to cell apoptosis,
  ECM degradation and IDD progression. IDD, intervertebral disc degeneration; ECM,
  extracellular matrix; NP, nucleus pulposus
