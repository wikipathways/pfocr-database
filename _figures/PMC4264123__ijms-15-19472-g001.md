caption: 'Hypothetical diagram of LPS-triggered inflammation and apoptosis pathways
  summarized in A. japonicus coelomocytes. Genes listed here play important roles
  in these potential pathways. On the left, three pathways will promote the expression
  of inflammation factors. The existence of the Type I type I interferon (IFN) pathway
  was unclear for the absence of IFN homologues in invertebrates. On the right, the
  apoptosis pathway will result in the degradation of DNA. The abbreviations: LPS,
  lipopolysaccharide; CCP, Complement control proteins; TRIF, TLR and interleukin-1
  receptor (TIR) domain-containing adaptor inducing IFN-β; Cyto C, Cytochrome C; TRAF,
  tumor necrosis factor (TNF)-receptor-associated factor; TBK1, TRAF family member-associated
  NF-κB activator (TANK)-binding kinase 1; IRF3/7, interferon regulatory factor 3/7;
  CD, cluster of differentiation; LBP, lipopolysaccharide binding protein; TLR, toll-like
  receptor; MyD88, myeloid differentiation primary response gene 88; FADD, Fas-associated
  death domain protein; IRAK1/4, interleukin-1 receptor-associated kinase 1/4; NF-κB,
  nuclear factor-κ-B.'
