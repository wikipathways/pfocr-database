caption: Regulation of GLI1 the isoforms by the canonical and noncanonical pathways.
  (A) Canonical activation of GLI1 is initiated by Shh, which binds and inhibits PTCH1
  relieving PTCH1-mediated suppression of SMO. Derepressed SMO releases GLI1 from
  SUFU-mediated cytoplasmic sequestration. In turn, GLI1 translocalizes to the nucleus
  to induce transcription of target genes. Dyrk1 enhances GLI1 activity. Both GLI1ΔN
  and tGLI1 respond to Shh; however, it is unknown whether they are subjected to the
  regulation by other components of the Hedgehog pathway. (B) GLI1 can be affected
  by several noncanonical pathways. Activators of GLI1 include TGF-β, K-Ras, PKC-α,
  PDGF-A, PI3K-AKT, and estradiol. PKC-δ, PKA, and p53 suppress GLI1. The response
  of tGLI1 or GLI1ΔN to noncanonical pathway activation is currently unknown.
