caption: An activated tyrosine kinase receptor induces class IA PI3K activation, which
  in turn phosphorylates the lipid phosphatidyl-inositol bisphosphate (PIP2) to generate
  phosphatidyl-inositol triphoshate (PIP3). PIP3 recruits PDK1 and AKT to the plasma
  membrane, where PDK1 phosphorylates AKT on Thr308. The phosphorylation of AKT on
  Ser473 is mediated by PDK2 and controls AKT activity. AKT phosphorylates a wide
  variety of substrates, including FOXO transcription factors, Bad, p27kip1, p21Cip,
  GSK-3β, MDM2, and TSC2. AKT-driven TSC1/TSC2 complex inactivation leads to the accumulation
  of GTP-bound Rheb. Rheb-GTP then activates mTORC1. mTORC1 regulates protein synthesis
  by regulating p70S6K and 4E-BP1. In this pathway, PTEN phosphatase acts as a negative
  regulator through dephosphorylation of PIP3 at the 3’position.
