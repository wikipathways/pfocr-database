caption: Nonlinear signaling through the PI3K-Akt pathway. Growth factors activate
  receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs) that further activate parallel growth pathways.
  The MAPK pathway, which signals via Ras and downstream effectors, is an example
  of a linear growth-promoting pathway. The PI3K-Akt pathway is an example of a nonlinear
  pathway that signals through protein and lipid effectors and through layers of oncoproteins
  (red) and growth suppressors (blue). Negative feedback loops of the PI3K-Akt pathway
  are shown in green (repressing) or orange (activating). The effectors of the pathway
  whose alterations show combinatorial additive effects with PTEN inactivation in
  cancers are encircled.
