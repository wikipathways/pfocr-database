caption: The proposed hypothesis illustrates the potential mechanism of radiotherapy
  augmenting CD8+ T cells against NSCLC. Irradiation (IR) activates the cGAS-STING
  signaling pathway through the broken DNA fragments, resulting in IFNα and CXCL9
  overexpression and secretion. The secreted cytokines increase IFNγ expression and
  recruit CD8+ T cells through CXCR3 receptor homing to the tumor microenvironment.
  The in111 labeled PD-1-antibody was used to detect CD8+ T cells homing to tumors.
  CD8+ T cells contact A549 cells to elicit autocrine IL2-mediated GZMB and PRF1 expression,
  resulting in tumor apoptosis. Particularly, PBMCs co-injected with A549 significantly
  suppressed tumor growth in the A549-derived tumor xenografts in vivo.
