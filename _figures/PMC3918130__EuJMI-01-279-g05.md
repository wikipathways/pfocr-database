caption: 'Adenovirus elicits IFN-αβ production by a novel viral induction pathway.
  Left: Type-I IFN induction pathways by viruses other than Ads. Recognition of viral
  RNAs or DNA in the cytoplasm leads to IFN-αβ production via the RNA helicases RIG-I/MDA-5
  and the DNA sensor DAI requiring IRF3 in any infected cell type. Alternatively,
  in dendritic cells and macrophages, endosomal TLR3 or TLR7-9 activation leads to
  the induction of IFN-αβ by activating IRF3 or IRF7, respectively. Right: Adenoviruses
  are recognized in mononuclear phagocytes after endosomal escape in the cytoplasm
  by a so far unknown receptor requiring JNK MAP kinases and IRF7. This activates
  type-I IFN production independent of TLRs, RIG-I/MDA-5, DAI, and IRF3. Ad-induced
  IFN-αβ production is inhibited by viral early gene expression'
