  endothelium. R. rickettsii (Rr) infection of endothelial cells induces breakdown
  of adherens junctions and accumulation of cytoplasmic β-catenin which may drive
  pathway activity (left). R. conorii (Rc) infection leads to a suppression of secreted
  Wnt antagonist DKK1, which may facilitate activation of Wnt signaling to induce
  an anti-inflammatory environment during infection. IL-6 and IL-8 secretion are suppressed
  during infection in a DKK1-dependent manner.
