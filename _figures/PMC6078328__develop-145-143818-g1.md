caption: Simplified schematic of the ERK pathway. Growth factors activate the ERK
  pathway by binding to transmembrane receptor tyrosine kinases (RTKs). The RTKs signal
  to the membrane-tethered GTPase Ras, which then activates the core phosphorylation
  cascade from the kinase Raf to mitogen-activated protein kinase kinase (MAP2K or
  MEK), to extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK). ERK can translocate to the
  nucleus, where it interacts with transcription factors to regulate target gene expression.
