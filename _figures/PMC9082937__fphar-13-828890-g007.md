caption: Schematic indicating that moderate increase bilirubin can inhibit doxorubicin-induced
  gap junction’ dysfunction and conduction abnormalities via regulating AMPK-Axl-SOCS3
  signaling axis. Doxorubicin significantly increases the phosphorylation of JNK and
  Cx43 in mouse cardiomyocytes, leading to gap junction’ dysfunction and conduction
  abnormalities and causing cardiotoxicity. Bilirubin induces SOCS3 expression in
  an AMPK-Axl-dependent manner, inhibiting JNK-Cx43 signaling pathway to improve gap
  junction’ function and conduction abnormalities.
