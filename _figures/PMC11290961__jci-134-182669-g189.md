caption: Kisspeptin stimulation of kisspeptin receptors on astrocytes decreases their
  production of prostaglandin E2 (PGE2). PGE2 is a stimulatory input onto GnRH neurons.
  Consequently, the kisspeptin-astrocyte signaling pathway constrains kisspeptin-induced
  stimulation of GnRH neurons. Kisspeptin neurons in the arcuate nucleus of the hypothalamus
  are responsible for pulsatile GnRH/LH secretion and respond to negative feedback
  from sex steroids. Kisspeptin neurons in the preoptic nucleus of the hypothalamus
  respond to positive feedback from higher levels of sex steroids to induce the midcycle
  ovulatory LH surge in females. FSH, Follicle Stimulating Hormone; LH, Luteinizing
  Hormone; GnRH, Gonadotropin Releasing Hormone; KNDy, Kisspeptin Neurokinin B, Dynorphin
