caption: Class I PI3K signaling pathway simplified. Class I PI3Ks reside as dimers
  in the cells. After receiving an external stimulus, the regulatory p85 subunit interacts
  with the intracellular section of the activated receptor. The activation of a catalytic
  p110 isoform triggers a lipid membrane-associated cascade of phosphorylations (PIP2
  to PIP3). The PI3K pathway is mediated mainly by PIP3, which is a secondary messenger
  that acts as a docking site. In the PI3K-AKT pathway, PIP3 can bind to both downstream
  effector proteins PDK1 and AKT. AKT modification activates the mammalian target
  of rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1) by direct phosphorylation, which results in synthesis
  of growth-, proliferation-, and survival-related proteins ().
