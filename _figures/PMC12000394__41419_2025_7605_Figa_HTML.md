caption: Molecular mechanisms of HBsAg suppression of the IFNβ signaling pathway and
  triggering of early autophagy. HBsAg targets the kinase domain of TBK1, thereby
  disrupting the TBK1–IRF3 complex and inhibiting type I interferon production. On
  the other hand, HBsAg enhances TBK1 dimerization and phosphorylation, which upregulates
  the phosphorylation of p62 to induce p62-mediated autophagy. Furthermore, HBV infection
  causes the accumulation of autophagosomes. This is achieved by HBsAg suppressing
  the SNAP29 promoter activity, which blocks autophagosome–lysosome fusion
