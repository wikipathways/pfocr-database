caption: Immune checkpoint proteins impair proliferation and multi-functionality of
  T cells. Interaction between immune checkpoint markers and their ligands transmits
  inhibitory signals to CD4+ and CD8+ T cells. These inhibitory signals limit TCR-
  and CD28-mediated co-stimulatory pathway, including the NF-κB, NFAT, and AP-1 signaling
  pathways, which suppress proliferation, cytokine production, and cytotoxic response.
  IC blockers are able to cause ligand dissociation from the ICs and restore T cell
  activation.
