caption: Activation of PI3K leads to phosphorylation of the p85 subunit of the PI3K
  heterodimer, which subsequently activates the catalytic subunit, p110. This phosphorylated
  heterodimer catalyses the conversion of PIP2 into PIP3. PIP3 mediates the activation
  of AKT via phosphorylation by PDK-1 at threonine T308 and PDK-2 (mTOR2) at serine
  residue S473, for maximal AKT activation. Activated AKT then mediates the activation
  of mTOR, which results in cellular growth, survival, angiogenesis and proliferation.
