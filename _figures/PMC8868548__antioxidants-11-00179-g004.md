caption: The heme oxygenase pathway signaling during exercise. Exercising (aerobic)
  raises plasma bilirubin levels by (1) suppression of the glucuronyl transferase
  enzyme UGT1A1 that conjugates bilirubin for removal from blood, and (2) activation
  of the heme oxygenase pathway (HO-1-BVRA-PPARα). The increased bilirubin combats
  reactive oxygen species (ROS) and ROS-induced inflammation and DNA damage. The bilirubin
  also activates the nuclear receptor transcription factor, PPARα, to reduce adiposity.
