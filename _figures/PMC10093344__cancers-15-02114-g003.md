caption: Tumor immunosuppressive mechanism of AIM2 in TIDCs. Upon recognizing the
  tumor-derived DNA taken up into the TIDC cytoplasm, AIM2 induces the production
  of IL-1β and IL-18, which in turn promote the intratumoral infiltration of regulatory
  T-cells (Tregs). Additionally, AIM2 suppresses STING type I IFN signaling, a pathway
  that promotes intratumoral infiltration of CD8+ T-cells and suppresses that of Tregs.
