caption: 'Signaling cascades mediated by 8-PN in tissues and cells (BioRender.com,
  license purchased). First panel: 8-PN affected steroidogenesis through cAMP-dependent
  pathway in primary culture of porcine Leydig cells []. Second panel: in mouse skeletal
  muscle, 8-PN stimulated the PI3K/Akt signaling pathway at AS160, which triggers
  GLUT4 translocation to plasma membrane []. Third panel: 8-PN at physiological concentrations
  activated the PI3K/Akt/P70S6K1 pathway in mouse myotubes and accelerated muscle
  recovery from disuse atrophy []. Fourth panel: In mice, 8-PN activated AMPK, which
  plays a pivotal role in lipid and glucose metabolism in muscle and liver []. Fifth
  panel: 8-PN is a potential ligand for the human farnesoid X receptor (FXR), based
  on collective findings from fluorescence titration, molecular docking studies and
  hydrogen deuterium exchange mass spectrometry []. Sixth panel: 8-PN directly activated
  the inhibitory NO/cGMP/PKG/VASP pathway in human platelets []. Seventh panel: 8-PN
  rapidly activated ERK1/2 MAP kinase in MCF-7 cells [].'
