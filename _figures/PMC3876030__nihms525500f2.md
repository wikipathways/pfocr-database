  M1–M5. The seven transmembrane domains of the family A G-protein-coupled receptors
  (GPCRs) are highlighted in blue. The orthosteric binding site is indicated in red.
  A putative allosteric site is illustrated in green and not indicative of a singular
  or unique site. The downstream effectors indicate M1signaling related to the (a)
  amyloidogenic precursor protein (APP) and subsequent secretase activity to generate
  soluble amyloidogenic precursor protein alpha (sAPPα), soluble amyloidogenic precursor
  protein beta (sAPPβ) and Aβ. (b) NMDAR through the CAKβ and SFK pathway. Abbreviations:
  AC, adenylyl cyclase; ATP, adenosine triphosphate; cAMP, cyclic adenosine monophosphate;
  ERK1/2, extracellular signal-regulated kinase; IP3, inositol triphosphate; PKC,
  protein kinase C; PLC, phospholipase C, CAKβ, cell adhesion kinase β; SFK, Src family
  kinases.'
