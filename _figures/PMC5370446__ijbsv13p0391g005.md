caption: A proposed model that α-MSH activates mTORC1 and protects against H2O2-Induced
  loss of dendrites in human skin melanocytes. Extracellular H2O2 activates p38 and
  NFκB leading to apoptosis and loss of dendrites. α-MSH activates mTORC1 pathway
  via cell surface receptor MC1R which is blocked by rapamycin, leading to protein
  synthesis, cell survival and more dendrites. TNFα activates cell surface receptor
  which induces IκB phosphorylation and degradation. This results in translocation
  of NF-κB subunits p65 and p50 from cytoplasm to nucleus, and expression of more
  cytokines, apoptosis, and loss of dendrites. TNFα may also induce intracellular
  H2O2 production that activates NF-κB pathway.
