caption: Opposing influences on Foxo cellular localization and transcriptional activity.
  A major pathway of Foxo regulation consists of growth factor–mediated nuclear exclusion
  and inactivation (top right). Many tumors have been found to contain phosphorylated
  Akt or inflammation-driven phosphorylated IKKβ as a mechanism of inactivating Foxo
  tumor-suppressive activity. In addition, a major pathway of Ras-mediated transformation
  is the Erk-mediated phosphorylation of Foxo (bottom right), which results in its
  MDM2-mediated ubiquitination and degradation. Opposing these inactivating pathways
  is oxidative stress (top left), which results in either Jnk- or MST1-mediated Foxo
  phosphorylation. Nuclear Foxo transcriptional specificity can be altered by SIRT1-mediated
  deacetylation, which results in a prosurvival program of gene expression. OGT, O-linked
  β-N-acetylglucosamine transferase; PIP2, phosphatidylinositol-4, 5-bisphosphate;
  PIP3, phosphatidylinositol-3,4,5-trisphosphate.
