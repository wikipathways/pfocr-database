caption: Modules for cofactor balancing and phosphorylation. Modules of (A) ATP regeneration
  by polyphosphate, (B) direct phosphorylation of starch by inorganic phosphate (C)
  direct phosphorylation of glucose by polyphosphate, (D) direct phosphorylation of
  xylulose by polyphosphate, (E) NAD+ regeneration by water-forming NADH oxidase,
  (F) the purge valve for NADP+/NADPH, (G) NAD(P)H regeneration by phosphite, and
  (H) NAD+ salvage synthesis. Red, blue, and orange boxes indicate the input, output,
  and the substrate required for the module. PPK, polyphosphate kinase; polyPn, polyphosphate;
  GP, glucan phosphorylase; PGM, phosphoglucomutase; G1P, glucose 1-phosphate; G6P,
  glucose 6-phosphate; PGK, polyphosphate-dependent glucokinase; XI, xylose isomerase;
  PXK, polyphosphate-dependent xylulokinase; Xyl, xylose; Xlu, xylulose; Xu5P, xylulose
  5-phosphate; NOX, NADH oxidase; PDH, phosphite dehydrogenase; NAM, nicotinamide;
  ADPR, adenosine diphosphate ribose. ENZNAD+ and ENZNAPD+ indicates enzymes that
  use NAD+ or NADP+ as a cofactor; m indicates the time of NADH and NADPH regeneration;
  n indicates the time of NADPH consumption downstream of the module.
