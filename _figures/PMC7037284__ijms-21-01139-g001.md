caption: 'Guanylyl cyclase-cGMP signaling pathway. The guanylyl cyclase (GC) catalyzes
  the production of cGMP from GTP. NO, NO donors, and sGC stimulators activate the
  soluble GC (sGC), a heterodimer with a heme prosthetic group in its reduced iron
  (Fe2+) state, whereas the sGC activators activate the heme-free sGC, independent
  of NO. The natriuretic peptides (NPs) as well as NP analogs activate the particulate
  GC (pGC). The membrane-permeable cGMP analogs lead directly to an increase of the
  intracellular cGMP level. Cyclic GMP binds to cGMP-dependent protein kinases (PKGs),
  cGMP-gated ion channels (CGCs), and cyclic nucleotide-regulated phosphodiesterases
  (PDEs), which modulate several downstream cellular and physiological responses.
  PDE5 is a cGMP-specific PDE that inhibits the degradation of cGMP to 5′-GMP. MRP4/5
  act as exporters for cGMP. Abbreviations are: ANP, atrial natriuretic peptide; BNP,
  B-type natriuretic peptide; CNP, C-type natriuretic peptide; MEP4/5, multidrug resistance
  proteins 4 and 5; NO, nitric oxide.'
