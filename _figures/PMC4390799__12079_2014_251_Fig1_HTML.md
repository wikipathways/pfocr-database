caption: Potential for therapeutic modulation of the UPR. Strong ER stress and activation
  of the UPR triggers apoptosis. In contrast, mild UPR activation induces a beneficial
  ER hormetic response by reducing the load of misfolded proteins and by activating
  cellular protective mechanisms such as an antioxidant response and autophagy. The
  PERK–ATF4 pathway plays a key role in ER adaptation. PERK phosphorylates Nrf2, which
  promotes transcription of antioxidant genes. PERK mediates phosphorylation of eIF2α
  and ATF4-dependent transcriptional activation of autophagy genes. ATF6-dependent
  induction of XBP1 is also important for ER adaptation and is required for cytoprotection
  via induction of autophagy. Specific degradation of death receptor 5 (DR5) mRNA
  by RIDD contributes to ER adaptation and cytoprotection
