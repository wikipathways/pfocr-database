caption: The ABA biosynthesis pathway from zeaxanthin to xanthoxin. Synthesis of trans-violaxanthin
  (t-viola) is catalyzed by zeaxanthin epoxidase (ZEP). The formation of cis-isomers
  of c-viola and c-neo is thought to require two enzymes, NSY to produce trans-neoxanthin
  (t-neo) and an isomerase for trans-to-cis conversion of both neoxanthin and violaxanthin.
  Cleavage of cis-xanthophylls is catalyzed by a family of NCEDs and produces xanthoxin,
  the 15-carbon precursor of ABA.
