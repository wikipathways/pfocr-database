caption: Proposed functions of Spry1 and Spry2 in regulation of eyelid closure. Spry1
  and Spry2 a) redundantly and negatively modulate FGF signaling in the conjunctival
  epithelial cells to suppress cell proliferation, b) mediate eyelid epithelial-mesenchymal
  communication (reflected by reduced Shh and increased Wnt signaling in Spry CKO
  eyelids) and c) regulate peridermal cell motility (ERK-independent modulation of
  phospho-c-Jun levels and F actin polymerization). The FGF signaling pathway modulated
  by Sprys is likely to be initiated by FGF10 from the eyelid mesenchyme.
