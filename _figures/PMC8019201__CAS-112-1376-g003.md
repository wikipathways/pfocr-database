caption: The molecular mechanisms of the role of enhancer of zeste homolog 2 (EZH2)
  in a polycomb repressive complex 2 (PRC2)‐independent manner. A, EZH2 acts as a
  transcriptional coactivator by forming complexes with other transcription factors.
  B, EZH2 acts as a transcription factor via directly binding to the target genes’
  promoter. C, The phosphorylation of EZH2 turns it from a chromatin silencer to a
  transcriptional coactivator. D, EZH2 can methylate or phosphorylate nonhistone substrates
  to regulate gene silencing and transactivation in a PRC2‐dependent or ‐independent
  manner. E, EZH2 binds to internal ribosome entry site 1 (IRES1) of p53 mRNA to promote
  the expression of p53 protein. F, EZH2 forms a complex with long noncoding RNA (lncRNA)
  ANRIL and p65 to activate the NF‐κB pathway
