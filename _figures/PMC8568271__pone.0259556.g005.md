caption: Upon ligand binding TRAF6 binds to the Interleukin-1 receptor. Ajuba recruits
  p62 andFIG atypical protein kinase C (aPKC). aPKC phosphorylates the inhibitory
  kB kinase B, leading to its degradation and the release of NF-kB to affect gene
  transcription. NF-kB induces expression of p62 and the de-ubiquitination enzyme
  CYLD. This provides negative feedback to the pathway by de-ubiquitinating TRAF6,
  a process that is dependent on the UBA domain of p62. As such, the expression of
  p62 UBA domain mutant proteins does not provide negative feedback to the pathway
  and NF-kB remains activated.
