caption: Activation of NF-κB by CagA released by H. pylori. Multiple activation pathways
  are CagA dependent; activation of growth factor receptor-bound protein 2 (GRB2)
  leads to Ras/Raf activation, which in turn activates NF-κB. This pathway can also
  occur via P21-activated kinase 1 (PAK1) []. NF-κB activation can also occur through
  c-met and phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase (PI3K) upstream activation, as well as via
  tumour necrosis factor receptor-associated factor 6 (TRAF6) and transforming growth
  factor-β-activated kinase 1 (TAK1). The TRAF6/TAK1 pathway can be activated by H.
  pylori liposaccharide (LPS) activation of toll-like receptor 4 (TLR4). Figure adapted
  from “NF-κB Signalling Pathway”, by BioRender.com (2021). Retrieved from https://app.biorender.com/biorender-templates
