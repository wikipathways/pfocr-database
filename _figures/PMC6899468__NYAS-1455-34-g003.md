caption: 'Schematic diagram showing the signaling pathways used by CST to provide
  cardioprotection. CST‐induced cardioprotection begins by the activation of G protein–coupled
  or cytokine receptors and the consequent recruitment of signaling pathways: (1)
  the reperfusion injury salvage kinase (RISK) pathway, including PI‐3K–AKT, ERK1/2,
  and the downstream target glycogen synthase kinase 3 beta (GSK‐3β); and (2) the
  PKG pathways. These salvage pathways activate downstream mediators, such as endothelial
  nitric oxide synthase (eNOS), GSK‐3β, hexokinase II (HKII), protein kinase C‐epsilon
  (PKCε), the mitochondrial ATP–dependent potassium channel (KATP) with consequent
  inhibition of mitochondrial permeability transition pore (MPTP). PDK1, pyruvate
  dehydrogenase kinase 1; ROS, reactive oxygen species.'
