caption: Wnt/β-Catenin Pathway. When the WNT ligands bind to the LRP5/6 and FZD (left),
  the phosphorylation and degradation of β-catenin are inhibited, and then β-catenin
  accumulates and translocates to the nucleus, where it partners with TCF to activate
  Wnt-responsive genes. In the absence of WNT ligands (right), cytoplasmic β-catenin
  forms a complex with several proteins, leading to proteasomal degradation. As a
  result, β-catenin levels remain low in the cytoplasm, inhibiting target gene transcription.
