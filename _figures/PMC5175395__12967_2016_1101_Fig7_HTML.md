caption: The role of SIRT1/PGC-1α repression in the pathophysiology of AMD. SIRT1
  deacetylates and activates PGC-1α. AMPK increases SIRT1 activity and directly phosphorylates
  and activates PGC-1α. The reduction in SIRT1 activity would reduce deacetylation
  rate of PGC-1α. Hyperacetylation decreases PGC-1α activity which translates to lower
  mitochondrial content and activity, lowered mitochondrial respiratory capacity,
  lowered ROS detoxification and increased ROS production, contributing to the pathophysiology
  of AMD. Ac acetylation, p phosphorylation
