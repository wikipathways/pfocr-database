caption: Translational control in antidepressant action. Antidepressant treatment
  regulates translation through the posttranslational modifications of the components
  in translation regulatory pathway. Chronic treatment of fluoxetine triggers the
  activation of the mTOR signaling pathway (upward arrow) leading to phosphorylation
  of 4E-BPs and S6K1. A single treatment of a subanesthetic dose of ketamine suppresses
  eEF2K activity (downward arrow) to increase protein synthesis, including BDNF, through
  dephosphorylation of eEF2.
