caption: 1,25(OH)2D3-induced signaling pathways involved in the regulation of cell
  proliferation, apoptosis, and inflammation in cancer. 1,25(OH)2D3 hampers the transition
  from the G1 to the S phase of the cell cycle either directly, through upregulation
  of different cyclin-dependent kinase inhibitors, or indirectly through the induction
  of other growth factors (e.g., TGF-β, EGF). In addition, 1,25(OH)2D3 induces apoptosis
  through activation of the intrinsic apoptotic pathway or by interference with other
  signaling pathways such as TNF-α, EGF, β-catenin, and prostaglandins. 1,25(OH)2D3
  has also an immunosuppressive activity, as indicated by the repression of NFκB-mediated
  gene transcription, which results in a suppressed production of inflammatory cytokines,
  such as IL-1, IL-6, IL-8, and TNF-α.
