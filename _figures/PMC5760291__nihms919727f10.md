caption: The intracellular bacteria Brucella abortus enters the host cell and ensures
  its survival by forming the Brucella-containing vacuole (BCV). Initially, the recognition
  of bacterial c-di-GMP activates STING and triggers type I interferon response and
  upregulation of NOS2 and GBPs expression. GBPs promote lysis of the BCV by exposing
  bacterial components, like bacterial DNA in the cytosol, thus enabling activation
  of AIM2 and IL-1β secretion. Additionally, release of Brucella genomic DNA in the
  cytosol may activate cGAS generating cGAMP (dotted line) resulting in further amplification
  of type I IFN signaling pathway.
