caption: The human galactose metabolic pathway. Exogenous and endogenously produced
  α-D-galactose is phosphorylated by galactokinase (GALK) to form galactose-1-phosphate
  (gal-1-p) (highlighted). In the presence of galactose-1-phosphate uridyltransferase
  (GALT), galactose-1-phosphate will react with uridine diphosphoglucose (UDP-glucose)
  to form glucose-1-phosphate and uridine diphosphogalactose (UDP-galactose). UDP-galactose
  can also be formed from UDP-glucose via the UDP galactose-4′-epimerase (GALE) reaction.
  ATP, adenosine triphosphate; ADP, adenosine diphosphate.
