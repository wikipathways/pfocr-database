caption: Mechanisms of Annexin A1 in inflammation. Annexin A1, glucocorticoid-induced
  synthesis in neutrophils, activates the MAPK/ERK signaling pathway through FPRs
  and reduces neutrophil activity to inhibit inflammatory responses. Externalization
  of Annexin A1 promotes clearance of apoptotic cells and stimulates T cell proliferation
  and differentiation, contrary to the action of glucocorticoids. ICAM1 was released
  through endothelial cells induced by cleaved Annexin A1 to enhance the transendothelial
  migration capacity of neutrophils and promote the inflammatory response.
