caption: Schematic diagram of hydrogen sulfide (H2S) regulating mesenchymal stem cell
  (MSC) function. H2S is physiologically generated by cystathionine beta-synthase
  (CBS) and cystathionine gamma-lyase (CSE) in MSCs. The levels of endogenous or exogenous
  H2S affect sulfhydration of calcium channels to regulate WNT/β-catenin-mediated
  osteogenic mast gene Runx2, controlling MSC differentiation and bone homeostasis.
  H2S also regulates MSC survival and proliferation via alteration of mitochondrial
  function and the PI3K/Akt pathway. TRP, transient receptor potential.
