caption: Schematic representation of the Hippo signaling pathway-YAP/TAZ in human
  endothelial cells. When the Hippo signaling pathway is active/on (Left), multiple
  upstream signals regulate the phosphorylation of MST1/MST2, LATS1/LATS2 kinases,
  and phosphorylates YAP/TAZ proteins. Phosphorylation of YAP/TAZ recruits 14-3-3
  proteins that stimulate cytoplasmic retention or proteolytic degradation. When Hippo
  signaling pathway is inactive/off (Right), YAP/TAZ are not phosphorylated, localize
  to the nucleus, form a complex with transcription factor TEADs and regulate genes
  required for endothelial cell proliferation, migration and survival.
