caption: Signaling of the phosphatase and tensin homolog deleted on chromosome ten
  (PTEN)/PI3K/Akt pathway. PTEN converts phosphatidylinositol (3,4,5)-triphosphate
  (PIP3) into phosphatidylinositol (4,5)-bisphosphate (PIP2), thus inhibiting Akt
  activation and peripheral nerve regeneration. Activation of PI3K and Akt signals
  through deletion of PTEN [BPV (pic) or siRNA] increases regeneration of axons in
  injury. These impacts appear to be independent of mammalian target of rapamycin
  (mTOR) but require the activity of PI3K and Akt (Duraikannu, original illustration).
