caption: IRFs Respond to TLR Signaling and Participate in the Expression and Function
  of IFNs. The basal expression of IRF1 maintains the expression of ISGs, thereby
  conferring antiviral activity. Following viral infection, higher expression of IRF1
  is triggered, along with potential cytokines, inducing the expression of IFN and
  ISGs. Additionally, the secretion of IFN can activate the STAT1-STAT2-IRF9 complex,
  inducing IRF1 and IRF7 as part of a positive feedback loop. The binding of PAMPs
  with TLR activates the IRFs signaling pathway, promoting the expression of IFN,
  pro-inflammatory cytokines, and chemokines, thereby activating the host’s innate
  immune defense
