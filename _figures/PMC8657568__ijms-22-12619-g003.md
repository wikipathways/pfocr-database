caption: The calcium signal pathway has a wide range of regulatory effects on dehydrin
  expression. Sensors in the plasma membrane are stimulated by abiotic stress and
  promote the production of the secondary messenger Ca2+. Changes in calcium concentration
  are perceived by the calcium sensor proteins CDPKs, CBLs, CMLs, and CaMs. The latter
  either participate in the ABA signaling pathway or initiate phosphorylation/dephosphorylation
  cascade reactions of TFs such as CAMTAs and CBFs that eventually participate in
  dehydrin gene activation. CRLK1/2 regulates COR in the upstream of the MEKK1-MKK2-MPK4
  cascade pathway. We use arrows and bars showing positive and negative adjustment,
  respectively.
