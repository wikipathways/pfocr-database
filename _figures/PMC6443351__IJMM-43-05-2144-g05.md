caption: Schematic view of Scrophularia buergeriana extract (SBE) regulating reactive
  oxygen species (ROS) levels and the apoptotic signaling cascade. Excessive glutamate
  caused ROS generation and induces neuronal cell apoptosis. The generated ROS triggers
  p38 and Bcl-2-associated X protein (Bax) protein and damage to mitochondrial function.
  The activated caspase-3 and poly (adenosine diphosphate (ADP)-ribose) polymerase
  (PARP) proteins result in apoptosis. SBE treatment reduces the production of ROS
  by regulating the expression of enzymes, including superoxide dismutase (SOD)1,
  SOD2 and glutathione peroxidase-1 (GPx-1) and also inhibits the apoptotic pathway.
