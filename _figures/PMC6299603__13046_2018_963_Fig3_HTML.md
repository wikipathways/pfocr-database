caption: Altered gut microbiota is responsible for pancreatic cancer progression.
  a. High-fat diet intake alters the gut microbiota composition. Altered gut microbiota
  secrete lipopolysaccharides (LPS), which enters circulation by damaging intestinal
  tight junction proteins. Circulatory LPS then binds to the toll like receptor on
  immune cells to recruit MyD88 or TRIF adaptor molecules. These molecules further
  activate MAPK and NF-κB pathways to activate  several inflammatory cytokines, leading
  to cancer cell proliferation. b. Short-chain fatty acids (SCFAs) are released from
  resistant starch by the gut microbiota that enters the circulation. Afterward, SCFAs
  bind to G-protein-coupled receptors to activate the MAPK signaling pathway, triggering
  cancer cell proliferation.
