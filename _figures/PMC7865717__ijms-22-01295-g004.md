caption: Proposed model of resveratrol action during oxidative stress in glaucoma.
  Trans-resveratrol (RSV) activates sirtuin-1 (Sirt-1), which leads to the phosphorylation
  of Akt and inhibits Bax activity. RSV also activates the AMPK pathway, which leads
  to mitochondrial DNA transcription and replication. RSV is also able to alter Oma-1
  and Yeme-1 mRNA expression, leading to the alteration of the L-Opa-1/S-Opa-1 (long-optical
  autophagy 1/short optical autophagy) ratio. In addition, RSV also lowers ROS levels
  not only by activating the scavenger pathway, but also by facilitating the translocation
  of Nrf2 into the nucleus, thereby favoring interaction between Nfr2 and the antioxidant
  response element (ARE) leading to HO- production.
