caption: Crucial role of EGF signalling augments healthy lifespan in worms. EGF signalling
  triggers the highly conserved RAF/MAPK pathway to regulate the expression of SKR-5
  and Hsp16 through the EOR-1/2 transcription factors. SKR-5 promotes the ubiquitination
  of substrates through the Cullin1 ligase complex and targets them for degradation
  by 26S proteasomes. On the other hand, activation of PLC-γ leads to the release
  of intracellular calcium from the ER, which contributes to healthy lifespan. Binding
  of EGF to its receptor can be regulated by putative EGF-binding proteins HPA-1/2.
  Inactivation of mTOR and autophagy induction can also promote lifespan. Several
  components of EGFR and RAS pathways are avoided for simplicity.
