caption: metabolic pathways in cancer cells. Highly proliferating cells promote glucose
  catabolism and glutamine catabolism by regulating key metabolic pathways, driving
  molecular synthesis and maintaining energy balance through molecular interactions.
  Cancer cells use aerobic glycolysis to produce ATP and promote pyruvate synthesis
  to promote glycolysis by increasing the expression of glycolytic enzymes. Tumor
  cells also produce macromolecules such as NADPH and 5-carbon sugar-driven nucleic
  acids via the pentose phosphate pathway. The citrate from the TCA cycle is exported
  to the cytosol and further converted to acetyl-CoA for the synthesis of lipid acids.
