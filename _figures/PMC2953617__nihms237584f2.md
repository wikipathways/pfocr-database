caption: JS genes implicated in the Shh, Wnt, and PDGFα signaling pathway. ARL13B
  and INPP5E localize to the cilium, whereas AHI1 and CEP290 localize to the basal
  body. CEP290, a centrosomal protein, is involved in Rab8 contained vesicle transport
  and ciliogenesis through its interaction with PCM-1. The disruption of ARL13B causes
  defects in ciliary structure and Shh signaling. ARL13B is required for body axis
  formation, renal function, and neural tube patterning. INPP5E plays an essential
  role in stabilizing cilia in response to serum stimulation, possibly by controlling
  PDGFα signaling. AHI1 interacts with and facilitates β-catenin (β-Cat) accumulation
  in the nucleus to positively modulate Wnt signaling. Wnt/β-catenin signaling via
  AHI1 is essential for maintaining renal homeostasis.
