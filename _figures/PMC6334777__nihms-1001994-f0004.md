caption: As shown in Panel A, canonical MAPK signaling transduces extracellular signal
  through receptor tyrosine kinase (RTK), which activates Ras, then RAF, then MEK,
  and then extracellular signal-regulated kinase (ERK) proteins, which in turn regulate
  cell-specific nuclear targets and gene transcription programs. Activating mutations
  such as BRAF V600E drive constitutive ERK activation and downstream transcriptional
  targets, including BCL2L1 (up-regulated) and CCR7 (down-regulated). The pie chart
  in Panel B shows the proportions of cases with specific activating MAPK mutations
  in a primarily pediatric series from one center.
