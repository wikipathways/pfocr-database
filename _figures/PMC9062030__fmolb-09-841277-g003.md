caption: The main forms of circulating cardiospecific troponins (). Description. The
  troponin complex is released from damaged cardiomyocytes (for example, in necrosis)
  in various molecular forms. The triple complex (TnT-TnI-TnC) degrades into a binary
  complex and free cardiospecific Tns. Then the binary complex also degrades into
  free Tns and troponin fragments (both immunoreactive and non-immunoreactive fragments).
  Free cardiospecific Tns can be released from cardiomyocytes with minor injuries
  (psychoemotional stress and physical exertion) and then also degrade into free cardiospecific
  troponins (TnT and TnI) and their fragments ().
