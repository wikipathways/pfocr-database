caption: Schematic diagram. When the Wnt signal is weak, β‐catenin is incorporated
  in a destruction complex that contains glycogen synthase kinase 3 (GSK3), adenomatous
  polyposis coli (APC) and axin 1, which results in the phosphorylation of β‐catenin
  and its degradation. OSR1 can enhance the expression level of GSK3β and downregulates
  the activity of the Wnt signaling pathway. SOX9 enhances the transcription and expression
  level of β‐catenin. The high level of β‐catenin increases the formation of the β‐catenin‐TCF/LEF
  complex and the transcriptional activities of Wnt target genes. OSR1 suppresses
  the transcription of SOX9, indirectly reverses the activating effect of SOX9 on
  β‐catenin and the Wnt signaling pathway
