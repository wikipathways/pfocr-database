caption: Schematic overview on a striatal medium spiny neuron synapse. Dopaminergic
  signaling in striatal medium spiny neurons is mediated by the cAMP signaling pathway.
  Activation of D1-type dopamine receptors leads to activation of adenylyl cyclase
  5 and subsequent increase in cAMP levels, while activation of D2-type dopamine receptors
  results in inhibition of adenylyl cyclase 5 and reduced levels of cAMP. cAMP in
  turn modulates activity of the protein kinase A, which phosphorylates further downstream
  effectors including DARP-32 and CREB. Arrows indicate mutations in genes involved
  in postsynaptic dopaminergic signaling in striatal medium spiny neurons
