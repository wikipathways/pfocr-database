caption: PI3K/Akt/mTOR/ERK/NF-κB signalling axis pathway plays an important role in
  regulating a broad range of cellular functions, including cell survival, growth,
  proliferation, invasion, apoptosis, angiogenesis, cell cycle and migration. Gedunin
  and epalrestat inhibit ROS generation and AR expression in the SCC131 cell line,
  and the coactivation of ERK and Akt is coupled with IKK/NF-κB signalling abrogation.
  Epalrestat and gedunin influence subcellular localization and phosphorylation, which
  modulate the expression of transcription factors and key oncogenic signalling kinases.
  Gedunin and epalrestat inhibit AR-mediated ROS signalling, which leads to the upregulation
  of Bcl-2, Bcl-XL, p-Bad, survivin, MMP-2, MMP-9, cyclin D1, c-Myc, PCNA, VEGF, and
  VEGFR2 and downregulation of Bax, cytochrome c, Apaf1, caspase-3, caspase-9, PARP,
  TIMP-2, RECK and p21.
