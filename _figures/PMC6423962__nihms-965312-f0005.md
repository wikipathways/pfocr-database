caption: The cascade cyclization of squalene epoxide, catalyzed by lanosterol synthase
  (LSS), provides the first sterol, lanosterol. Processing of lanosterol to cholesterol
  can proceed via the Kandutsch-Russell and/or Bloch pathways, which use the same
  enzymes and process substrates that vary only in the presence or absence of the
  C24 double bond. Intermediates in blue have been confirmed in our GC/MS-based sterol
  profiling assay using authentic standards. Sterol 14-reductase activity in mouse
  is shared by two genes, TM7SF2 and LBR. Consistent with past reports (ref. 21),
  inhibition of sterol 14-reductase activity can lead to accumulation of the expected
  upstream intermediate (FF-MAS) or 14-dehydrozymostenol, also known as cholesta-8,14-dien-3-β-ol.
  Green indicates enzyme targets and small molecules whose inhibition promotes oligodendrocyte
  formation.
