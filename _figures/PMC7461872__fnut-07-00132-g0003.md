caption: Effects of Fve-type FIPs on the immune system, adapted from Hsu et al. ().
  Fve-type could enhance expression of cell adhesion molecules and regulate secretion
  of cytokines and chemokines on APCs (including DCs and macrophage) and T cells,
  which could also contribute to APCs and T cell reaction. Fve-type FIPs could up-regulate
  IL-2, IFN-γ, LT, TNF-α, IL-10, IL-12, IL-22, TGF-β, and down-regulate IL-4, IL-5,
  IL-13, IL-17 in T cells, which may contribute to anti-allergic and anti-inflammatory
  effects in combination with IgE downregulation, eosinophil inhibition and IgG2a
  induction. Mechanistically, T cell cytokine production (IL-2 and IFN-γ) has been
  described in the down-left part, including activation of signaling pathway and Ca2+
  flow.
