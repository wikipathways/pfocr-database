caption: Increased expression of N-cadherin stabilizes certain growth factor receptors
  (e.g., FGFR) to activate their downstream signaling pathways including MAPK/Erk
  and PI3K/Akt pathways through interacting with their extracellular domains in thyroid
  cancer cells. The activation of MAPK/Erk and PI3K/Akt cascades upregulates the EMT
  regulatory factors such as Twist, Snail and Slug. These transcription factors then
  promotes the process of EMT through transcriptionally regulating the EMT-associated
  markers such as E-cadherin, Vimentin and N-cadherin and MMPs production. Newly synthesized
  N-cadherin is subsequently translocated to cellular membrane where it plays a tumor-promoting
  function through interacting with some growth factor receptors. In addition, N-cadherinalso
  promotes cell cycle progression through modulating p16/Rb pathway via Erk-dependent
  or –independent mechanisms.
