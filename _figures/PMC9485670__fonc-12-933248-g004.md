caption: Model of oncogenic KIT signaling on intracellular compartments in GISTs.
  KIT is normally transported from the endoplasmic reticulum to the Golgi apparatus,
  followed by full glycosylation. After reacting with the Golgi apparatus, KIT can
  activate the PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway, MEK-Erk pathway, and STAT5. M-COPA inhibits
  oncogenic signaling by blocking the transport of KIT from the endoplasmic reticulum
  to the Golgi because KIT activates downstream molecules only on the Golgi apparatus.
