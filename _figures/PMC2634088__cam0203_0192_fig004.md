caption: Functional Interactions of NG2. On the apical cell surface, the thr-2314
  phosphorylated NG2 proteoglycan (green oval) activates α3β1 integrin (yellow dimer)
  signaling to promote enhanced proliferation. NG2 also promotes cell proliferation
  via potentiation of growth factor/growth factor receptor signaling (indicated here
  by red FGF and blue FGFR dimer). On leading edge lamellipodia, thr-2256 phosphorylated
  NG2 activates α3β1 integrin signaling to promote enhanced motility. NG2-mediated
  integrin signaling can also enhance cell survival via the PI3/AKT pathway. NG2 also
  provides a linkage between the cell surface and the extracellular matrix via its
  interaction with type VI collagen (triple turqouise rods). Signaling pathways are
  shown in abbreviated format due to space considerations.
