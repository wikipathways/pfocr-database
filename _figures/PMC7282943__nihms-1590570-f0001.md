caption: A simplified schematic of the TGFβ signaling pathway. The TGFβ receptor complex
  is a heterotetramer of TGFβRI and TGFβRII dimers. Upon TGFβ ligand binding, the
  TGFβ receptor phosphorylates Smad2 and Smad3, which then form a complex with Smad4
  that localizes to the nucleus, binds to SBEs at genetic loci and regulates downstream
  transcription. Smad7 is a genetic product of canonical TGFβ signaling that inhibits
  this process. Additionally, the TGFβR complex can activate non-canonical signaling
  pathways independently of Smad proteins.
