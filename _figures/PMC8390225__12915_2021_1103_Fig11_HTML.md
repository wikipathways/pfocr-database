caption: Graphical abstract for FGF-2-dependent regulation of sVEGFR1 splice variants
  in endothelial cells and its contribution to angiogenesis and lung tumorigenesis.
  In endothelial cells, FGF-2 stimulates a SRPK1/SRSF1/SRSF3 signaling pathway that
  controls VEGFR1 splicing in favor of sVEGFR1 splice variants, in particular sVEGFR1-ex12
  (variant 4), that contribute to FGF-2 pro-angiogenic functions. In squamous lung
  carcinoma patients (LUSC), elevated sVEGFR1-ex12 usage value correlates with FGF-2/FGFR1
  mRNA levels and with poor prognosis, thereby supporting a role of the FGF-2/FGFR1/sVEGFR1-ex12
  signaling network in both physiological and pathological angiogenesis
