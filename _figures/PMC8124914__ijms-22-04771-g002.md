caption: Biological role of NO in pathophysiological conditions. NO-mediated activation
  of cGMP, PKG, and VASP can cause platelet inhibition while NO-mediated induction
  of pro-apoptotic proteins such as PARP, AIF, cytochrome C, and cleaved caspase-3
  can induce cell death. NO-mediated activation of cGMP, PKG, Rho A, and Rho kinase
  can alter smooth muscles relaxation. Inhibition of NAD, NADPH, and GSH by NO increases
  the probability of cell death. Lipid peroxidation caused by NO induces oxidative
  stress or damage. S-nitrosylation elicited by NO can cause neurotoxicity or neurodegeneration.
  NO-mediated induction of PKG and calcium signaling causes exitotoxicity and contraction
  effects. NO is also involved in neutrophil infiltration and endothelial dysfunctions
  through effects of mitochondrial respiration, through NK cell toxicity, and through
  activation of the GAPDH-PARP pathway and its functions.
