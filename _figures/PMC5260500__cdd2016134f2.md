  and in vivo the GTPase activity of RAS proteins (H-Ras, K-Ras, and N-Ras) facilitating
  GTP hydrolysis to GDP. As a RAS-GAP, DAB2IP negatively controls activation of MAP
  kinases in response to exogenous stimuli (i.e., EGF, epidermal growth factor) and
  operates to finely orchestrate proliferation and differentiation of normal and cancer
  cells., , ,  As a RAS-GAP, DAB2IP can also inhibit RAS-dependent activation of the
