caption: The postulated mechanism of tocotrienol in protecting against glucocorticoid-induced
  osteoporosis. Tocotrienol can increase OPG secretion by osteoblasts and reduce RANKL
  secretion by osteoblasts, fibroblasts and immune cells, thus reducing osteoclasts
  formation. Tocotrienol may also suppress osteoclast formation directly. In addition,
  tocotrienol can reduce DKK1 and sclerostin levels; thus, the Wnt signalling pathway
  and osteogenesis are not inhibited.
