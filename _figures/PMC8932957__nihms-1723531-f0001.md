caption: Starvation conditions in yeast cause a major lifespan extension mediated
  in large part by the lack of amino acids and sugars. On one hand, amino-acid restriction
  causes the inactivation of TOR–Pkh–S6k signaling; on the other hand, low glucose
  levels promote reduced activity of the Ras–adenylate cyclase (Cyr1)–PKA pathway.
  Both the amino-acid and the sugar pathways converge on and inactivate the serine
  threonine kinase Rim15. This, in turn, contributes to the activation of stress-resistance
  transcription factors Gis1, which binds to post diauxic shift (PDS) motif, and Msn2
  and Msn4, orthologs of mammalian early growth response protein 1 (EGR1),, which
  bind to stress-responsive element (STRE) motif.
