caption: Schematic representation of decorin’s effects on β-catenin and Myc stabilization
  by directly down-regulating EGFR and Met. (A) Activation of both the Wnt and RTK
  pathways leads to stabilization of β-catenin and Myc proteins and their subsequent
  translocation into the nuclei where downstream transcription is activated. (B) When
  the Wnt pathway is blocked by absence or block of Wnt factors, or when RTK activity
  is attenuated by exogenous decorin, there is phosphorylation of β-catenin and Myc
  at specific residues. Especially important is phosphorylation at Threonine 58 (T58)
  of Myc, because this modification acts as priming for subsequent ubiquitination
  and 26S proteasomal degradation. For additional details see the text. Adapted in
  part from Albihn et al.
