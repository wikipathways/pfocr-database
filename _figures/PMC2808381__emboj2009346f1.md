caption: Hog1 stress-activated protein kinase (SAPK) signalling. Two independent upstream
  osmosensing mechanisms lead to the activation of specific MAPKKKs. A ‘two-component'
  osmosensor (Sln1–Ypd1 and Ssk1 proteins) regulates the Ssk2 and Ssk22 MAPKKKs, whereas
  the second branch of the pathway activates the Ste11 MAPKKK through the transmembrane
  protein Sho1 and the mucin-like proteins Msb2 and Hkr1. In response to osmostress,
  Pbs2 integrates both signals and activates the Hog1 SAPK, which induces a set of
  osmoadaptive responses.
