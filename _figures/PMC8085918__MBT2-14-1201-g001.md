caption: In vivo galactocerebroside biosynthesis pathway in Mycoplasma pneumoniae
  according to functional analysis. M. pneumoniae favours the import on glucose over
  galactose in vivo. Once in the cytosol, UDP‐glucose in converted into UDP‐galactose
  by the epimerase containing the functional domain PF16363, encoded by gene mpn257.
  UDP‐galactose is then used by one of the glycosyltransferases encoded by mpn028,
  mpn483 or mpn075, all containing the functional domain M200535. The import of ceramide
  and the synthesis of galactocerebroside are supported by experimental evidences.
