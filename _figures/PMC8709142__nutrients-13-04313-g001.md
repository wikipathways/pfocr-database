caption: Possible molecular mechanisms of bromelain’s anti-tumor activity are realized
  at 3 levels of cellular metabolism. In vitro and in vivo studies have shown that
  bromelain inhibits the proliferation of cancer cells primarily by I) modulating
  the expression of genes crucial for cell differentiation and proliferation (MAPK
  signaling pathway, Akt, Cox-2, NF-κB), II) induction of cell death by apoptosis/autophagy,
  and III) blocking the cell cycle by inhibiting cyclins which are necessary for this
  process.
