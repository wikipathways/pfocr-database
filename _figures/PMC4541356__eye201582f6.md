caption: The RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK signal transduction pathway. Extracellular signaling
  ligands bind to receptor tyrosine kinases in the cell membrane. This results in
  activation of RAS, which in turn activates RAF. RAF phosphorylates and activates
  MEK. MEK phosphorylates and activates ERK. Once activated, ERK translocates to the
  nucleus where it binds to transcription factors to stimulate cell growth and proliferation.
  Mutations resulting in abnormal activation of this pathway may contribute to many
  human cancers.
