caption: Innate immune recognition of viral infection and evasion mechanisms by SARS-CoV-2.
  Viral infection is sensed by various innate immune receptors, including cytoplasmic
  RNA sensors (RIG-I and MDA5) and TLRs (TLR3, TLR4, TLR7, and TLR8). Upon recognition,
  proinflammatory genes and IFNs are upregulated by transcription factors, NF-κB,
  and IRF3. The secreted type I (IFN-α and -β) and III (IFN-λ) IFNs bind to IFNα/β
  receptor and IFNλ receptor, respectively, which activate the JAK-STAT signaling
  pathway to upregulate ISG expression. SARS-CoV-2 proteins that have been reported
  to interfere with IFN responses are indicated. SARS-CoV-2, severe acute respiratory
  syndrome coronavirus 2; TLR, Toll-like receptor; IFN, interferon; NF-κB, nuclear
  factor-κB; IRF3, interferon regulatory factor 3; JAK-STAT, Janus kinase-signal transducer
  and activator of transcription; ISG, IFN-stimulated genes.
