caption: PGE2, the main catalyzed product of COX-2 from arachidonic acid, could bind
  to the EP receptor on the cell membrane, thereby activating the JAK2, followed by
  the phorphorylating of STAT3 in the Tyr705 site. The activated p-STAT3 forms the
  homo-or heterodimers, which translocate into the nucleus, bind to the downstream
  target gene MMP-2, MMP-9, and accelerate the tumor progression. Inhibiting the COX-2/PGE2
  mediated JAK2/STAT3 signaling pathway, could be one of the mechanisms of the inhibitory
  effect of berberin on the invasion and metastasis in colorectal cancer.
