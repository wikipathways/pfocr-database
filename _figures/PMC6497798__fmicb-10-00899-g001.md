caption: 'Autophagy initiates host immunity and Toxoplasma immune evasion. The host
  eliminates intracellular Toxoplasma through autophagy proteins in two ways: the
  canonical CD40-CD40L-mediated and the noncanonical IFN-γ-dependent autophagy pathways.
  Toxoplasma can evade elimination through host autophagy by following means. (A)
  In the canonical autophagy pathway, MIC3, and MIC6 secreted from Toxoplasma phosphorylate
  EGFR and activate the PI3K/AKT signaling pathway, which has an impact on LC3 function.
  The activation of FAK-Src through Toxoplasma invasion prevents this parasite from
  being targeted. (B) ROP5, ROP18, and GRA7 form a complex with ROP17 and load onto
  the PVM, inactivating IRGs through phosphorylation.'
