caption: (a) Eukaryotes use an elaborate E1-E2-E3 mediated mechanism for the attachment
  of ubiquitin to protein substrates. (b) Similarly to eukaryotes, small archaeal
  ubiquitin-like modifier proteins (termed SAMPs) can form protein conjugates in the
  archaeon H. volcanii by a pathway that is dependent upon the synthesis of an E1
  homolog (termed UbaA). Based on genome sequence, E2 and E3 homologs are not predicted
  for this pathway. UbaA and SAMP proteins appear to also be linked to sulfur incorporation
  pathways (such as the biosynthesis of MoCo and thiolated tRNA) that require an E1-type
  adenylation reaction for the formation of a thiocarboxylated sulfur carrier protein
  with a ubiquitin-type β-grasp fold
