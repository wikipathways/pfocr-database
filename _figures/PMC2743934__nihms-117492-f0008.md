caption: Group I mGluRs activate eEF2K via calcium-calmodulin (CaM). eEF2K phosphorylates
  eEF2, which inhibits elongation generally but increases Arc/Arg3.1 translation.
  Arc/Arg3.1 forms a complex with endophilin2/3 (Endo) and dynamin (Dyn) and induces
  the internalization of AMPAR (). FMRP inhibits the translation of Arc/Arg3.1 at
  the basal state. Arc/Arg3.1 induction alone is not sufficient for mGluR-LTD, indicating
  that mGluR activates another pathway that is required to internalize AMPAR (). In
  Fmr1 KO mice, the synthesis of Arc/Arg3.1 protein is constitutively derepressed,
  and de novo synthesis of Arc/Arg3.1 is not required for mGluR-LTD.
