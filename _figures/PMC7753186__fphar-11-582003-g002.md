caption: Mechanisms involved in SARS-CoV-2 infection delineating an ACE/ACE2-ATR1-Cholesterol-HDAC
  axis. SARS-CoV-2 infection occurs with ACE2 and S-protein interactions (facilitated
  by membrane cholesterol) reducing ACE2 availability. For this reason, excessive
  angiotensin II (ANG II) accumulates and then activates AT1R (Angiotensin II receptor
  type 1). The latter in turn stimulates HDAC to up-regulate ACE2 expression and synthesis
  facilitating SARS-CoV-2 infection. Angiotensin II and AT1R activity increase HDAC
  function activating inflammatory responses with relevant pathogenetic implications.
  During HDAC activation, histones are deacetylated and the increased availability
  of regenerated Acetyl-CoA can feed cholesterol synthesis.
